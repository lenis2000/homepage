Domino Tilings of the Aztec Diamond

Aztec diamond of size \(M\):
Staircase-shaped region tiled by \(1 \times 2\) and \(2 \times 1\) dominoes.
Number of tilings: \(\displaystyle 2^{M(M+1)/2}\)
Arctic circle theorem [Jockusch–Propp–Shor 1998]
Under the uniform measure, the frozen boundary is a circle inscribed in the diamond
Height function:
Domino tilings \(\leftrightarrow\) interlacing particle arrays \(\leftrightarrow\) height functions on the square grid, just like lozenge tilings
Same universality: GFF fluctuations, Airy processes at the edge, translation-invariant Gibbs measures in the bulk [Chhita–Johansson–Young 2015], [Johansson 2005]
