One-Periodic Random Edge Weights [Bufetov–P.–Zografos 2025]

Model:
Attach i.i.d. random weights \(W_1, \ldots, W_M \sim \mathcal{B}_M\) on \(\mathbb{R}_{>0}\) to edges in each diagonal layer of the Aztec diamond of size \(M\). Unlabeled edges have weight \(1\).
Measure on tilings:
\(\displaystyle \mathbf{P}(D) = \frac{1}{Z} \prod_{e \in D} \operatorname{weight}(e)\)
Two regimes as \(M \to \infty\):
  1. Critical vanishing variance: \(\operatorname{Var}[\mathcal{B}_M] \sim 1/M\).
    Same limit shape; fluctuations = GFF + Brownian motion
  2. Fixed distribution: \(\mathcal{B}_M = \mathcal{B}\) independent of \(M\).
    New limit shape; fluctuations on scale \(\sqrt{M}\) = Brownian motion
Bernoulli weights
\(\mathbf{P}(W_i=\tfrac12)=\mathbf{P}(W_i=5)=\tfrac12\)
Uniform weights
\(W_i \sim \mathrm{Unif}[0,2]\)
Random tilings of size \(M=200\) and \(M=300\)
