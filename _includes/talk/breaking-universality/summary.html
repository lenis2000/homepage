Summary

Universality in dimer models:
Random lozenge and domino tilings exhibit: deterministic limit shapes, local convergence to translation-invariant Gibbs measures, GFF global fluctuations, Airy processes at edges.
Mechanism 1 — The Waterfall [Knizel–P. 2025]:
A strong double-well potential (q-Racah weights with fixed \(q\)) induces dimensional collapse: 2D Gibbs measures → 1D barcode process with emergent period-two structure. Powered by q-Racah orthogonal polynomials.
Mechanism 2 — Random edge weights [Bufetov–P.–Zografos 2025]:
Diagonally layered i.i.d. random weights on the Aztec diamond deform limit shapes and add Brownian motion to the fluctuations (on scale \(\sqrt{M}\) or \(O(1)\), depending on variance scaling). Powered by annealed Schur generating functions.
Common thread:
Both mechanisms break universality while preserving exact solvability. The key is finding the right algebraic structure:
  • q-Racah polynomials at the top of the q-Askey scheme
  • Schur generating functions for disordered systems
Open questions:
  1. Rigorously justify the barcode kernel computations (divergent spectral series)
  2. Simpler mechanisms for dimensional collapse?
  3. Random weights with more general (non-one-periodic) structure?
  4. Combine both mechanisms: q-deformed weights + disorder?
