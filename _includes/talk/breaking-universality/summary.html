Summary

  • Universality is well-developed for dimer models. Includes deterministic limit shapes, translation-invariant Gibbs measures locally, GFF fluctuations, Airy processes at edges, and other typical behavior
  • The waterfall/barcode phenomenon [Knizel–P. 2025] describes an instance of dimensional collapse in the local behavior. So far, isolated in the q-Racah hexagon tilings, but should be present more broadly
  • Random edge weights (disordered dimer models) is a new rich world with many new instances of universal asymptotic behavior [Bufetov–P.–Zografos 2025], [Moulard–Toninelli 2025], [Zografos 2025], [Duits–Van Peski 2025]
  • Results presented today result on specifics of breaking universality while preserving exact solvability
