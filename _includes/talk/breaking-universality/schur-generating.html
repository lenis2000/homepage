Schur Generating Functions

Dominos \(\leftrightarrow\) particles:
A domino tiling is encoded by a sequence of partitions. Under fixed one-periodic weights, the joint law is a Schur process [Johansson 2002], [Okounkov–Reshetikhin 2003]
Schur generating function [Gorin–Panova 2012], [Bufetov–Gorin 2013]:
\(\displaystyle S_{\rho}(u_1,\ldots,u_N) = \sum_{\lambda} \rho(\lambda) \frac{s_\lambda(u_1,\ldots,u_N)}{s_\lambda(1^N)}\)
where \(\rho\) is the distribution of a partition \(\lambda\), and \(u_j \in \mathbb{C}\)
For random weights [Bufetov–P.–Zografos 2025]:
The annealed SGF has an explicit product form (\(M\) is the size of the Aztec diamond, \(N\) is the level):
\(\displaystyle S_{\rho}(x_1,\ldots,x_N) = \mathbf{E}\Bigl(\prod_{i=1}^N (1-\beta+x_i\beta)\Bigr)^{M-N}\),
where \(\beta\) is an independent copy of the random weights \(W_j\).
Key point: Asymptotics of the \(N\)-th root of the SGF as \(N\to\infty\) yield LLN and CLT for the particle system (and thus the height function of the domino tilings).
