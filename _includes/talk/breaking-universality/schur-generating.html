Schur Generating Functions

Schur process structure:
Domino tilings of the Aztec diamond with one-periodic weights are governed by Schur measures [Johansson 2002], [Okounkov–Reshetikhin 2003]
Schur generating function [Bufetov–Gorin 2013]:
\(\displaystyle S_{\rho_N}(u_1,\ldots,u_k, 1^{N-k}) = \sum_{\lambda \in \mathbb{GT}_N} \rho_N(\lambda) \frac{s_\lambda(u_1,\ldots,u_k,1^{N-k})}{s_\lambda(1^N)}\)
For random weights [Bufetov–P.–Zografos 2025]:
\(\displaystyle \lim_{N\to\infty} \sqrt[N]{S_{\rho_N}(u_1,\ldots,u_k,1^{N-k})} = \bigl(\mathbf{E}_{\mathcal{B}}\prod_{i=1}^k (1-\beta+u_i\beta)\bigr)^a\)
Key point: The annealed limit of the SGF factors into expectations over the weight distribution \(\mathcal{B}\). This drives both the LLN and CLT for the height function.
Limit shape from the SGF:
Solve for \(z\) in the upper half-plane:
\(\displaystyle \mathcal{F}(z) = \frac{z}{z-1} + az \, \mathbf{E}_{\mathcal{B}} \frac{\beta}{1-\beta+\beta z} = \alpha^{-1} y\)
Limiting density:
\(\displaystyle (\alpha,y)\mapsto \frac{1}{\pi}\,\mathrm{Arg}\bigl(z(\alpha,y)\bigr)\)
Arctic curve: locus where \(\mathcal{F}(z)=\alpha^{-1}y\) has a double root in \(z\). Has an exact parametrization.
Self-averaging: The limit shape is the same for almost every realization of the random weights (quenched = annealed at the LLN level)
