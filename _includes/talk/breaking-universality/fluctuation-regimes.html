Non-GFF Fluctuations [Bufetov–P.–Zografos 2025]

Regime 1: Critical scaling
\(\operatorname{Var}(\alpha_k) \sim 1/M\)
Height fluctuations = GFF + independent Brownian motion
Both live on the same scale \(O(1)\)
Critical scaling fluctuations
\(h - \mathbb{E}[h]\) at scale \(O(1)\)
Regime 2: Fixed distribution
\(\operatorname{Var}(\alpha_k) = \text{const}\)
Fluctuations on scale \(\sqrt{M}\), given by Brownian motion alone
The GFF is invisible — the disorder dominates
Fixed distribution fluctuations
\(h - \mathbb{E}[h]\) at scale \(\sqrt{M}\)
Contrast with the waterfall:
The waterfall breaks universality via a strong potential (q-Racah weights) that collapses 2D → 1D. Here, random disorder adds a new Brownian component to the fluctuations.
Common thread:
Both mechanisms preserve exact solvability — via q-Racah orthogonal polynomials (waterfall) or Schur generating functions (random weights)
