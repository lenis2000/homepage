Spectral Approach in the Transversal Direction

2D extended correlation kernel [BGR]:
\(\displaystyle K(s,x; t,y) = \begin{cases} -\frac{\phi_t(y)}{\phi_s(x)}(\mathbb{U}_s \cdots \mathbb{U}_{t-1})_{x,y} & s < t \\[0.5em] \frac{\phi_t(y)}{\phi_s(x)}[K_s]_{x,y} + \mathbf{1}_{x=y} & s = t \\[0.5em] \frac{\phi_t(y)}{\phi_s(x)}(K_s - L_{\text{top}}\mathbb{U}_{t-1}^{-1}\cdots \mathbb{U}_s^{-1})_{x,y} & s > t \end{cases}\)
Inter-slice operators \(\mathbb{U}_t\):
Coefficients are explicit and product-form
Convergence: Inter-slice operators \(\mathbb{U}_t\) converge in our regime, coefficient-wise
Limiting inter-slice operators:
\(\displaystyle \mathfrak{a}(x) = \frac{1-q^{2x}}{1-q^{2x+1}} \quad \text{(step right)}\)
Function \(\mathfrak{a}(x)\) rapidly decays at \(\pm \infty\)
Key property:
The limit is a "very bounded" operator in \(\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})\)
Its inverse is "very unbounded"
Approach:
  1. Diagonalize \(\mathfrak{a}\) into orthonormal eigenfunctions \(\mathcal{F}_n\)
  2. Pretend that the inverse operator makes sense
  3. Write down the barcode density \(\rho(t)\)
