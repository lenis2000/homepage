Inter-slice Operators and Their Limits

  • Put the physics hat on.
  • Diagonalize \(\mathfrak{U}\) into orthonormal eigenfunctions
  • Pretend that the inverse operator makes sense, and write down the barcode density \(\rho(t)\)
We get a guess
\(\displaystyle \rho(t) = \mathfrak{a}\bigl(1-\tfrac{t}{2}\bigr) \sum_{n \in \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}} \frac{-q^{-n} \mathcal{F}_n^t(0) \mathcal{F}_n^{t-1}(0)}{\|\mathcal{F}_n\|_{\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})}^2}\)
  • How good is the guess?
\(\displaystyle \mathcal{F}_n(x) := \sqrt{1 - \kappa^{-2} q^{-2x}} \sum_{i=-n}^{n} \frac{(-1)^{n+i} q^{-i/2}}{(q;q)_{n-i}(q;q)_{n+i}} \cdot \frac{1}{\theta_{\sqrt{q}}\bigl(|\kappa| q^{x+i+\frac{1}{2}}\bigr)}\)
\(\displaystyle \mathcal{F}_n^t(x) := \mathcal{F}_n\bigl(x - \tfrac{t-1}{2}\bigr)\)
\(\displaystyle (\mathfrak{U}^{\mathrm{barcode}}_t \mathcal{F}_n^t)(x) = -q^n \mathcal{F}_n^{t+1}(x)\)
\(t, x \in \mathbb{Z}, \quad n \in \tfrac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\)
\(\displaystyle \mathfrak{a}(x) := \left[\frac{-\kappa^2 q^{2x-1}}{(1-\kappa^2 q^{2x})(1-\kappa^2 q^{2x-1})}\right]^{1/2}\)
\(\displaystyle \theta_{\sqrt{q}}(z) := \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} q^{k^2/2} z^k = (q;q)_\infty (-z;q)_\infty (-q/z;q)_\infty\)
