Inter-slice Operators and Their Limits

Inter-slice operators: Inter-slice operators converge (under scaling). Coefficients are explicit and product-form.
Limit operator:
The limit is a "very bounded" operator on \(\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})\)
Key obstacle:
Its inverse is "very unbounded"
Physics approach: If we put the physics hat on, we can get the answer for the barcode kernel
Three-term recurrence:
\(\displaystyle \mathfrak{a}(x) \mathcal{F}_n\left(x - \frac{1}{2}\right) + \mathfrak{a}\left(x + \frac{1}{2}\right) \mathcal{F}_n\left(x + \frac{1}{2}\right) = -q^n \mathcal{F}_n(x)\)
\(\displaystyle \mathfrak{a}(x) := \left[\frac{-\kappa^2 q^{2x-1}}{(1-\kappa^2 q^{2x})(1-\kappa^2 q^{2x-1})}\right]^{1/2}, \qquad x \in \tfrac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}\)
Eigenfunctions \(\mathcal{F}_n(x)\):
\(\displaystyle \mathcal{F}_n(x) := \sqrt{1 - \kappa^{-2} q^{-2x}} \sum_{i=-n}^{n} \frac{(-1)^{n+i} q^{-i/2}}{(q;q)_{n-i}(q;q)_{n+i}} \cdot \frac{1}{\theta_{\sqrt{q}}\left(|\kappa| q^{x+i+\frac{1}{2}}\right)}\)
Involves theta functions and q-factorials
Barcode density:
\(\displaystyle \rho(t) = \mathfrak{a}\left(1-\frac{t}{2}\right) \sum_{n \in \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}} \frac{-q^{-n} \mathcal{F}_n^t(0) \mathcal{F}_n^{t-1}(0)}{\|\mathcal{F}_n\|_{\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})}^2}\)
where \(\mathcal{F}_n^t(x) := \mathcal{F}_n\left(x - \frac{t-1}{2}\right)\)
