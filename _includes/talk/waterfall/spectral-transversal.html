Spectral Approach in the Transversal Direction

The 2d (extended) correlation kernel is [BGR]
\(\displaystyle \widetilde{K}(s,x;t,y) = \begin{cases} \bigl(\mathfrak{U}_t^{-1}\mathfrak{U}_{t+1}^{-1}\ldots\mathfrak{U}_{s-1}^{-1}\bigr)_y K_s(x,y), & s > t;\\[6pt] K_t(x,y), & s = t;\\[6pt] \bigl(\mathfrak{U}_{t-1}\ldots\mathfrak{U}_{s+1}\mathfrak{U}_s\bigr)_y \bigl(-\mathbf{1}_{x=y} + K_s(x,y)\bigr), & s < t. \end{cases}\)
Relies on inter-slice operators (whose coefficients are explicit and product-form)
\(\displaystyle \widetilde{\mathfrak{C}}_n^t f_n^{t+1}(x) = \sqrt{\frac{\widetilde{w}_t(x-1)}{\widetilde{w}_{t+1}(x)}} f_n^t(x-1)\, a_1^t(x-1) + \sqrt{\frac{\widetilde{w}_t(x)}{\widetilde{w}_{t+1}(x)}} f_n^t(x)\, a_0^t(x) \;=:\; \bigl(\mathfrak{U}_t f_n^t\bigr)(x)\)
  • Inter-slice operators \(\mathfrak{U}_t\) converge in our regime, coefficient-wise
  • The limit is a "very bounded" operator in \(\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})\) - its inverse is "very unbounded"
