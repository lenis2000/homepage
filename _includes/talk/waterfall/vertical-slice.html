Spectral Approach on Vertical Slices?

Question:
For fixed \(q\), does the kernel converge to the discrete sine process?
Answer: No.
The correlation kernel converges to 0 or the identity operator
Follows from path concentration [Knizel-P. 2025]
Limiting q-difference operators:
We compute the limits. "Nice" only outside the waterfall and at the central point
Example: \(\displaystyle (Tf)(x) = c\cdot(1+q^{2x})f(x+1) + (1+q^{-2x})f(x-1) - (1+q^{-1})^2 f(x)\)
Problem:
  • Symmetric but not self-adjoint
  • Von Neumann deficiency \((1,1)\)
  • Self-adjoint extension is not unique: a 1-dimensional family
  • Extension may depend on position in slice
Conclusion: The interesting collapsed 1D behavior happens in the transversal direction
