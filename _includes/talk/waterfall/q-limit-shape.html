q-Area: Global Limit Shape

Limit shape [Vershik 1996]
As \(N \to \infty\) with \(q = e^{-\gamma/N}\), the limit shape satisfies: \(\displaystyle A e^{-\gamma y} + B e^{-\gamma x} = 1\)
\(\displaystyle A = \frac{1-e^{-\gamma}}{1-e^{-\gamma(1+\alpha)}}\), \(\displaystyle B = \frac{1-e^{-\gamma \alpha}}{1-e^{-\gamma(1+\alpha)}}\), \(\alpha\) = aspect ratio
How to get limit shape?
  • Approximate any curve by a piecewise-linear trajectory
  • Locally, we know how many configurations approximate a given line
  • Integrate and maximize to get the typical shape
Variational principle for the limit shape \(y=f(x)\):
Maximize entropy − γ · area =
\(\displaystyle \int \sigma(f'(x)) \, dx - \gamma \cdot \int f(x) \, dx\)
\(\sigma(s) = (s+1)\log(s+1) - s\log(s)\) = entropy of paths with slope \(s\):
\(\sigma(s) = \lim\limits_{N\to\infty} \dfrac{1}{N} \log\# \)paths from \((0,0)\) to \((N,\lfloor sN\rfloor)\)
