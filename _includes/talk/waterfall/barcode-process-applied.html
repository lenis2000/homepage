The Conjectural Barcode Process [Knizel-P. 2025]

Diagonal slice, q = 0.85
Horizontal cross-section
Spectral analysis (nonrigorous):
  • Put the physics hat on
  • Diagonalize the transversal operators via q-Racah eigenfunctions \(\mathcal{F}_n\)
  • Write barcode density as a series:
    \(\displaystyle \rho(t) = \sum_{n \in \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}} \frac{(-q^{-n})\mathcal{F}_n(-\frac{t}{2})\mathcal{F}_n(1-\frac{t}{2})}{\|\mathcal{F}_n\|^2_{\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})}}\)
  • But... this series does not converge!
  • Diverges "mildly": \(+c - c + c - c + \ldots\)
  • So, we postulate that the sum "has two limits"!
  • Two subsequential limits:
\(\rho_0\) := lim (even partial sums) \(\approx 0.5611\)
\(\rho_1\) := lim (odd partial sums) \(\approx 0.4389\)
  • Both limits are independent of \(t\)
  • \(\rho_0+\rho_1 = 1\) (global direction of the waterfall plane)
  • And indeed, the barcode process is 2-periodic (!)
