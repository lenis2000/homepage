Summary

Local randomness → global shapes: Random surfaces concentrate around deterministic limit shapes as the mesh refines. Local statistics are universal — determined only by the slope.
Variational principle & PDE: Limit shapes maximize entropy subject to boundary conditions. The Euler–Lagrange equation is a complex Burgers equation with a free boundary (arctic curve).
Breaking the framework: The q-Racah deformation introduces a weight favoring/disfavoring volume. In the frozen regime, instead of losing all randomness, the system retains entropy by collapsing 2D phases into 1D — the waterfall.
This work [Knizel–P. 2025]: Justified the global behavior (limit shape) and the dimensional collapse mechanism. Horizontal lozenges are exponentially suppressed in the waterfall region.
