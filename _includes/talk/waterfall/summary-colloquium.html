Summary

Local randomness → global shapes: Random up-right paths, surfaces, 1-Lipschitz function, etc., concentrate around deterministic limit shapes, typically with global Gaussian fluctuations. In 1D and 2D, we saw that local statistics are universal — determined only by the slope.
Variational principle & PDE: Limit shapes maximize entropy subject to boundary conditions and volume/energy constraints.
The Euler–Lagrange equation for random surfaces is the complex Burgers equation \(u_x=u\,u_y\).
Beyond the classical picture: "Freezing" (\(q \not\to 1\)) breaks local universality. In the \(q\)-Racah example, freezing is combined with a strong energy differential in a specific direction, which leads to a novel waterfall phenomenon: 2D Gibbs measures collapse into 1D, but there is some residual randomness (the barcode interface).
