Summary

Models with \(q^{\text{area}}\) or \(q^{\text{volume}}\) weights typically freeze in the fixed-\(q\) regime, while \(q = e^{-\gamma/N}\) yields local behavior similar to \(q=1\)
In the q-Racah model, the frozen regime retains randomness (entropy) at the cost of collapsing 2D local phases into 1D
This work: We justify the global behavior and the dimensional collapse
Found limiting difference operators on each vertical slice
Further spectral analysis requires closure of non-self-adjoint operators
Inter-slice operators lead to conjectural two-periodic universal barcode process
Nonrigorous manipulations vs. strong numerical and simulation evidence
Open:
  • Barcode process: existence and convergence to it
  • Edge of waterfall behavior (should be q-Gibbs, translation-invariant, possibly 2-periodic)
