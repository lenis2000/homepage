Correlation Kernel as Spectral Projection

Determinantal structure:
Correlation functions are determinantal: \(\rho_k(x_1,\ldots,x_k) = \det[K(x_i, x_j)]_{i,j=1}^k\)
Spectral interpretation:
The correlation kernel of the q-Racah OPE is an operator in \(\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})\) which is the orthogonal spectral projection associated with a three-term q-difference equation for the q-Racah polynomials
[Olshanski 2008] first applied spectral projections to determinantal point processes
Kernel = projection onto spectral interval
\(K = \mathbf{1}_{[\lambda_{\min}, \lambda_{\max}]}(D_q)\)
\(D_q\) = q-difference operator
Classical limit \(q \to 1\):
Difference operators converge to \(\frac{d^2}{dx^2}\)
Spectral projection → multiplication by \(\mathbf{1}_{[a,b]}\)
Result [BGR 2009]:
As \(N \to \infty\) with \(q = e^{-\gamma/N} \to 1\), the spectral projections converge.
Inverse Fourier → Discrete sine kernel
