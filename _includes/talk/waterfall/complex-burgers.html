The PDE Behind the Limit Shape

Euler–Lagrange equation:
The variational principle \(\max \iint \sigma(\nabla h)\,dx\,dy\) yields a PDE for the complex slope
Complex Burgers equation [Kenyon–Okounkov 2007]
\[\frac{\partial f}{\partial \bar{z}} = 0 \quad \text{(liquid region)}\]
The complex slope \(f(z)\) is analytic inside the liquid region, satisfying a Burgers-type equation on the boundary
Free boundary (arctic curve):
The PDE solution changes type at the arctic curve:
  • Inside: complex slope → random (liquid)
  • Outside: real slope → deterministic (frozen)
Analogy: like the transition from elliptic to hyperbolic PDE — the arctic curve is a free boundary of the variational problem
