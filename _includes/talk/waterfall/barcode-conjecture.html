Universal Barcode Process Conjecture

Full barcode kernel:
\(\displaystyle K_{\text{barcode}}(s,t) = \sum_{n} \frac{\mathcal{F}_n(s)\mathcal{F}_n(t)}{\|\mathcal{F}_n\|^2}\) (regularized sum)
This formula is defined only for \(t \geq 1\); for \(t < 1\) we extend by symmetry.
The full kernel is two-periodic (i.e., \(2 \times 2\) block).
Conjecture [Knizel-P. 2025]:
  1. The barcode process is universal: depends only on \((q, \kappa)\) and not on the geometry of the hexagon (as long as the waterfall develops), or the point of observation inside the waterfall
  2. The \(M\)-limit in \(K_{\text{barcode}}\) exists and is equal to the limit of the transversal kernel of the q-Racah lozenge tilings of the hexagon
Evidence (numerical):
  • Batch collected simulation data
  • Density matching across geometries
  • Two-point correlation structure
Evidence (analytic):
  • Limits in formulas
  • Inter-slice operator convergence
  • Consistent with concentration result
Open questions:
  • Fluctuations of the height function: bounded or logarithmic?
  • Two-point correlations: two-periodicity and exponential decay
  • Behavior at the edge of the waterfall (should be q-Gibbs)
