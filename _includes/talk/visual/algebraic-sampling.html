What Algebraic Structures Can Do for You

Layer-by-layer sampling:
Instead of local flips, build the surface one horizontal layer at a time. Each transition \(S \to S+1\) adds a new interlacing path.
Commuting transfer matrices [Borodin–Gorin–Rains 2009]
Each layer is sampled from explicit transition probabilities given by commuting Markov operators — computed from the algebraic structure of the weights.
Polynomial time:
Exact sample in \(O(N^2 T)\) time.
Compare: Glauber CFTP needed \(\sim e^{cN}\) steps on the previous slide.
