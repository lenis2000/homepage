When Sampling is Hard

CFTP can be slow:
Exponentially slow in \(N\) — prohibitively long to get an exact sample. The monotone sandwich may take \(\sim e^{cN}\) steps to collapse.
Waterfall model [Knizel–P. 2025]:
The interpolation between \(q^{\text{volume}}\) and \(q^{-\text{volume}}\) produces a waterfall — a collapse of 2D randomness into a 1D interface. No known CFTP for this model.
Alternative approaches:
  • Algebraic exact sampling [Borodin–Gorin–Rains 2009]
  • Schur process sampling
  • Determinantal point processes
