## Evaluation Criteria

# 1. Opening hook - does "Does Chaos hide the Truth?" create genuine intrigue?
#    Is the typography and timing right? Does it feel like entering a gallery piece?
# 2. Chaos phase - does the initial randomness feel genuinely chaotic or just noisy?
#    Is there enough visual energy?
# 3. Emergence - is the transition from chaos to order perceivable and satisfying?
#    Does the viewer feel the "crystallization" happening?
# 4. Reveal - when the Penrose triangle emerges, is it a genuine moment of wonder?
#    Does "Local Truth, Global Paradox" land as a statement?
# 5. Interactive phase - does the entropy slider invite exploration? Is the connection
#    between slider and visual change intuitive?
# 6. Concept-form unity - does the visual form (impossible triangle from probabilistic tiles)
#    genuinely embody the philosophical claim about data vs probability?
# 7. Audio - does the sonification add or distract? (evaluate from code if not audible)
# 8. Pacing - are the 4s chaos + 15s annealing durations right for the emotional arc?
