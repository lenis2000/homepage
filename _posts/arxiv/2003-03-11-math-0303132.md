{% raw %}<p>We consider inhomogeneous Bernoulli measures of the form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mo>∏</mo><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi></mrow></msub><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>x</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\prod_{x\in\Lambda} p_x</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>x</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_x</annotation></semantics></math></span> are prescribed and uniformly bounded above and below away from 0 and 1. A comparison inequality is proved between the Kawasaki and Bernoulli-Laplace Dirichlet forms. Together with a recent result of Caputo on the gap of the Bernoulli-Laplace model, this proves a spectral gap of the correct order L^{-2} on cubes of side length L for the Kawasaki dynamics. The two block estimate of hydrodynamic limits is also obtained.</p>{% endraw %}
