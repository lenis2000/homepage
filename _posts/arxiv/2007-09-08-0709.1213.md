We indicate how this information will be used in a later publication to obtain full and explicitly computable asymptotic expansions with error bounds for all zeros of the Taylor polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><msubsup><mo>∑</mo><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msubsup><msup><mi>z</mi><mi>k</mi></msup><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>k</mi><mo stretchy="false">!</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_{n-1}(z) = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} z^k/ k!</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our proof is based on a representation of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_{n-1}(nz)</annotation></semantics></math></span> in terms of an integral of the form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mo>∫</mo><mi>γ</mi></msub><mfrac><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mi>ϕ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup><mrow><mi>s</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>z</mi></mrow></mfrac><mi>d</mi><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\int_{\gamma} \frac{e^{n\phi(s)}}{s-z}ds</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
