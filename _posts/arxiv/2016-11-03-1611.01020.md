{% raw %}<p>We study the asymptotic behavior, as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, of ratios of Toeplitz determinants <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>e</mi><mi>h</mi></msup><mi>d</mi><mi>μ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>d</mi><mi>μ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_n(e^h d\mu)/D_n(d\mu)</annotation></semantics></math></span> defined by a measure <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> on the unit circle and a sufficiently smooth function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The approach we follow is based on the theory of orthogonal polynomials. We prove that the second order asymptotics depends on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h</annotation></semantics></math></span> and only a few Verblunsky coefficients associated to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As a result, we establish a relative version of the Strong Szeg\H{o} Limit Theorem for a wide class of measures <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> with essential support on a single arc. In particular, this allows the measure to have a singular component within or outside of the arc.</p>{% endraw %}
