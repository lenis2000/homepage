{% raw %}<p>A previous paper of the author developed a general method for producing explicit central elements of quantized Lie algebras using Lusztig's inner product. This method had previously been applied for the type <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C_2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mn>3</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_3</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mn>4</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_4</annotation></semantics></math></span> Lie algebras. The current paper repeats the calculation for the type <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>B</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> Lie algebra, which is actually isomorphic to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> Lie algebra. The explicit expression for the corresponding quantum Hamiltonian is computed.</p>{% endraw %}
