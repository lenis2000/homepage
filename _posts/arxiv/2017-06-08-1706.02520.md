{% raw %}<p>We consider heat transport through systems with broken time-reversal symmetry. We apply strong magnetic fields to weakly charged particle systems, where the dynamics are dominated by the Lorentz force and spring forces. The standard momentum conservation is not satisfied and the sound wave disappears depending on the charge structure. We introduce an exactly solvable model and demonstrate the appearance of a new universality class. The absence of sound waves affects the relation between equilibrium correlation and the diverging thermal conductivity in the open system.</p>{% endraw %}
