{% raw %}<p>Multiline queues are versatile combinatorial objects that play a key role in understanding the remarkable connection between the asymmetric simple exclusion process (ASEP) on a circle and Macdonald polynomials. Specializing the results of Corteel--Mandelshtam--Williams (2018) to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> case yields a formula for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials through the Ferrari--Martin (2007) algorithm with a major index (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext mathvariant="monospace">maj</mtext></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\texttt{maj}</annotation></semantics></math></span>) statistic. In this paper, we reinterpret the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext mathvariant="monospace">maj</mtext></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\texttt{maj}</annotation></semantics></math></span> statistic as a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext mathvariant="monospace">charge</mtext></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\texttt{charge}</annotation></semantics></math></span> statistic on reading words, thereby bypassing the Ferrari--Martin algorithm to obtain an elegant formula for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials. Our methods naturally extend to the case of bosonic multiline queues, with which we obtain analogous results for the modified Hall--Littlewood polynomials using a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext mathvariant="monospace">cocharge</mtext></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\texttt{cocharge}</annotation></semantics></math></span> statistic on reading words. Twisted multiline queues (GMLQs) are obtained from the action of the symmetric group on the rows of a multiline queue. The Ferrari--Martin algorithm was extended to GMLQs by Arita--Ayyer--Mallick--Prolhac (2011), and Aas--Grinberg--Scrimshaw (2020) showed it is preserved under this action. We extend these results by defining a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext mathvariant="monospace">maj</mtext></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\texttt{maj}</annotation></semantics></math></span> statistic on GMLQs that is also preserved under this action. This yields a novel family of formulas, indexed by compositions, for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials. Additionally, we define a procedure on both GMLQs and bosonic multiline queues that we call collapsing, which can can be realized via the Kashiwara (crystal) operators on type-A Kirillov--Reshetikhin crystals. As an application, we naturally recover the Lascoux--Schützenberger <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext mathvariant="monospace">charge</mtext></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\texttt{charge}</annotation></semantics></math></span> formula for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker and modified Hall--Littlewood polynomials, and the classical and dual Cauchy identities for Schur functions.</p>{% endraw %}
