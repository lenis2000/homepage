{% raw %}<p>We prove a new tableaux formula for the symmetric Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P_{\lambda}(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> that has considerably fewer terms and simpler weights than previously existing formulas. Our formula is a sum over certain sorted non-attacking tableaux, weighted by the queue inversion statistic quinv. The quinv statistic originates from a formula for the modified Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mover accent="true"><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="true">~</mo></mover><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\widetilde{H}_{\lambda}(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> due to Ayyer, Martin, and the author (2022), and is naturally related to the dynamics of the asymmetric simple exclusion process (ASEP) on a circle. We prove our results by introducing probabilistic operators that act on non-attacking tableaux to generate a set of tableaux whose weighted sum equals <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P_{\lambda}(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. These operators are a modification of the inversion flip operators of Loehr and Niese (2012), which yield an involution on tableaux that preserves the major index statistic, but fails to preserve the non-attacking condition. Our tableaux are in bijection with the multiline queues introduced by Martin (2020), allowing us to derive an alternative multiline queue formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P_{\lambda}(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Finally, our formula recovers an alternative formula for the Jack polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>J</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>α</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J_{\lambda}(X;\alpha)</annotation></semantics></math></span> due to Knop and Sahi (1996) using the same queue inversion statistic.</p>{% endraw %}
