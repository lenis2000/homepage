{% raw %}<p>We study the equilibrium fluctuations of an interacting particle system evolving on the discrete ring with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\in\mathbb N</annotation></semantics></math></span> points, denoted by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">T</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb T_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and with three species of particles that we name <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A,B</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C</annotation></semantics></math></span>, but such that at each site there is only one particle. We prove that proper choices of density fluctuation fields (that match those from nonlinear fluctuating hydrodynamics theory) associated to the (two) conserved quantities converge, in the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, to a system of stochastic partial differential equations, that can either be the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck equation or the Stochastic Burgers equation. To understand the cross interaction between the two conserved quantities, we derive a general version of the Riemann-Lebesgue lemma which is of independent interest.</p>{% endraw %}
