{% raw %}<p>The singular values of products of standard complex Gaussian random matrices, or sub-blocks of Haar distributed unitary matrices, have the property that their probability distribution has an explicit, structured form referred to as a polynomial ensemble. It is furthermore the case that the corresponding bi-orthogonal system can be determined in terms of Meijer G-functions, and the correlation kernel given as an explicit double contour integral. It has recently been shown that the Hermitised product <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>M</mi></msub><mo>⋯</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><msub><mi>X</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mi>A</mi><msubsup><mi>X</mi><mn>1</mn><mi>T</mi></msubsup><msubsup><mi>X</mi><mn>2</mn><mi>T</mi></msubsup><mo>⋯</mo><msubsup><mi>X</mi><mi>M</mi><mi>T</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_M \cdots X_2 X_1A X_1^T X_2^T \cdots X_M^T</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where each <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a standard real complex Gaussian matrix, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A</annotation></semantics></math></span> is real anti-symmetric shares exhibits analogous properties. Here we use the theory of spherical functions and transforms to present a theory which, for even dimensions, includes these properties of the latter product as a special case. As an example we show that the theory also allows for a treatment of this class of Hermitised product when the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> are chosen as sub-blocks of Haar distributed real orthogonal matrices.</p>{% endraw %}
