{% raw %}<p>In this paper we study a random walk in a one-dimensional dynamic random environment consisting of a collection of independent particles performing simple symmetric random walks in a Poisson equilibrium with density <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho \in (0,\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. At each step the random walk performs a nearest-neighbour jump, moving to the right with probability <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0em">∘</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_{\circ}</annotation></semantics></math></span> when it is on a vacant site and probability <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0em">∙</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_{\bullet}</annotation></semantics></math></span> when it is on an occupied site. Assuming that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mo>∘</mo></msub><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_\circ \in (0,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mo>∙</mo></msub><mo mathvariant="normal">≠</mo><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mn>2</mn></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_\bullet \neq \tfrac12</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we show that the position of the random walk satisfies a strong law of large numbers, a functional central limit theorem and a large deviation bound, provided <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho</annotation></semantics></math></span> is large enough. The proof is based on the construction of a renewal structure together with a multiscale renormalisation argument.</p>{% endraw %}
