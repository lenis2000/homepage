{% raw %}<p>We study <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-pushTASEP, a discrete time interacting particle system whose distribution is related to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker measure. We prove a uniform in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> lower tail bound on the fluctuation scale for the location <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>x</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x_N(N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the right-most particle at time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> when started from step initial condition. Our argument relies on a map from the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker measure to a model of periodic last passage percolation (LPP) with geometric weights in an infinite strip that was recently established in [arXiv:2106.11922]. By a path routing argument we bound the passage time in the periodic environment in terms of an infinite sum of independent passage times for standard LPP on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\times N</annotation></semantics></math></span> squares with geometric weights whose parameters decay geometrically. To prove our tail bound result we combine this reduction with a concentration inequality, and a crucial new technical result -- lower tail bounds on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\times N</annotation></semantics></math></span> last passage times uniformly over all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N \in \mathbb N</annotation></semantics></math></span> and all the geometric parameters in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(0,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This technical result uses Widom's trick [arXiv:math/0108008] and an adaptation of an idea of Ledoux introduced for the GUE [Led05a] to reduce the uniform lower tail bound to uniform asymptotics for very high moments, up to order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, of the Meixner ensemble. This we accomplish by first obtaining sharp uniform estimates for factorial moments of the Meixner ensemble from an explicit combinatorial formula of Ledoux [Led05b], and translating them to polynomial bounds via a further careful analysis and delicate cancellation.</p>{% endraw %}
