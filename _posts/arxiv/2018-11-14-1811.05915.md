{% raw %}<p>We present some applications of central limit theorems on mesoscopic scales for random matrices. When combined with the recent theory of "homogenization" for Dyson Brownian Motion, this yields the universality of quantities which depend on the behavior of single eigenvalues of Wigner matrices and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles. Among the results we obtain are the Gaussian fluctuations of single eigenvalues for Wigner matrices (without an assumption of 4 matching moments) and classical <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta=1, 2, 4</annotation></semantics></math></span>), Gaussian fluctuations of the eigenvalue counting function, and an asymptotic expansion up to order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>o</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">o(N^{-1})</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the expected value of eigenvalues in the bulk of the spectrum. The latter result solves a conjecture of Tao and Vu.</p>{% endraw %}
