{% raw %}<p>We compare two methods for analysing periodic dimer models. These are the matrix-valued orthogonal polynomials approach due to Duits and one of the authors, and the Wiener-Hopf approach due to Berggren and Duits. We establish their equivalence in the special case of the Aztec diamond. Additionally, we provide explicit formulas for the matrix-valued orthogonal polynomials/Wiener-Hopf factors in the case of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>×</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2 \times 2</annotation></semantics></math></span>-periodic Aztec diamond in terms of Jacobi theta functions related to the spectral curve of the model.</p>{% endraw %}
