Here, we prove that the LQG volume of LQG metric balls admits all finite moments. This answers a question of Gwynne and Miller and generalizes a result obtained by Le Gall for the Brownian map, namely, the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo>=</mo><msqrt><mrow><mn>8</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma= \sqrt{8/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> case. We use this moment bound to show that on a compact set the volume of metric balls of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>r</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r</annotation></semantics></math></span> is given by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>r</mi><mrow><msub><mi>d</mi><mi>γ</mi></msub><mo>+</mo><msub><mi>o</mi><mi>r</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r^{d_\gamma+o_r(1)}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>d</mi><mi>γ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d_\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the dimension of the LQG metric space. Using similar techniques, we prove analogous results for the first exit time of Liouville Brownian motion from a metric ball. Gwynne-Miller-Sheffield (2020) prove that the metric measure space structure of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span>-LQG a.s. determines its conformal structure when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo>=</mo><msqrt><mrow><mn>8</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma=\sqrt{8/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>; their argument and our estimate yield the result for all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma \in (0,2)</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
