{% raw %}<p>In a groundbreaking work, Duplantier, Miller and Sheffield showed that subcritical Liouville quantum gravity (LQG) coupled with Schramm-Loewner evolutions (SLE) can be described by the mating of two continuum random trees. In this paper, we consider the counterpart of their result for critical LQG and SLE, i.e., for the case when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>γ</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>=</mo><mi>κ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>16</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>κ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma^2=\kappa=16/\kappa=4</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We prove that as one sends <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi><mo>↓</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa \downarrow 4</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the subcritical setting, the space-filling SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>κ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_\kappa</annotation></semantics></math></span> in a disk degenerates to the CLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>4</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_4</annotation></semantics></math></span> exploration introduced by Werner and Wu, along with a collection of i.i.d.\ coin tosses indexed by the branch points of the exploration. Furthermore, in the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>16</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>γ</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>↓</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa=16/\gamma^2\downarrow 4</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit, the pair of continuum random trees collapse into a single continuum random tree, and we observe that upon applying an appropriate affine transform to the encoding Brownian motions before taking the limit, we get convergence to a pair of independent Brownian motions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>A</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(A,B)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The Brownian motion <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A</annotation></semantics></math></span> encodes the LQG distance from the CLE loops to the boundary of the disk, while the Brownian motion <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B</annotation></semantics></math></span> encodes the boundary lengths of the CLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>4</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_4</annotation></semantics></math></span> loops. In contrast to the subcritical setting, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>A</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(A,B)</annotation></semantics></math></span> does not determine the CLE-decorated LQG surface.</p>{% endraw %}
