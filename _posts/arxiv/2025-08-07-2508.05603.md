{% raw %}<p>We develop the theory of the discrete periodic Pitman transform, first introduced by Corwin, Gu, and the fifth author. We prove that, for polymers in a periodic environment, single-path and multi-path partition functions are preserved under the action of this transform on the weights in the polymer model. As a corollary, we prove that the discrete periodic Pitman transform satisfies the same braid relations that are satisfied for the full-line Pitman transform shown by Biane, Bougerol, and O'Connell. Combined with a new inhomogeneous Burke property for the periodic Pitman transform, we prove a multi-path invariance result for the periodic inverse-gamma polymer under permutations of the column parameters. In the limit to the full-line case, we obtain a multi-path extension of a recent invariance result of Bates, Emrah, Martin, Sepp\"al\"ainen, and the fifth author, in both positive and zero-temperature.</p>{% endraw %}
