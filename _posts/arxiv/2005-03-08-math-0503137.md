{% raw %}<p>We consider the Ising model on a general tree under various boundary conditions: all plus, free and spin-glass. In each case, we determine when the root is influenced by the boundary values in the limit as the boundary recedes to infinity. We obtain exact capacity criteria that govern behavior at critical temperatures. For plus boundary conditions, an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>L</mi><mn>3</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L^3</annotation></semantics></math></span> capacity arises. In particular, on a spherically symmetric tree that has <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mi>α</mi></msup><msup><mi>b</mi><mi>n</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{\alpha}b^n</annotation></semantics></math></span> vertices at level <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> (up to bounded factors), we prove that there is a unique Gibbs measure for the ferromagnetic Ising model at the relevant critical temperature if and only if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha\le1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our proofs are based on a new link between nonlinear recursions on trees and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>L</mi><mi>p</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L^p</annotation></semantics></math></span> capacities.</p>{% endraw %}
