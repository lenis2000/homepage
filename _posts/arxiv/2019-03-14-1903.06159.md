{% raw %}<p>The goal of this paper is to investigate the missing part of the story about the relationship between the orthogonal polynomial ensembles and Painlevé equations. Namely, we consider the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Racah polynomial ensemble and show that the one-interval gap probabilities in this case can be expressed through a solution of the discrete <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-P<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo fence="true">(</mo><msubsup><mi>E</mi><mn>7</mn><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msubsup><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msubsup><mi>A</mi><mn>1</mn><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msubsup><mo fence="true">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\left(E_7^{(1)}/A_{1}^{(1)}\right)</annotation></semantics></math></span> equation.
