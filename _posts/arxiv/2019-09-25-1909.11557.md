{% raw %}<p>We study the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) growth equation in one dimension with a noise variance <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>c</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">c(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> depending on time. We find that for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>c</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>∝</mo><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>α</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">c(t)\propto t^{-\alpha}</annotation></semantics></math></span> there is a transition at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha=1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. When <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&gt;1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the solution saturates at large times towards a non-universal limiting distribution. When <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&lt;1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span> the fluctuation field is governed by scaling exponents depending on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the limiting statistics are similar to the case when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>c</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">c(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is constant. We investigate this problem using different methods: (1) Elementary changes of variables mapping the time dependent case to variants of the KPZ equation with constant variance of the noise but in a deformed potential (2) An exactly solvable discretization, the log-gamma polymer model (3) Numerical simulations.</p>{% endraw %}
