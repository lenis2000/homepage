{% raw %}<p>We consider the log-Gamma polymer in the half-space with bulk weights distributed as $\operatorname{Gamma}^{-1}(2\theta)$ and diagonal weights as $\operatorname{Gamma}^{-1}(\alpha+\theta)$ for $\theta>0$ and $\alpha>-\theta$. We show that in the bound phase, i.e., when $\alpha \in (-\theta,0)$, the endpoint of the polymer lies within an $O(1)$ stochastic window of the diagonal. This result gives the first rigorous proof of the pinned phenomena for the half-space polymers in the bound phase conjectured by Kardar(1985). We also show that the limiting quenched endpoint distribution of the polymer around the diagonal is given by a random probability mass function proportional to the exponential of a random walk with log-Gamma type increments.</p>{% endraw %}
