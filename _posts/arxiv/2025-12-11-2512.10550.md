{% raw %}<p>We prove local convergence of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG model with zero boundary to the stationary <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG model, confirming a recent conjecture of Drillick and Lin (2024). The stationary <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG model is the one with both left and bottom boundaries of Poisson nucleations with rate parameters <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac{1}{\lambda(1-t)}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>, respectively, for some <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the proof, we consider the trajectories of certain second class particles via a basic monotone coupling of three <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG processes, and adapt microscopic concavity ideas used in particle models (e.g., Bal\'azs and Sepp\"al\"ainen (2009)), as well as blocking measure bounds like in Ferrari, Kipnis and Saada (1991).</p>{% endraw %}
