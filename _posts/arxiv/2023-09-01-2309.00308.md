{% raw %}<p>Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D</annotation></semantics></math></span> be a Jordan domain of unit capacity. We study the partition function of a planar Coulomb gas in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D</annotation></semantics></math></span> with a hard wall along <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi><mo>=</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>D</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta = \partial D</annotation></semantics></math></span>, \[Z_{n}(D) =\frac 1{n!}\int_{D^n}\prod_{1\le k < \ell \le n}|z_k-z_\ell|^{2} \prod_{k=1}^n d^2z_k.\] We are interested in how the geometry of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta</annotation></semantics></math></span> is reflected in the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> behavior of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>Z</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Z_n(D)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We prove that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a Weil-Petersson quasicircle if and only if \[ \log Z_n(D)= \log Z_n(\mathbb{D}) -I^L(\eta)/12 + o(1), \quad n\to \infty, \] where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>I</mi><mi>L</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>η</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">I^L(\eta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the Loewner energy of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">D</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{D}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the unit disc, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><msub><mi>Z</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">D</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">!</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>π</mi><mi>n</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\log Z_n(\mathbb{D}) = \log n!/\pi^n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We next consider piecewise analytic <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta</annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> corners of interior opening angles <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>π</mi><msub><mi>α</mi><mi>p</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>p</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pi \alpha_p, p=1,\ldots, m</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our main result is the asymptotic formula \[ \log Z_n(D)= \log Z_n(\mathbb{D}) - \frac 16\sum_{p=1}^m \left(\alpha_p+\frac 1{\alpha_p}-2 \right) \log n + o(\log n), \quad n\to \infty, \] which is consistent with physics predictions. The starting point of our analysis is an exact expression for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><msub><mi>Z</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\log Z_{n}(D)</annotation></semantics></math></span> in terms of a Fredholm determinant involving the truncated Grunsky operator for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The proof of the main result is based on careful asymptotic analysis of the Grunsky coefficients. As further applications of our method we also study the Loewner energy and the related Fekete-Pommerenke energy, a quantity appearing in the analysis of Fekete points, for equipotentials approximating the boundary of a domain with corners. We formulate several conjectures and open problems.</p>{% endraw %}
