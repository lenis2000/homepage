{% raw %}<p>We study two generalizations of the asymmetric simple exclusion process with two types of particles. Particles of type 1 can jump over particles of type 2, while particles of type 2 can only influence the jump rates of particles of type 1. We prove that the processes are self-dual and explicitly write the duality function. As an application, an expression for the r-th moment of the exponentiated current is written in terms of r-particle evolution. The construction and proofs of duality are accomplished using symmetry of the quantum groups <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\mathfrak{gl}_3)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">s</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">p</mi></mrow><mn>4</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\mathfrak{sp}_4)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with each node in the Dynkin diagram corresponding to a particle type, and the number of edges corresponding to the jump rates.</p>{% endraw %}
