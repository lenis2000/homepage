{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we continue the study of large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> problems for the Wick renormalized linear sigma model, i.e. <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>-component <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">Φ</mi><mn>4</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Phi^4</annotation></semantics></math></span> model, in two spatial dimensions, using stochastic quantization methods and Dyson--Schwinger equations. We identify the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limiting law of a collection of Wick renormalized <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">O(N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> invariant observables. In particular, under a suitable scaling, the quadratic observables converge in the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit to a mean-zero (singular) Gaussian field denoted by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{Q}</annotation></semantics></math></span> with an explicit covariance; and the observables which are renormalized powers of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2n</annotation></semantics></math></span> converge in the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit to suitably renormalized <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th powers of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{Q}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The quartic interaction term of the model has no effect on the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit of the field, but has nontrivial contributions to the limiting law of the observables, and the renormalization of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th powers of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{Q}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the limit has an interesting finite shift from the standard one. Furthermore, we derive the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N</annotation></semantics></math></span> asymtotic expansion for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-point functions of the quadratic observables by employing graph representations and analyzing the order of each graph from Dyson--Schwinger equations. Finally, turning to the stationary solutions to the stochastic quantization equations, with the Ornstein--Uhlenbeck process being the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limiting dynamic, we derive here its next order correction in stationarity, as described by an SPDE with the right-hand side having explicit fixed-time marginal law which involves the above field <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{Q}</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
