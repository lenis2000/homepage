{% raw %}<p>We consider the edge statistics of Dyson Brownian motion with deterministic initial data. Our main result states that if the initial data has a spectral edge with rough square root behavior down to a scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>η</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub><mo>≥</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta_* \geq N^{-2/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and no outliers, then after times <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>≫</mo><msqrt><msub><mi>η</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t \gg \sqrt{ \eta_*}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the statistics at the spectral edge agree with the GOE/GUE. In particular we obtain the optimal time to equilibrium at the edge <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mi>ε</mi></msup><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t = N^{\varepsilon} / N^{1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> for sufficiently regular initial data. Our methods rely on eigenvalue rigidity results similar to those appearing in [Lee-Schnelli], the coupling idea of [Bourgade-Erd\H{o}s-Yau-Yin] and the energy estimate of [Bourgade-Erd\H{o}s-Yau].</p>{% endraw %}
