{% raw %}<p>This paper studies the mixing behavior of the Asymmetric Simple Exclusion Process (ASEP) on a segment of length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our main result is that for particle densities in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(0,1),</annotation></semantics></math></span> the total-variation cutoff window of ASEP is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the cutoff profile is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>F</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">G</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">U</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">E</mi></mrow></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1-F_{\mathrm{GUE}},</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>F</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">G</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">U</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">E</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F_{\mathrm{GUE}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the Tracy-Widom distribution function. This also gives a new proof of the cutoff itself, shown earlier by Labb\'{e} and Lacoin. Our proof combines coupling arguments, the result of Tracy-Widom about fluctuations of ASEP started from the step initial condition, and exact algebraic identities coming from interpreting the multi-species ASEP as a random walk on a Hecke algebra.</p>{% endraw %}
