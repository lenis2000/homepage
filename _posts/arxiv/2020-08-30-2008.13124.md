{% raw %}<p>Previous works have considered the leading correction term to the scaled limit of various correlation functions and distributions for classical random matrix ensembles and their <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> generalisations at the hard and soft edge. It has been found that the functional form of this correction is given by a derivative operation applied to the leading term. In the present work we compute the leading correction term of the correlation kernel at the spectrum singularity for the circular Jacobi ensemble with Dyson indices <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 1,2</annotation></semantics></math></span> and 4, and also to the spectral density in the corresponding <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensemble with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> even. The former requires an analysis involving the Routh-Romanovski polynomials, while the latter is based on multidimensional integral formulas for generalised hypergeometric series based on Jack polynomials. In all cases this correction term is found to be related to the leading term by a derivative operation.</p>{% endraw %}
