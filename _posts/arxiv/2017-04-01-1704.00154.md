In the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit, they satisfy the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_{N-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span> quantum <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-system. We identify the elements in the spherical <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_{N-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span> DAHA which are represented by these operators, as well as within the quantum toroidal algebra of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>g</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mn>1</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">gl_1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the elliptic Hall algebra. Finally we derive constant term identities for the current that allow to interpret them in terms of shuffle products.
