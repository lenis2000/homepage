{% raw %}<p>Scaling level-spacing distribution functions in the ``bulk of the spectrum'' in random matrix models of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\times N</annotation></semantics></math></span> hermitian matrices and then going to the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, leads to the Fredholm determinant of the sine kernel <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>sin</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>π</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>π</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sin\pi(x-y)/\pi (x-y)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Similarly a double scaling limit at the ``edge of the spectrum'' leads to the Airy kernel <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">A</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">i</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msup><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">A</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">i</mi></mrow><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>−</mo><msup><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">A</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">i</mi></mrow><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">A</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">i</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[{\rm Ai}(x) {\rm Ai}&#x27;(y) -{\rm Ai}&#x27;(x) {\rm Ai}(y)]/(x-y)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We announce analogies for this Airy kernel of the following properties of the sine kernel: the completely integrable system of P.D.E.'s found by Jimbo, Miwa, M{\^o}ri and Sato; the expression, in the case of a single interval, of the Fredholm determinant in terms of a Painlev{\'e} transcendent; the existence of a commuting differential operator; and the fact that this operator can be used in the derivation of asymptotics, for general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, of the probability that an interval contains precisely <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> eigenvalues.</p>{% endraw %}
