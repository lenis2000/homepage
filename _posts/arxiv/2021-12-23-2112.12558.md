{% raw %}<p>We establish analogues of the geometric Pitman <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>M</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2M-X</annotation></semantics></math></span> theorem of Matsumoto and Yor and of the classical Dufresne identity, for a multiplicative random walk on positive definite matrices with Beta type II distributed increments. The Dufresne type identity provides another example of a stochastic matrix recursion, as considered by Chamayou and Letac (J. Theoret. Probab. 12, 1999), that admits an explicit solution.</p>{% endraw %}
