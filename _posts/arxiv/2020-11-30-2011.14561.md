{% raw %}<p>We prove the analogue of the strong Szeg{\H o} limit theorem for a large class of bordered Toeplitz determinants. In particular, by applying our results to the formula of Au-Yang and Perk \cite{YP} for the next-to-diagonal correlations <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">⟨</mo><msub><mi>σ</mi><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></msub><msub><mi>σ</mi><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">⟩</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\langle \sigma_{0,0}\sigma_{N-1,N} \rangle</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the anisotropic square lattice Ising model, we rigorously justify that the next-to-diagonal long-range order is the same as the diagonal and horizontal ones in the low temperature regime. The anisotropy-dependence of the subleading term in the asymptotics of the next-to-diagonal correlations is also established. We use Riemann-Hilbert and operator theory techniques, independently and in parallel, to prove these results.</p>{% endraw %}
