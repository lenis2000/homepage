{% raw %}<p>Consider a continuous time random walk in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Z}</annotation></semantics></math></span> with independent and exponentially distributed jumps <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>±</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pm1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The model in this paper consists in an infinite number of such random walks starting from the complement of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>m</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{-m,-m+1,\ldots,m-1,m\}</annotation></semantics></math></span> at time -t, returning to the same starting positions at time t, and conditioned not to intersect. This yields a determinantal process, whose gap probabilities are given by the Fredholm determinant of a kernel. Thus this model consists of two groups of random walks, which are contained within two ellipses which, with the choice <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>≃</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m\simeq2t</annotation></semantics></math></span> to leading order, just touch: so we have a tacnode. We determine the new limit extended kernel under the scaling <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">⌊</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>t</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>σ</mi><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">⌋</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m=\lfloor2t+\sigma t^{1/3}\rfloor</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span> controls the strength of interaction between the two groups of random walkers.</p>{% endraw %}
