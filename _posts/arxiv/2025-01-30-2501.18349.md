{% raw %}<p>We define multideterminantal probability measures, a family of probability measures on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>k</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mi>n</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[k]^n</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>k</mi><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>k</mi><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[k]=\{1,2,\dots,k\}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, generalizing determinantal measures (which correspond to the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k=2</annotation></semantics></math></span>). We give examples coming from the positive Grassmannian, from the dimer model and from the spanning tree model. We characterize kernels of \emph{pure} <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-determinantal measures as those arising from <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-tuples of Grassmannian elements whose maximal minors have certain sign restrictions. As a special case we construct all kernels of pure determinantal measures via a pair of elements of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><msub><mi>r</mi><mrow><msub><mi>n</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Gr_{n_1,n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> having corresponding Plücker coordinates of the same signs. We also define and completely characterize determinantal probability measures on the permutation group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
