{% raw %}<p>We study longest and shortest directed paths in the following natural model of directed random planar maps: the uniform infinite bipolar-oriented triangulation (UIBOT), which is the local limit of uniform bipolar-oriented triangulations around a typical edge. We construct the Busemann function which measures directed distance to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> along a natural interface in the UIBOT. We show that in the case of longest (resp.\ shortest) directed paths, this Busemann function converges in the scaling limit to a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2/3</annotation></semantics></math></span>-stable L\'evy process (resp.\ a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>4</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">4/3</annotation></semantics></math></span>-stable L\'evy process). We also prove up-to-constants bounds for directed distances in finite bipolar-oriented triangulations sampled from a Boltzmann distribution, and for size-<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> cells in the UIBOT. These bounds imply that in a typical subset of the UIBOT with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> edges, longest directed path lengths are of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mn>3</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>4</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{3/4}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and shortest directed path lengths are of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mn>3</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>8</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{3/8}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. These results give the scaling dimensions for discretizations of the (hypothetical) <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mrow><mn>4</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{4/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-directed Liouville quantum gravity metrics. The main external input in our proof is the bijection of Kenyon-Miller-Sheffield-Wilson (2015). We do not use any continuum theory. We expect that our techniques can also be applied to prove similar results for directed distances in other random planar map models and for longest increasing subsequences in pattern-avoiding permutations.</p>{% endraw %}
