In the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>r</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r=s</annotation></semantics></math></span> the exact form of the global density is computed. The averaged characteristic polynomial for the corresponding generalized eigenvalue problem is calculated in terms of a particular generalized hypergeometric function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mrow><mi>s</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><msub><mi>F</mi><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_{s+1} F_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>. A double contour integral form of the corresponding correlation kernel is derived, which allows the hard edge scaled limit to be computed. The limiting kernel is given in terms of certain Meijer G-functions, and is identical to that found in the recent work of Kuijlaars and Zhang in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
