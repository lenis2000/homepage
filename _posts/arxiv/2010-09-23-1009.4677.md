{% raw %}<p>We compute the exact and limiting smallest eigenvalue distributions for two classes of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Jacobi ensembles not covered by previous studies. In the general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> case, these distributions are given by multivariate hypergeometric <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>2</mn></msub><msubsup><mi>F</mi><mn>1</mn><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>β</mi></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_2F_{1}^{2/\beta}</annotation></semantics></math></span> functions, whose behavior can be analyzed asymptotically for special values of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> which include <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>∈</mo><mn>2</mn><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">N</mi><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0em">+</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta \in 2\mathbb{N}_{+}</annotation></semantics></math></span> as well as for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Interest in these objects stems from their connections (in the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 1,2</annotation></semantics></math></span> cases) to principal submatrices of Haar-distributed (orthogonal, unitary) matrices appearing in randomized, communication-optimal, fast, and stable algorithms for eigenvalue computations \cite{DDH07}, \cite{BDD10}.</p>{% endraw %}
