{% raw %}<p>We focus on derangement characters of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><mi>L</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">GL(n,q)</annotation></semantics></math></span> which depend solely on the dimension of the space of fixed vectors. This family includes Thoma characters which become asymptotically irreducible as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We find explicit decomposition of Thoma characters into irreducibles, construct further derangement characters and seek for extremes in the family of derangement characters.</p>{% endraw %}
