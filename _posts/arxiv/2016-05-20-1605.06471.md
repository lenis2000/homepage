{% raw %}<p>Square ice is a statistical mechanics model for two-dimensional ice, widely believed to have a conformally invariant scaling limit. We associate a Peano (space filling) curve to a square ice configuration, and more generally to a so-called 6-vertex model configuration, and argue that its scaling limit is a space-filling version of the random fractal curve SLE$_\kappa$, Schramm--Loewner evolution with parameter $\kappa$, where $4<\kappa \leq 12+8\sqrt{2}$. For square ice, $\kappa=12$. At the "free-fermion point" of the 6-vertex model, $\kappa=8+4\sqrt{3}$. These unusual values lie outside the classical interval $2\le \kappa \le 8$.</p>{% endraw %}
