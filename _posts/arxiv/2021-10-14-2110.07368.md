{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we study the free energy of the directed polymer on a cylinder of radius <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span> with the inverse temperature <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Assuming the random environment is given by a Gaussian process that is white in time and smooth in space, with an arbitrary compactly supported spatial covariance function, we obtain precise scaling behaviors of the limiting free energy for high temperatures <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≪</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\ll1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, followed by large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi><mo>≫</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L\gg1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, in all dimensions. Our approach is based on a perturbative expansion of the PDE hierarchy satisfied by the multipoint correlation function of the polymer endpoint distribution. For the random environment given by the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1+1</annotation></semantics></math></span> spacetime white noise, we derive an explicit expression of the limiting free energy, confirming the result obtained through the replica method in [12].</p>{% endraw %}
