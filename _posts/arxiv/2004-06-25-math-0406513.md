{% raw %}<p>An essential spanning forest of an infinite graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a spanning forest of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> in which all trees have infinitely many vertices. Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> be an increasing sequence of finite connected subgraphs of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> for which <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>⋃</mo><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\bigcup G_n=G</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Pemantle's arguments imply that the uniform measures on spanning trees of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> converge weakly to an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Aut</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>G</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\operatorname {Aut}(G)</annotation></semantics></math></span>-invariant measure <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>μ</mi><mi>G</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu_G</annotation></semantics></math></span> on essential spanning forests of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show that if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a connected, amenable graph and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Γ</mi><mo>⊂</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">Aut</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>G</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Gamma \subset \operatorname {Aut}(G)</annotation></semantics></math></span> acts quasitransitively on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span>, then <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>μ</mi><mi>G</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu_G</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the unique <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span>-invariant measure on essential spanning forests of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> for which the specific entropy is maximal. This result originated with Burton and Pemantle, who gave a short but incorrect proof in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Γ</mi><mo>≅</mo><msup><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mi>d</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Gamma\cong\mathbb{Z}^d</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Lyons discovered the error and asked about the more general statement that we prove.</p>{% endraw %}
