{% raw %}<p>We study numerically the two-point correlation functions of height functions in the six-vertex model with domain wall boundary conditions. The correlation functions and the height functions are computed by the Markov chain Monte-Carlo algorithm. Particular attention is paid to the free fermionic point (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Delta=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>), for which the correlation functions are obtained analytically in the thermodynamic limit. A good agreement of the exact and numerical results for the free fermionic point allows us to extend calculations to the disordered (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">|\Delta|&lt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span>) phase and to monitor the logarithm-like behavior of correlation functions there. For the antiferroelectric (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Delta&lt;-1</annotation></semantics></math></span>) phase, the exponential decrease of correlation functions is observed.</p>{% endraw %}
