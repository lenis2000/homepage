{% raw %}<p>We study scaling limits of periodically weighted skew plane partitions with semilocal interactions and general boundary conditions. The semilocal interactions correspond to the Macdonald symmetric functions which are <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>-deformations of the Schur symmetric functions. We show that the height functions converge to a deterministic limit shape and that the global fluctuations are given by the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional Gaussian free field as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q,t\to 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the mesh size goes to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Specializing to the noninteracting case, this verifies the Kenyon-Okounkov conjecture for the case of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>r</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">v</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">l</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">u</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">e</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r^{\mathrm{volume}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> measure under general boundary conditions. Our approach uses difference operators on Macdonald processes.</p>{% endraw %}
