{% raw %}<p>We consider the one dimensional totally asymmetric simple exclusion process with initial product distribution with densities <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>≤</mo><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo>&lt;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>≤</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0 \leq \rho_0 &lt; \rho_1 &lt;...&lt; \rho_n \leq 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> in $(-\infty,c_1\ve^{-1})$, $[c_1\ve^{-1},c_2\epsilon^{-1}),...,[c_n \ve^{-1}, + \infty)$, respectively. The initial distribution has shocks (discontinuities) at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ϵ</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><msub><mi>c</mi><mi>k</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon^{-1}c_k</annotation></semantics></math></span>, k=1,...,n and we assume that in the corresponding macroscopic Burgers equation the n shocks meet in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>r</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r^*</annotation></semantics></math></span> at time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^*</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The microscopic position of the shocks is represented by second class particles whose distribution in the scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ϵ</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon^{-1/2}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is shown to converge to a function of n independent Gaussian random variables representing the fluctuations of these particles ``just before the meeting''. We show that the density field at time $\ve^{-1}t^*$, in the scale $\ve^{-1/2}$ and as seen from $\ve^{-1}r^*$ converges weakly to a random measure with piecewise constant density as $\ve \to 0$; the points of discontinuity depend on these limiting Gaussian variables. As a corollary we show that, as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϵ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon\to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the distribution of the process at site $\epsilon^{-1}r^*+\ve^{-1/2}a$ at time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ϵ</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><msup><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon^{-1}t^*</annotation></semantics></math></span> tends to a non trivial convex combination of the product measures with densities <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mi>k</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho_k</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the weights of the combination being explicitly computable.</p>{% endraw %}
