{% raw %}<p>Half-space models in the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) universality class exhibit rich boundary phenomena that alter the asymptotic behavior familiar from their full-space counterparts. A distinguishing feature of these systems is the presence of a boundary parameter that governs a transition between subcritical, critical, and supercritical regimes, characterized by different scaling exponents and fluctuation statistics. In this paper we construct the pinned half-space Airy line ensemble <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">h</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi></mrow><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{hs}; \infty}</annotation></semantics></math></span> on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[0,\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span> -- a natural half-space analogue of the Airy line ensemble -- expected to arise as the universal scaling limit of supercritical half-space KPZ models. The ensemble <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">h</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi></mrow><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{hs}; \infty}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is obtained as the weak limit of the critical half-space Airy line ensembles <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">h</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi></mrow><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>ϖ</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{hs}; \varpi}</annotation></semantics></math></span> introduced in arXiv:2505.01798 as the boundary parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϖ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varpi</annotation></semantics></math></span> tends to infinity. We show that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">h</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi></mrow><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{hs}; \infty}</annotation></semantics></math></span> has a Pfaffian point process structure with an explicit correlation kernel and that, after a parabolic shift, it satisfies a one-sided Brownian Gibbs property described by pairwise pinned Brownian motions. Far from the origin, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">h</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi></mrow><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}^{\mathrm{hs}; \infty}</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges to the standard Airy line ensemble, while at the origin its distribution coincides with that of the ordered eigenvalues (with doubled multiplicity) of the stochastic Airy operator with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 4</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
