{% raw %}<p>We consider the Glauber dynamics of a ferromagnetic Ising-Kac model on a three-dimensional periodic lattice of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>N</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2N + 1)3</annotation></semantics></math></span>, in which the flipping rate of each spin depends on an average field in a large neighborhood of radius <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mi>γ</mi></mfrac><mo>&lt;</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac1\gamma &lt;&lt; N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We study the random fluctuations of a suitably rescaled coarse-grained spin field as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma \to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>; we show that near the mean-field value of the critical temperature, the process converges in distribution to the solution of the dynamical <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi mathvariant="normal">Φ</mi><mn>3</mn><mn>4</mn></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Phi^4_3</annotation></semantics></math></span> model on a torus. Our result settles a conjectured from Giacomin, Lebowitz and Presutti (DOI:10.1090/SURV/064/03). The dynamical <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi mathvariant="normal">Φ</mi><mn>3</mn><mn>4</mn></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Phi^4_3</annotation></semantics></math></span> model is given by a non-linear stochastic partial differential equation (SPDE) which is driven by an additive space-time white noise and which requires renormalisation of the non-linearity. A rigorous notion of solution for this SPDE and its renormalisation is provided by the framework of regularity structures arXiv:1303.5113. As in the two-dimensional case arXiv:1410.1179, the renormalisation corresponds to a small shift of the inverse temperature of the discrete system away from its mean-field value.</p>{% endraw %}
