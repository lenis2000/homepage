{% raw %}<p>We study a Markov process on a system of interlacing particles. At large times the particles fill a domain that depends on a parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eps&gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The domain has two cusps, one pointing up and one pointing down. In the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>↓</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon\downarrow 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> the cusps touch, thus forming a tacnode. The main result of the paper is a derivation of the local correlation kernel around the tacnode in the transition regime <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>↓</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon \downarrow 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We also prove that the local process interpolates between the Pearcey process and the GUE minor process.</p>{% endraw %}
