{% raw %}<p>Consider a Markov chain <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><msub><mo stretchy="false">}</mo><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{X_n\}_{n\ge 0}</annotation></semantics></math></span> with an ergodic probability measure <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>π</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pi</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Ψ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Psi</annotation></semantics></math></span> a function on the state space of the chain, with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>-tails with respect to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>π</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pi</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha\in (0,2)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We find sufficient conditions on the probability transition to prove convergence in law of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>α</mi></mrow></msup><msubsup><mo>∑</mo><mi>n</mi><mi>N</mi></msubsup><mi mathvariant="normal">Ψ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{1/\alpha}\sum_n^N \Psi(X_n)</annotation></semantics></math></span> to a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>-stable law. ``Martingale approximation'' approach and ``coupling'' approach give two different sets of conditions. We extend these results to continuous time Markov jump processes <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>t</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_t</annotation></semantics></math></span>, whose skeleton chain satisfies our assumptions. If waiting time between jumps has finite expectation, we prove convergence of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>α</mi></mrow></msup><msubsup><mo>∫</mo><mn>0</mn><mrow><mi>N</mi><mi>t</mi></mrow></msubsup><mi>V</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>s</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>d</mi><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{-1/\alpha}\int_0^{Nt} V(X_s) ds</annotation></semantics></math></span> to a stable process. In the case of waiting times with infinite average, we prove convergence to a Mittag-Leffler process.</p>{% endraw %}
