{% raw %}<p>We study analogues of the Yangian of the Lie algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>g</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">gl_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the other classical Lie algebras <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>o</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">so_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">sp_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We call them twisted Yangians. They are coideal subalgebras in the Yangian <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Y</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>g</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y(gl_N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>g</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">gl_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> and admit homomorphisms onto the universal enveloping algebras <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>o</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U(so_N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U(sp_N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> respectively. In every twisted Yangian we construct a family of maximal commutative subalgebras parametrized by the regular semisimple elements of the corresponding classical Lie algebra. The images in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>o</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U(so_N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U(sp_N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of these subalgebras are also maximal commutative.</p>{% endraw %}
