{% raw %}<p>We construct two types of equilibrium dynamics of infinite particle systems in a locally compact Polish space <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span>, for which certain fermion point processes are invariant. The Glauber dynamics is a birth-and-death process in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span>, while in the case of the Kawasaki dynamics interacting particles randomly hop over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We establish conditions on generators of both dynamics under which corresponding conservative Markov processes exist.</p>{% endraw %}
