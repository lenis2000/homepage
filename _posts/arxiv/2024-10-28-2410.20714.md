{% raw %}<p>We investigate the probability that a random polynomial with independent, mean-zero and finite variance coefficients has no real zeros. Specifically, we consider a random polynomial of degree <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2n</annotation></semantics></math></span> with coefficients given by an i.i.d. sequence of mean-zero, variance-1 random variables, multiplied by an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mi>α</mi><mn>2</mn></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac\alpha{2}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-regularly varying sequence for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&gt;-1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show that the probability of no real zeros is asymptotically <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>b</mi><mi>α</mi></msub><mo>+</mo><msub><mi>b</mi><mn>0</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{-2(b_\alpha+b_0)}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>b</mi><mi>α</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b_\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the persistence exponents of a mean-zero, one-dimensional stationary Gaussian processes with covariance function as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">e</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">c</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">h</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>s</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathrm{sech}((t-s)/2)^{\alpha+1}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our work generalizes the previous results of Dembo et al. [DPSZ02] and Dembo \& Mukherjee [DM15] by removing the requirement of finite moments of all order or Gaussianity. In particular, in the special case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha= 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, our findings confirm a conjecture by Poonen and Stoll [PS99, Section 9.1] concerning random polynomials with i.i.d. coefficients.</p>{% endraw %}
