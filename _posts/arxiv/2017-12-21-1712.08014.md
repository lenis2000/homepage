{% raw %}<p>We study the interpolation Macdonald functions, remarkable inhomogeneous generalizations of Macdonald functions, and a sequence <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>A</mi><mn>1</mn></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo><msup><mi>A</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A^1, A^2, \ldots</annotation></semantics></math></span> of commuting operators that are diagonalized by them. Such a sequence of operators arises in the projective limit of finite families of commuting q-difference operators studied by Okounkov, Knop and Sahi. The main theorem is an explicit formula for the operators <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>A</mi><mi>k</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A^k</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our formula involves the family of Hall-Littlewood functions and a new family of inhomogeneous Hall-Littlewood functions, for which we give an explicit construction and identify as a degeneration of the interpolation Macdonald functions in the regime <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q \rightarrow 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This article is inspired by the recent papers of Nazarov-Sklyanin on Macdonald and Sekiguchi-Debiard operators, and our main theorem is an extension of their results.</p>{% endraw %}
