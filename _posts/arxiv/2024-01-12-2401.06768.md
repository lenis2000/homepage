{% raw %}<p>A minimal surface in a random environment (MSRE) is a surface which minimizes the sum of its elastic energy and its environment potential energy, subject to prescribed boundary conditions. Apart from their intrinsic interest, such surfaces are further motivated by connections with disordered spin systems and first-passage percolation models. We wish to study the geometry of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional minimal surfaces in a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>d</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(d+n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional random environment. Specializing to a model that we term harmonic MSRE, in an ``independent'' random environment, we rigorously establish bounds on the geometric and energetic fluctuations of the minimal surface, as well as versions of the scaling relation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>χ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>ξ</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>d</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\chi=2\xi+d-2</annotation></semantics></math></span> that ties together these two types of fluctuations. In particular, we prove, for all values of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, that the surfaces are delocalized in dimensions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d\le 4</annotation></semantics></math></span> and localized in dimensions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>5</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d\ge 5</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Moreover, the surface delocalizes with power-law fluctuations when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d\le 3</annotation></semantics></math></span> and sub-power-law fluctuations when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d=4</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our localization results apply also to harmonic minimal surfaces in a periodic random environment.</p>{% endraw %}
