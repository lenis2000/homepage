{% raw %}<p>The <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG model introduced in Aggarwal, Borodin, and Wheeler (2021) is a deformed version of the polynuclear growth (PNG) model. In this paper, we prove the hydrodynamic limit of the model using soft techniques. One key element of the proof is the construction of a colored version of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG model, which allows us to apply the superadditive ergodic theorem and obtain the hydrodynamic limit, albeit without identifying the limiting constant. We then find this constant by proving a law of large numbers for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>-points, which generalizes Groeneboom (2001). Along the way, we construct the stationary <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>-PNG model and prove a version of Burke's theorem for it.</p>{% endraw %}
