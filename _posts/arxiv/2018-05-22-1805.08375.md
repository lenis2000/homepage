{% raw %}<p>We consider the number of partitions of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> whose Young diagrams fit inside an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>×</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m \times \ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> rectangle; equivalently, we study the coefficients of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-binomial coefficient <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mo fence="true">(</mo><mfrac linethickness="0px"><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>+</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><mi>m</mi></mfrac><mo fence="true">)</mo></mrow><mi>q</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\binom{m+\ell}{m}_q</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We obtain sharp asymptotics throughout the regime <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi><mo>=</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">Θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell = \Theta (m)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">Θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>m</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n = \Theta (m^2)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Previously, sharp asymptotics were derived by Tak\'acs only in the regime where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi><mi>m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msqrt><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msqrt><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">|n - \ell m /2| = O(\sqrt{\ell m (\ell + m)})</annotation></semantics></math></span> using a local central limit theorem. Our approach is to solve a related large deviation problem: we describe the tilted measure that produces configurations whose bounding rectangle has the given aspect ratio and is filled to the given proportion. Our results are sufficiently sharp to yield the first asymptotic estimates on the consecutive differences of these numbers when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is increased by one and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m, \ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> remain the same, hence significantly refining Sylvester's unimodality theorem.</p>{% endraw %}
