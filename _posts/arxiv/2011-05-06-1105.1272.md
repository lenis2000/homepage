Probability distributions on the set of Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns of depth <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> arise naturally as distributions of eigenvalue minor processes of random Hermitian matrices of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In particular we consider the case where the eigenvalues of the random matrix are fixed. This corresponds to choosing uniformly from the set of Gelfand-Tsetlin patterns whose <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mtext>th</mtext></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^\text{th}</annotation></semantics></math></span> level is fixed at the eigenvalues of the matrix.
