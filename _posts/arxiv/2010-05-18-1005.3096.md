{% raw %}<p>A bordering of GUE matrices is considered, in which the bordered row consists of zero mean complex Gaussians N<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>σ</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mo>+</mo><mi>i</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">N</mi><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>σ</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[0,\sigma/2] + i {\rm N}[0,\sigma/2]</annotation></semantics></math></span> off the diagonal, and the real Gaussian N<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>μ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>σ</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msqrt><mn>2</mn></msqrt><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[\mu,\sigma/\sqrt{2}]</annotation></semantics></math></span> on the diagonal. We compute the explicit form of the eigenvalue probability function for such matrices, as well as that for matrices obtained by repeating the bordering. The correlations are in general determinantal, and in the single bordering case the explicit form of the correlation kernel is computed. In the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit it is shown that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> and/or <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span> can be tuned to induce a separation of the largest eigenvalue. This effect is shown to be controlled by a single parameter, universal correlation kernel.</p>{% endraw %}
