{% raw %}<p>The bijection between composition structures and random closed subsets of the unit interval implies that the composition structures associated with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>∩</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S \cap [0,1]</annotation></semantics></math></span> for a self-similar random set <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>⊂</mo><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo>+</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S\subset {\mathbb R}_+</annotation></semantics></math></span> are those which are consistent with respect to a simple truncation operation. Using the standard coding of compositions by finite strings of binary digits starting with a 1, the random composition of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is defined by the first <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> terms of a random binary sequence of infinite length. The locations of 1s in the sequence are the places visited by an increasing time-homogeneous Markov chain on the positive integers if and only if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>W</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S = \exp(-W)</annotation></semantics></math></span> for some stationary regenerative random subset <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the real line. Complementing our study in previous papers, we identify self-similar Markovian composition structures associated with the two-parameter family of partition structures.</p>{% endraw %}
