{% raw %}<p>In this article we investigate the behavior of multi-matrix unitary invariant models under a potential <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mi>β</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mi>β</mi><mi>U</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_\beta=\beta U+W</annotation></semantics></math></span> when the inverse temperature <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> becomes very large. We first prove, under mild hypothesis on the functionals <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U,W</annotation></semantics></math></span> that as soon at these potentials are "confining" at infinity, the sequence of spectral distribution of the matrices are tight when the dimension goes to infinity. Their limit points are solutions of Dyson-Schwinger's equations. Next we investigate a few specific models, most importantly the "strong single variable model" where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a sum of potentials in a single matrix and the "strong commutator model" where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo>=</mo><mo>−</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>Y</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U = -[X,Y]^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
