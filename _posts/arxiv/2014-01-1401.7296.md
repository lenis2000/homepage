{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we investigate the mixing time of the simple exclusion process on the circle with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> sites, with a number of particle <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k(N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> tending to infinity, both from the worst initial condition and from a typical initial condition. We show that the worst-case mixing time is asymptotically equivalent to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>8</mn><msup><mi>π</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(8\pi^2)^{-1}N^2\log k</annotation></semantics></math></span>, while the cutoff window, is identified to be <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Starting from a typical condition, we show that there is no cutoff and that the mixing time is of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
