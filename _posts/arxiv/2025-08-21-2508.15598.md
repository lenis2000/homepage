{% raw %}<p>We construct a family of invariant measures from the perspective of a shock in the KPZ fixed point. These measures are parameterized by a positive number <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>θ</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\theta&gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and are supported on functions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>f</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f</annotation></semantics></math></span> satisfying <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mi>lim</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></msub><mfrac><mrow><mi>f</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow></mfrac><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>θ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lim_{|x| \to \infty} \frac{f(x)}{|x|} = 2\theta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Each can be described as the sum of a Brownian motion and an independent Bessel-<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">3</annotation></semantics></math></span> process with drift. We show that these measures appear as the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit of the measures constructed by Barraquand, Corwin, and Yang for the conjectural open KPZ fixed point on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>L</mi><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[0,L]</annotation></semantics></math></span>, after recentering by an appropriately defined shock location. Furthermore, we show that, with respect to the standard, deterministic recentering at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x = 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, all extremal invariant measures for the KPZ fixed point are Brownian motions with drift. To do this, we first show that any extremal invariant measures must be supported on functions having fixed asymptotic slopes at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>±</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pm \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Using a one-force-one-solution principle from the work of Busani, Seppäläinen, and the second author, this rules out all other invariant measures except those having left slope <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>θ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">-2\theta</annotation></semantics></math></span> and right slope <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>θ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">+2\theta</annotation></semantics></math></span> for some <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>θ</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\theta&gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. To handle this case, we derive the limiting fluctuations of the shock for a special choice of initial condition. Additionally, we derive the limiting fluctuations of the shock for the case of the invariant measure from the perspective of a shock, and for the case of initial data <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>f</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>θ</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f(x) = 2\theta|x|</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
