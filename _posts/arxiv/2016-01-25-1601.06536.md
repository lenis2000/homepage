{% raw %}<p>We consider discretisations of the Macdonald--Mehta integrals from the theory of finite reflection groups. For the classical groups, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">A</mi><mrow><mi>r</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathrm{A}_{r-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">B</mi><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathrm{B}_r</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">D</mi><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathrm{D}_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we provide closed-form evaluations in those cases for which the Weyl denominators featuring in the summands have exponents <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our proofs for the exponent-<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1</annotation></semantics></math></span> cases rely on identities for classical group characters, while most of the formulas for the exponent-<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2</annotation></semantics></math></span> cases are derived from a transformation formula for elliptic hypergeometric series for the root system <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">B</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">C</mi></mrow><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathrm{BC}_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As a byproduct of our results, we obtain closed-form product formulas for the (ordinary and signed) enumeration of orthogonal and symplectic tableaux contained in a box.</p>{% endraw %}
