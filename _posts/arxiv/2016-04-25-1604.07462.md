{% raw %}<p>There is a natural left and right invariant Haar measure associated with the matrix groups GL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and SL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> due to Siegel. For the associated volume to be finite it is necessary to truncate the groups by imposing a bound on the norm, or in the case of SL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, by restricting to a fundamental domain. We compute the asymptotic volumes associated with the Haar measure for GL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and SL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> matrices in the case of that the operator norm lies between <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>R</mi><mn>1</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R_1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msub><mi>R</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/R_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the former, and this norm, or alternatively the 2-norm, is bounded by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the latter. By a result of Duke, Rundnick and Sarnak, such asymptotic formulas in the case of SL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> imply an asymptotic counting formula for matrices in SL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb Z)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We discuss too the sampling of SL<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_N(\mathbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> matrices from the truncated sets. By then using lattice reduction to a fundamental domain, we obtain histograms approximating the probability density functions of the lengths and pairwise angles of shortest length bases vectors in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N=2</annotation></semantics></math></span> and 3, or equivalently of shortest linearly independent vectors in the corresponding random lattice. In the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N=2</annotation></semantics></math></span> these distributions are evaluated explicitly.</p>{% endraw %}
