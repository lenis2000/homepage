{% raw %}<p>It is well known that the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials, which are <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> specializations of the Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P_\lambda(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, expand positively as the sum of Schur polynomials. Macdonald polynomials have a quasisymmetric refinement: the quasisymmetric Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>γ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_\gamma(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and a nonsymmetric refinement: the ASEP polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>f</mi><mi>α</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f_\alpha(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We study the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> specializations of both these families of polynomials and show analogous properties: the quasisymmetric Macdonald polynomials expand positively as a sum of quasisymmetric Schur functions, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mtext>QS</mtext><mi>γ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{QS}_\gamma(X)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and the ASEP polynomials expand positively as a sum of Demazure atoms, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mi>α</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}_\alpha(X)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As a corollary of the latter, we prove more generally that any permuted basement Macdonald polynomial has a positive expansion in the Demazure atoms at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We give a description of the structure coefficients of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>γ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_\gamma(X;q,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>f</mi><mi>α</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f_\alpha(X;q,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span> in both cases in terms of the charge statistic on a restricted set of semistandard tableaux.</p>{% endraw %}
