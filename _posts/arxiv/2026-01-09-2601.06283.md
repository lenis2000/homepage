{% raw %}<p>We develop the basic theory of eigenvalues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-adic random matrices, analogous to the classical theory for random matrices over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{R}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{C}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Such eigenvalue statistics were proposed as a model for the zeroes of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-adic <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span>-functions by Ellenberg-Jain-Venkatesh, who computed the limiting distribution of the number of eigenvalues in a unit disc. We compute the full joint distribution of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> eigenvalues of an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n \times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> matrix with Haar distribution, obtaining Coulomb gas type formulas as in the archimedean case, with Vandermonde terms leading to eigenvalue repulsion. From these Coulomb gas density functions we derive asymptotics of eigenvalue statistics as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. These include exact computations, such as a closed form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>θ</mi><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><msqrt><mi>p</mi></msqrt><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>x</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>y</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>p</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho(x,y) = 1 - \theta_3(-\sqrt{p};||x-y||^2/p)</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the limiting pair correlation of eigenvalues in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mi>p</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Z}_p</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and similar results in quadratic extensions. Such formulas yield concrete numerical predictions on zeroes of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-adic <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span>-functions. For eigenvalues in arbitrary extensions of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Q</mi><mi>p</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Q}_p</annotation></semantics></math></span> we also give precise estimates on their pair-repulsion and expected number of eigenvalues in each extension. Finally, we compute the asymptotic probability that all eigenvalues lie in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mi>p</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Z}_p</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our proofs combine results from several distinct areas: <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-adic orbital integrals, roots of random <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-adic polynomials, the Sawin-Wood moment method for random modules, and Markov chains associated with measures on integer partitions.</p>{% endraw %}
