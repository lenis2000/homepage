{% raw %}<p>We consider a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-TASEP model started from step initial condition where all but finitely many particles have speed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a few particles are slower. It is shown in [9] that the rescaled particles position of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-TASEP with identical hopping rates obeys a central limit theorem \`a la Tracy-Widom. We adapt this work to the case of different hopping rates and show that one observes the so-called BBP transition. Our proof is a refinement of Ferrari-Vet\"o's and does not require any condition on the parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span> nor the macroscopic position of particles.</p>{% endraw %}
