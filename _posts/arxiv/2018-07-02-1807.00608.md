{% raw %}<p>We analytically study the long time and large space asymptotics of a new broad class of solutions of the KdV equation introduced by Dyachenko, Zakharov, and Zakharov. These solutions are characterized by a Riemann--Hilbert problem which we show arises as the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> of a gas of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>-solitons. We show that this gas of solitons in the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> is slowly approaching a cnoidal wave solution for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>→</mo><mo>−</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x \to - \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> (up to terms of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{O} (1/x)</annotation></semantics></math></span>), while approaching zero exponentially fast for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>→</mo><mo>+</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x\to+\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We establish an asymptotic description of the gas of solitons for large times that is valid over the entire spatial domain, in terms of Jacobi elliptic functions.</p>{% endraw %}
