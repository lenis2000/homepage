{% raw %}<p>We prove the second author's "denominator conjecture" [40] concerning the common denominators of coefficients of certain linear forms in zeta values. These forms were recently constructed to obtain lower bounds for the dimension of the vector space over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb Q</annotation></semantics></math></span> spanned by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>m</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>h</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1,\zeta(m),\zeta(m+2),...,\zeta(m+2h)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h</annotation></semantics></math></span> are integers such that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m\ge2</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h\ge0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In particular, we immediately get the following results as corollaries: at least one of the eight numbers <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>5</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>7</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>19</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta(5),\zeta(7),...,\zeta(19)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is irrational, and there exists an odd integer <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>j</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">j</annotation></semantics></math></span> between 5 and 165 such that 1, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>3</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta(3)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta(j)</annotation></semantics></math></span> are linearly independent over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Q}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This strengthens some recent results in [41] and [8], respectively. We also prove a related conjecture, due to Vasilyev [49], and as well a conjecture, due to Zudilin [55], on certain rational approximations of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>4</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta(4)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The proofs are based on a hypergeometric identity between a single sum and a multiple sum due to Andrews [3]. We hope that it will be possible to apply our construction to the more general linear forms constructed by Zudilin [56], with the ultimate goal of strengthening his result that one of the numbers <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>5</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>7</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>9</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>11</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta(5),\zeta(7),\zeta(9),\zeta(11)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is irrational.</p>{% endraw %}
