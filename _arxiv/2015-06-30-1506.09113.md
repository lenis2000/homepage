{% raw %}<p>A completely elementary and self-contained proof of convergence of Gaussian multiplicative chaos is given. The argument shows further that the limiting random measure is nontrivial in the entire subcritical phase <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>γ</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msqrt><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>d</mi></mrow></msqrt><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\gamma &lt; \sqrt{2d})</annotation></semantics></math></span> and that the limit is universal (i.e., the limiting measure is independent of the regularisation of the underlying field)</p>{% endraw %}
