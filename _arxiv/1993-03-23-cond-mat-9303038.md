{% raw %}<p>We consider an exclusion process, with particles injected with rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> at the origin and removed with rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> at the right boundary of a one-dimensional chain of sites. The particles are allowed to hop onto unoccupied sites, to the right only. For the special case of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha=\beta=1</annotation></semantics></math></span> the model was solved previously by Derrida et al. Here we extend the solution to general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha,\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The phase diagram obtained from our exact solution differs from the one predicted by the mean field approximation.</p>{% endraw %}
