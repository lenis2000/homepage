{% raw %}<p>The positive Grassmannian <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><msubsup><mi>r</mi><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><mrow><mo>≥</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Gr_{k,n}^{\geq 0}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the subset of the real Grassmannian where all Pl\"ucker coordinates are nonnegative. It has a beautiful combinatorial structure as well as connections to statistical physics, integrable systems, and scattering amplitudes. We will explain how ideas from oriented matroids, tropical geometry, and cluster algebras shed light on the structure of the positive Grassmannian and the amplituhedron.</p>{% endraw %}
