{% raw %}<p>We revisit the transfer-matrix approach to directed polymers in random media and show that a single ensemble of random transfer-matrix products provides a unified realization of the canonical one-point fluctuation laws in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(1+1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> dimensions. For a fixed disorder realization, the polymer partition function is obtained as a contraction of the same product matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and different contractions reproduce the standard KPZ subclasses: Tracy-Widom GUE (point-to-point), GOE (point-to-line), GSE (half-space point-to-point), and Baik-Rains (stationary line-to-point). In each case, we observe <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^{1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> free-energy fluctuation growth and convergence of standardized distributions with low-order cumulants close to the corresponding universal benchmarks. Viewing geometry-dependent subclasses as projections of a single matrix-product ensemble naturally suggests additional observables intrinsic to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As an example, we examine the leading eigenvalue <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>λ</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda_1(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> whose logarithm exhibits <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^{1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> scaling, while its standardized statistics remain distinct from the canonical Tracy-Widom laws within the accessible range. This transfer-matrix perspective thus organizes known KPZ one-point subclasses within a finite-dimensional matrix framework and highlights matrix-level fluctuation observables beyond geometry-selected universality classes.</p>{% endraw %}
