{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we study lower tail probabilities of the height function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>M</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{h}(M,N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the stochastic six-vertex model. We introduce a novel combinatorial approach to demonstrate that the tail probabilities <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">P</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="fraktur">h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>M</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≥</mo><mi>r</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{P}(\mathfrak{h}(M,N) \ge r)</annotation></semantics></math></span> are log-concave in a certain weak sense. We prove further that for each <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> the lower tail of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi mathvariant="fraktur">h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo stretchy="false">⌊</mo><mi>α</mi><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">⌋</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">-\mathfrak{h}(\lfloor \alpha N \rfloor, N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> satisfies a Large Deviation Principle (LDP) with speed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a rate function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi mathvariant="normal">Φ</mi><mi>α</mi><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Phi_\alpha^{(-)}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which is given by the infimal deconvolution between a certain energy integral and a parabola. Our analysis begins with a distributional identity from BO17 [arXiv:1608.01564], which relates the lower tail of the height function, after a random shift, with a multiplicative functional of the Schur measure. Tools from potential theory allow us to extract the LDP for the shifted height function. We then use our weak log-concavity result, along with a deconvolution scheme from our earlier paper [arXiv:2307.01179], to convert the LDP for the shifted height function to the LDP for the stochastic six-vertex model height function.</p>{% endraw %}
