{% raw %}<p>We study general properties for the family of stochastic processes with polynomial regression property, that is that every conditional moment of the process is a polynomial. It turns out that then there exists a family of polynomial martingales <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mo fence="true">{</mo><msub><mi>M</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>t</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo fence="true">}</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\left\{ M_{n}(X_{t},t)\right\}_{n\geq1}</annotation></semantics></math></span> that contains complete information on the distribution (both marginal and transitional) of the process. We specify conditions expressed in terms of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mi>n</mi><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msubsup><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_{n}^{\prime}s</annotation></semantics></math></span> under which a given process has independent increments and further is a Levy process, contains reversed martingales, is a harness or quadratic harness. We also give conditions under which some of these martingales are also reversed martingales.</p>{% endraw %}
