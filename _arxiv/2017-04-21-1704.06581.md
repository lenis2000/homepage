{% raw %}<p>We study a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2+1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional stochastic interface growth model, that is believed to belong to the so-called Anisotropic KPZ (AKPZ) universality class [Borodin and Ferrari, 2014]. It can be seen either as a two-dimensional interacting particle process with drift, that generalizes the one-dimensional Hammersley process [Aldous and Diaconis 1995, Seppalainen 1996], or as an irreversible dynamics of lozenge tilings of the plane [Borodin and Ferrari 2014, Toninelli 2015]. Our main result is a hydrodynamic limit: the interface height profile converges, after a hyperbolic scaling of space and time, to the solution of a non-linear first order PDE of Hamilton-Jacobi type with non-convex Hamiltonian (non-convexity of the Hamiltonian is a distinguishing feature of the AKPZ class). We prove the result in two situations: (i) for smooth initial profiles and times smaller than the time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>h</mi><mi>o</mi><mi>c</mi><mi>k</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_{shock}</annotation></semantics></math></span> when singularities (shocks) appear or (ii) for all times, including <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><msub><mi>T</mi><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>h</mi><mi>o</mi><mi>c</mi><mi>k</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t&gt;T_{shock}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, if the initial profile is convex. In the latter case, the height profile converges to the viscosity solution of the PDE. As an important ingredient, we introduce a Harris-type graphical construction for the process.</p>{% endraw %}
