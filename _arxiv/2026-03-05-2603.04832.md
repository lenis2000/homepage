{% raw %}<p>We prove the equivalent of the Baik, Ben Arous, Péché (2004) phenomenon for a novel, doubly sparse model where both the Wigner noise matrix and signal vector(s) are sparse. Specifically, we consider a deformed sub-Gaussian sparse Wigner ensemble with a fixed number of sub-Gaussian spike vectors of the same-order sparsity added. We show that spike vectors with signals greater than one are correlated with the top eigenvectors of the deformed ensemble and that each spike vector of signal greater than one induces an outlier eigenvalue. Notably, our results hold in the supercritical sparsity regime for the Wigner matrix (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>≫</mo><mfrac><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><mi>n</mi></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q \gg \frac{\log n}{n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>) and for any sparse spike vector with an unbounded number of entries (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mi>p</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">np\to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>). No further relationship between the sparsities of the noise matrix (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>) and spike vectors (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>) is necessary. This generalizes the work of Benaych-Georges and Nadakuditi (2010) and Péché (2005).</p>{% endraw %}
