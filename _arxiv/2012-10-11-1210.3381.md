{% raw %}<p>We review some occurrences of Painlev\'e II transcendents in the study of two-dimensional Yang-Mills theory, fluctuation formulas for growth models, and as distribution functions within random matrix theory. We first discuss settings in which the parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the Painlev\'e equation is zero, and the boundary condition is that of the Hasting-MacLeod solution. As well as expressions involving the Painlev\'e transcendent itself, one encounters the sigma form of the Painlev\'e II equation, and Lax pair equations in which the Painlev\'e transcendent occurs as coefficients. We then consider settings which give rise to general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> Painlev\'e II transcendents. In a particular random matrix setting, new results for the corresponding boundary conditions in the cases <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>=</mo><mo>±</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha = \pm 1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, 1 and 2 are presented.</p>{% endraw %}
