We introduce its virtual group algebra, a completion of the conventional group algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">C</mi><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>S</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb C[S(\infty)]</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The virtual group algebra is obtained by taking large-<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> limits of the finite-dimensional group algebras <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">C</mi><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>S</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb C[S(n)]</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the so-called tame representations of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S(\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We establish a connection with the centralizer construction of Molev-Olshanski [J. Algebra, 237 (2001), 302-341; arXiv:math/0002165] and Drinfeld-Lusztig degenerate affine Hecke algebras. This makes it possible to describe the structure of the virtual group algebra.
