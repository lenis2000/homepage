{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we study the edge behavior of Dyson Brownian motion with general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Specifically, we consider the scenario where the averaged initial density near the edge, on the scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>η</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta_*</annotation></semantics></math></span>, is lower bounded by a square root profile. Under this assumption, we establish that the fluctuations of extreme particles are bounded by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>n</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\log n)^{{\rm O}(1)}n^{-2/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> after time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>C</mi><msqrt><msub><mi>η</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C\sqrt{\eta_*}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our result improves previous edge rigidity results from [1,24] which require both lower and upper bounds of the averaged initial density. Additionally, combining with [24], our rigidity estimates are used to prove that the distribution of extreme particles converges to the Tracy-Widom <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> distribution in short time.</p>{% endraw %}
