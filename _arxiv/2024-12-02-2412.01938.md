{% raw %}<p>Heckman-Polychronakos operators form a prominent family of commuting differential-difference operators defined in terms of the Dunkl operators <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="script">D</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal D_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="script">P</mi><mi>m</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><msubsup><mo>∑</mo><mrow><mi>i</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><mi>N</mi></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>x</mi><mi>i</mi></msub><msub><mi mathvariant="script">D</mi><mi>i</mi></msub><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>m</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal P_m= \sum_{i=1}^N (x_i \mathcal D_i)^m</annotation></semantics></math></span>. They have been known since 1990s in connection with trigonometric Calogero-Moser-Sutherland Hamiltonian and Jack symmetric polynomials. We explicitly compute the eigenvalues of these operators for symmetric and skew-symmetric eigenfunctions, as well as partial sums of eigenvalues for general polynomial eigenfunctions.</p>{% endraw %}
