{% raw %}<p>We study the dilogarithm identities from algebraic, analytic, asymptotic, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-theoretic, combinatorial and representation-theoretic points of view. We prove that a lot of dilogarithm identities (hypothetically all !) can be obtained by using the five-term relation only. Among those the Coxeter, Lewin, Loxton and Browkin ones are contained. Accessibility of Lewin's one variable and Ray's multivariable (here for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\le 2</annotation></semantics></math></span> only) functional equations is given. For odd levels the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><mo>^</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\hat{sl_2}</annotation></semantics></math></span> case of Kuniba-Nakanishi's dilogarithm conjecture is proven and additional results about remainder term are obtained. The connections between dilogarithm identities and Rogers-Ramanujan-Andrews-Gordon type partition identities via their asymptotic behavior are discussed. Some new results about the string functions for level <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> vacuum representation of the affine Lie algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><mo>^</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\hat{sl_n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> are obtained. Connection between dilogarithm identities and algebraic <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-theory (torsion in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="bold">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_3({\bf R})</annotation></semantics></math></span>) is discussed. Relations between crystal basis, branching functions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>b</mi><mi>λ</mi><mrow><mi>k</mi><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b_{\lambda}^{k\Lambda_0}(q)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and Kostka-Foulkes polynomials (Lusztig's <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-analog of weight multiplicity) are considered. The Melzer and Milne conjectures are proven. In some special cases we are proving that the branching functions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>b</mi><mi>λ</mi><mrow><mi>k</mi><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b_{\lambda}^{k\Lambda_0}(q)</annotation></semantics></math></span> are equal to an appropriate limit of Kostka polynomials (the so-called Thermodynamic Bethe Ansatz limit). Connection between "finite-dimensional part of crystal base" and Robinson-Schensted-Knuth correspondence is considered.</p>{% endraw %}
