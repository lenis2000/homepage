{% raw %}<p>Building on and from the work of Brydges and Imbrie, we give an elementary calculation of the volume of the space of branched polymers of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the plane and in 3-space. Our development reveals some more general identities, and allows exact random sampling. In particular we show that a random 3-dimensional branched polymer of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> has diameter of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>n</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
