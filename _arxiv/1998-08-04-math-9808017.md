{% raw %}<p>The number of plane partitions contained in a given box was shown by MacMahon to be given by a simple product formula. By a simple bijection, this formula also enumerates lozenge tilings of hexagons of side-lengths <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>c</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>a</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a,b,c,a,b,c</annotation></semantics></math></span> (in cyclic order) and angles of 120 degrees. We present a generalization in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>b</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b=c</annotation></semantics></math></span> by giving simple product formulas enumerating lozenge tilings of regions obtained from a hexagon of side-lengths <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>k</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>a</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>k</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a,b+k,b,a+k,b,b+k</annotation></semantics></math></span> (where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> is an arbitrary non-negative integer) and angles of 120 degrees by removing certain triangular regions along its symmetry axis.</p>{% endraw %}
