{% raw %}<p>We establish new properties of inhomogeneous spin <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials, which are symmetric polynomials generalizing <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> Macdonald polynomials. We show that these polynomials are defined in terms of a vertex model, whose weights come not from an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix, as is often the case, but from other intertwining operators of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">s</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo>^</mo></mover><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U&#x27;_q(\hat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)</annotation></semantics></math></span>-modules. Using this construction, we are able to prove a Cauchy-type identity for inhomogeneous spin <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials in full generality. Moreover, we are able to characterize spin <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials in terms of vanishing at certain points, and we find interpolation analogues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker and elementary symmetric polynomials.</p>{% endraw %}
