{% raw %}<p>We point out a simple criterion for convergence of polynomials to a concrete entire function in the Laguerre-P\'{o}lya (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">L</mi><mi mathvariant="script">P</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{LP}</annotation></semantics></math></span>) class (of all functions arising as uniform limits of polynomials with only real roots). We then use this to show that any random <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">L</mi><mi mathvariant="script">P</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{LP}</annotation></semantics></math></span> function can be obtained as the uniform limit of rescaled characteristic polynomials of principal submatrices of an infinite unitarily invariant random Hermitian matrix. Conversely, the rescaled characteristic polynomials of principal submatrices of any infinite random unitarily invariant Hermitian matrix converge uniformly to a random <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">L</mi><mi mathvariant="script">P</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{LP}</annotation></semantics></math></span> function. This result also has a natural extension to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles. Distinguished cases include random entire functions associated to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Sine, and more generally <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Hua-Pickrell, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Bessel and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Airy point processes studied in the literature.</p>{% endraw %}
