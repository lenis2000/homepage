{% raw %}<p>We present a numerical study of the evolution of height distributions (HDs) obtained in interface growth models belonging to the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) universality class. The growth is done on an initially flat substrate. The HDs obtained for all investigated models are very well fitted by the theoretically predicted Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) distribution. The first cumulant has a shift that vanishes as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^{-1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, while the cumulants of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>≤</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2\le n\le 4</annotation></semantics></math></span> converge to GOE as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^{-2/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> or faster, behaviors previously observed in other KPZ systems. These results yield a new evidence for the universality of the GOE distribution in KPZ growth on flat substrates.
