{% raw %}<p>Laguerre and Laguerre-type polynomials are orthogonal polynomials on the interval <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[0,\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span> with respect to a weight function of the form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>w</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>x</mi><mi>α</mi></msup><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><msubsup><mo>∑</mo><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><mi>m</mi></msubsup><msub><mi>q</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><msup><mi>x</mi><mi>k</mi></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>q</mi><mi>m</mi></msub><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">w(x) = x^{\alpha} e^{-Q(x)}, Q(x) = \sum_{k=0}^m q_k x^k, \alpha &gt; -1, q_m &gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The classical Laguerre polynomials correspond to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mi>x</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Q(x)=x</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The computation of higher-order terms of the asymptotic expansions of these polynomials for large degree becomes quite complicated, and a full description seems to be lacking in literature. However, this information is implicitly available in the work of Vanlessen, based on a non-linear steepest descent analysis of an associated so-called Riemann--Hilbert problem. We will extend this work and show how to efficiently compute an arbitrary number of higher-order terms in the asymptotic expansions of Laguerre and Laguerre-type polynomials. This effort is similar to the case of Jacobi and Jacobi-type polynomials in a previous paper. We supply an implementation with explicit expansions in four different regions of the complex plane. These expansions can also be extended to Hermite-type weights of the form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><msubsup><mo>∑</mo><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><mi>m</mi></msubsup><msub><mi>q</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><msup><mi>x</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>k</mi></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\exp(-\sum_{k=0}^m q_k x^{2k})</annotation></semantics></math></span> on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(-\infty,\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and to general non-polynomial functions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Q(x)</annotation></semantics></math></span> using contour integrals. The expansions may be used, e.g., to compute Gauss-Laguerre quadrature rules in a lower computational complexity than based on the recurrence relation, and with improved accuracy for large degree. They are also of interest in random matrix theory.</p>{% endraw %}
