{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we derive new combinatorial formulas for symmetric Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P_{\lambda}(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and integral Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>J</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J_{\lambda}(X;q,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, in terms of several new statistics and the major index for a partition <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Compared to previous formulas, these new ones contain the fewest terms. Moreover, three existing formulas for symmetric Macdonald polynomials established by Corteel--Mandelshtam--Williams (2022), Corteel--Haglund--Mandelshtam--Mason--Williams (2022) and Mandelshtam (2025) are recovered. Our proof relies on a new statistic on super fillings, employing the superization formulas of Haglund--Haimain--Loehr (2005) and Ayyer--Mandelshtam--Martin (2023), together with our recent approach to modified Macdonald polynomials.</p>{% endraw %}
