{% raw %}<p>We define a new diffusive matrix model converging towards the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Dyson Brownian motion for all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\in [0,2]</annotation></semantics></math></span> that provides an explicit construction of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles of random matrices that is invariant under the orthogonal/unitary group. We also describe the eigenvector dynamics of the limiting matrix process; we show that when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&lt; 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and that two eigenvalues collide, the eigenvectors of these two colliding eigenvalues fluctuate very fast and take the uniform measure on the orthocomplement of the eigenvectors of the remaining eigenvalues.</p>{% endraw %}
