{% raw %}<p>The Kirillov--Schilling--Shimozono (KSS) bijection appearing in theory of the Fermionic formula gives an one to one correspondence between the set of elements of tensor products of the Kirillov--Reshetikhin crystals (called paths) and the set of rigged configurations. It is a generalization of Kerov--Kirillov--Reshetikhin bijection and plays inverse scattering formalism for the box-ball systems. In this paper, we give an algebraic reformulation of the KSS map from the paths to rigged configurations, using the combinatorial R and energy functions of crystals. It gives a characterization of the KSS bijection as an intrinsic property of tensor products of crystals.</p>{% endraw %}
