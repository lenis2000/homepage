{% raw %}<p>We apply the method of determinants to study the distribution of the largest singular values of large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> m \times n </annotation></semantics></math></span> real rectangular random matrices with independent Cauchy entries. We show that statistical properties of the (rescaled by a factor of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mrow><msup><mi>m</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>⋅</mo><msup><mi>n</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \frac{1}{m^2\*n^2}</annotation></semantics></math></span>)largest singular values agree in the limit with the statistics of the inhomogeneous Poisson random point process with the intensity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mi>π</mi></mfrac><msup><mi>x</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>3</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \frac{1}{\pi} x^{-3/2} </annotation></semantics></math></span> and, therefore, are different from the Tracy-Widom law. Among other corollaries of our method we show an interesting connection between the mathematical expectations of the determinants of complex rectangular <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> m \times n </annotation></semantics></math></span> standard Wishart ensemble and real rectangular <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>m</mi><mo>×</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> 2m \times 2n </annotation></semantics></math></span> standard Wishart ensemble.</p>{% endraw %}
