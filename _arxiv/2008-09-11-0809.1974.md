{% raw %}<p>We study a totally asymmetric simple exclusion process (TASEP) with one defect site, hopping rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q&lt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, near the system boundary. Regarding our system as a pair of uniform TASEP's coupled through the defect, we study various methods to match a \emph{finite} TASEP and an \emph{infinite} one across a common boundary. Several approximation schemes are investigated. Utilizing the finite segment mean-field (FSMF) method, we set up a framework for computing the steady state current <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J</annotation></semantics></math></span> as a function of the entry rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">% \alpha </annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For the case where the defect is located at the entry site, we obtain an analytical expression for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J(\alpha, q) </annotation></semantics></math></span> which is in good agreement with Monte Carlo simulation results. When the defect is located deeper in the bulk, we refined the scheme of MacDonald, et.al. [Biopolymers, \textbf{6}, 1 (1968)] and find reasonably good fits to the density profiles before the defect site. We discuss the strengths and limitations of each method, as well as possible avenues for further studies.</p>{% endraw %}
