{% raw %}<p>Pipedreams and bumpless pipedreams are two combinatorial models that compute double Grothendieck polynomials. While studying matrix Schubert varieties, Pechenik, Speyer, and Weigandt defined a permutation statistic<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">r</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">a</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">j</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">c</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">o</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">d</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">e</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>⋅</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathsf{rajcode}(\cdot)</annotation></semantics></math></span> that captures the leading monomial of the top-degree components of a Grothendieck polynomial. Combinatorially, their result implies that there exists a unique pipedream (or bumpless pipedream) with row weight <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">r</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">a</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">j</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">c</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">o</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">d</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">e</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>w</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathsf{rajcode}(w)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and column weight <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">r</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">a</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">j</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">c</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">o</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">d</mi><mi mathvariant="sans-serif">e</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>w</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathsf{rajcode}(w^{-1})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. A construction of such a pipedream was subsequently given by Chou and Yu. In this paper, we resolve the bumpless pipedream version of this problem by providing an explicit algorithm.</p>{% endraw %}
