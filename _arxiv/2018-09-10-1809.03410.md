{% raw %}<p>Consider the Hopf--Cole solution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> h(t,x) </annotation></semantics></math></span> of the KPZ equation with narrow wedge initial condition. Regarding <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> t\to\infty </annotation></semantics></math></span> as a scaling parameter, we provide the first rigorous proof of the Large Deviation Principle (LDP) for the lower tail of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>t</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mfrac><mi>t</mi><mn>12</mn></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> h(2t,0)+\frac{t}{12} </annotation></semantics></math></span>, with speed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> t^2 </annotation></semantics></math></span> and an explicit rate function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Φ</mi><mo>−</mo></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \Phi_-(z) </annotation></semantics></math></span>. This result confirms existing physic predictions [Sasorov, Meerson, Prolhac 17], [Corwin, Ghosal, Krajenbrink, Le Doussal, Tsai 18], and [Krajenbrink, Le Doussal, Prolhac 18]. Our analysis utilizes the formula from [Borodin, Gorin 16] to convert LDP of the KPZ equation to calculating an exponential moment of the Airy point process. To estimate this exponential moment, we invoke the stochastic Airy operator, and use the Riccati transform, comparison techniques, and certain variational characterizations of the relevant functional.</p>{% endraw %}
