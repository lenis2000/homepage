{% raw %}<p>We propose a novel quantum integrable model for every non-simply laced simple Lie algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathfrak g}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which we call the folded integrable model. Its spectra correspond to solutions of the Bethe Ansatz equations obtained by folding the Bethe Ansatz equations of the standard integrable model associated to the quantum affine algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><msup><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msup><mo>^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\hat{\mathfrak g&#x27;})</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the simply-laced Lie algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathfrak g}&#x27;</annotation></semantics></math></span> corresponding to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathfrak g}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our construction is motivated by the analysis of the second classical limit of the deformed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathcal W}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-algebra of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathfrak g}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which we interpret as a "folding" of the Grothendieck ring of finite-dimensional representations of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><msup><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo mathvariant="normal" lspace="0em" rspace="0em">′</mo></msup><mo>^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\hat{\mathfrak g&#x27;})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We conjecture, and verify in a number of cases, that the spaces of states of the folded integrable model can be identified with finite-dimensional representations of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mrow></mrow><mi>L</mi></msup><mover accent="true"><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo>^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q({}^L\hat{\mathfrak g})</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mrow></mrow><mi>L</mi></msup><mover accent="true"><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo>^</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">^L\hat{\mathfrak g}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the (twisted) affine Kac-Moody algebra Langlands dual to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo>^</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\hat{\mathfrak g}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We discuss the analogous structures in the Gaudin model which appears in the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q \to 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Finally, we describe a conjectural construction of the simple <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathfrak g}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-crystals in terms of the folded <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-characters.</p>{% endraw %}
