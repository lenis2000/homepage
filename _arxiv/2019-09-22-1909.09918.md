{% raw %}<p>We consider properties of the ground state density for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional Fermi gas in an harmonic trap. Previous work has shown that the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional Fourier transform has a very simple functional form. It is shown that this fact can be used to deduce that the density itself satisfies a third order linear differential equation, previously known in the literature but from other considerations. It is shown too how this implies a closed form expression for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th non-negative integer moments of the density, and a second order recurrence. Both can be extended to general Re<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext> </mtext><mi>k</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>d</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\, k &gt; -d/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The moments, and the smoothed density, permit expansions in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mover accent="true"><mi>M</mi><mo>~</mo></mover><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/\tilde{M}^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mi>M</mi><mo>~</mo></mover><mo>=</mo><mi>M</mi><mo>+</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>d</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tilde{M} = M + (d+1)/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M</annotation></semantics></math></span> denoting the shell label. The moment expansion substituted in the second order recurrence gives a generalisation of the Harer--Zagier recurrence, satisfied by the coefficients of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> expansion of the moments of the spectral density for the Gaussian unitary ensemble in random matrix theory.</p>{% endraw %}
