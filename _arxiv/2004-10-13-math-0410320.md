This property is no longer valid for other values of the parameters; in general, zeros are complex. In this paper we study the strong asymptotics of Jacobi polynomials where the real parameters <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>α</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha_n,\beta_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> depend on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> in such a way that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block"><semantics><mrow><munder><mrow><mi>lim</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></munder><mfrac><msub><mi>α</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mi>n</mi></mfrac><mo>=</mo><mi>A</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mspace width="1em"/><munder><mrow><mi>lim</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></munder><mfrac><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mi>n</mi></mfrac><mo>=</mo><mi>B</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\alpha_{n}}{n}=A, \quad \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{\beta_{n}}{n}=B, </annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A,B \in \mathbb{R}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We restrict our attention to the case where the limits <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A,B</annotation></semantics></math></span> are not both positive and take values outside of the triangle bounded by the straight lines A=0, B=0 and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>B</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A+B+2=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As a corollary, we show that in the limit the zeros distribute along certain curves that constitute trajectories of a quadratic differential. The non-hermitian orthogonality relations for Jacobi polynomials with varying parameters lie in the core of our approach; in the cases we consider, these relations hold on a single contour of the complex plane. The asymptotic analysis is performed using the Deift-Zhou steepest descent method based on the Riemann-Hilbert reformulation of Jacobi polynomials.</p>{% endraw %}
