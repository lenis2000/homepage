{% raw %}<p>In the theory of symmetric Jack polynomials the coefficients in the expansion of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>th elementary symmetric function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>e</mi><mi>p</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">e_p(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span> times a Jack polynomial expressed as a series in Jack polynomials are known explicitly. Here analogues of this result for the non-symmetric Jack polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>η</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_\eta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span> are explored. Necessary conditions for non-zero coefficients in the expansion of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>e</mi><mi>p</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>η</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">e_p(z) E_\eta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span> as a series in non-symmetric Jack polynomials are given. A known expansion formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>z</mi><mi>i</mi></msub><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>η</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">z_i E_\eta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is rederived by an induction procedure, and this expansion is used to deduce the corresponding result for the expansion of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mo>∏</mo><mrow><mi>j</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>j</mi><mo mathvariant="normal">≠</mo><mi>i</mi></mrow><mi>N</mi></msubsup><msub><mi>z</mi><mi>j</mi></msub><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>η</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\prod_{j=1, j\ne i}^N z_j E_\eta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and consequently the expansion of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>e</mi><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>η</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">e_{N-1}(z) E_\eta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span> case the coefficients for special terms in the expansion are presented.</p>{% endraw %}
