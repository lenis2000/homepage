{% raw %}<p>The misanthrope process is an interacting particle system where particles move between neighbouring sites with hop rates depending only on the number of particles at the departure and arrival sites. Motivated by a discretised version of the Hammersley--Aldous--Diaconis process, we introduce a partially asymmetric long range misanthrope process (PALRMP) on a finite one-dimensional lattice with periodic boundary conditions where particles can move between sites that are not necessarily neighbours, as long as there are no particles in between the departure and arrival sites. In this model, each site <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> has an inhomogeneous rate parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x_\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> associated to it, and the hop rate of a particle moving from site <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> to site <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> depends upon the parameter associated to the target site <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x_\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the direction the particle moves, and the number of particles at sites <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We also consider the homogeneous PALRMP, where all the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x_\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span>'s are 1. We find necessary and sufficient conditions on the hop rates under which the stationary distribution is of factorised form for both the PALRMP and the homogeneous PALRMP, as well as the extreme variants, namely the ones where the particle motion is totally asymmetric (TALRMP) and symmetric (SLRMP). As an illustrative example, we study in detail the discrete Hammersley--Aldous--Diaconis process.</p>{% endraw %}
