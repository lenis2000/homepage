{% raw %}<p>We study the decomposition of tensor powers of two dimensional irreducible representations of quantum <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">s</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{sl}_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> at even roots of unity into direct sums of tilting modules. We derive a combinatorial formula for multiplicity of tilting modules in the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th tensor power of two dimensional irreducible representations, interpret it in terms of lattice paths and find its asymptotic behavior when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We also describe the limit of character and Plancherel measures when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We consider both <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">s</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)</annotation></semantics></math></span> with divided powers and the small quantum <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">sl_2</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
