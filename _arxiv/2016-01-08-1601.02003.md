{% raw %}<p>We study the lengths of monotone subsequences for permutations drawn from the Mallows measure. The Mallows measure was introduced by Mallows in connection with ranking problems in statistics. Under this measure, the probability of a permutation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>π</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pi</annotation></semantics></math></span> is proportional to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>q</mi><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">i</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">n</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">v</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>π</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q^{{\rm inv}(\pi)}</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a positive parameter and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">i</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">n</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">v</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>π</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\rm inv}(\pi)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the number of inversions in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>π</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pi</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In our main result we show that when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>q</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0&lt;q&lt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the limiting distribution of the longest increasing subsequence (LIS) is Gaussian, answering an open question in [Bhatnagar and Peled, PTRF, 2015]. This is in contrast to the case when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=1</annotation></semantics></math></span> where the limiting distribution of the LIS when scaled appropriately is the GUE Tracy-Widom distribution. We also obtain a law of large numbers for the length of the longest decreasing subsequence (LDS) and identify the precise constant in the order of the expectation, answering a further open question in [Bhatnagar and Peled, PTRF, 2015].</p>{% endraw %}
