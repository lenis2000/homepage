{% raw %}<p>Zamolodchikov found an integrable field theory related to the Lie algebra E<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>8</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_8</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which describes the scaling limit of the Ising model in a magnetic field. He conjectured that there also exist solvable lattice models based on E<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>8</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_8</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the universality class of the Ising model in a field. The dilute A<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>3</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_3</annotation></semantics></math></span> model is a solvable lattice model with a critical point in the Ising universality class. The parameter by which the model can be taken away from the critical point acts like a magnetic field by breaking the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Integer_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> symmetry between the states. The expected direct relation of the model with E<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>8</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_8</annotation></semantics></math></span> has not been found hitherto. In this letter we study the thermodynamics of the dilute A<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>3</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_3</annotation></semantics></math></span> model and show that in the scaling limit it exhibits an appropriate E<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>8</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_8</annotation></semantics></math></span> structure, which naturally leads to the E<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>8</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_8</annotation></semantics></math></span> scattering theory for massive excitations over the ground state.</p>{% endraw %}
