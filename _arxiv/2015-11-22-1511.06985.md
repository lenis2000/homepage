{% raw %}<p>The notion of a homogeneous standard filtration of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span>-algebras was introduced by the author in 1970. The main theorem asserted that a homogeneous filtration is standard, i.e., generated by a sequence of independent random variables, if and only if the standardness criterion is satisfied. In this paper we give detailed definitions and characterizations of Markov standard filtrations. The notion of standardness is essential for applications of probabilistic, combinatorial, and algebraic nature. At the end of the paper we present new notions of {\it shadow metric-measure space} related to nonstandard filtrations.</p>{% endraw %}
