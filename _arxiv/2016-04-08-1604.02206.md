Earlier Brubaker, Bump, Friedberg, Chinta and Gunnells had shown that these Whittaker functions can be identified with the partition functions of statistical mechanical systems. They postulated that a Yang-Baxter equation underlies the properties of these Whittaker functions. We confirm this, and identify the corresponding Yang-Baxter equation with that of the quantum affine Lie superalgebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><msqrt><mi>v</mi></msqrt></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_{\sqrt{v}}(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}(1|n))</annotation></semantics></math></span>, modified by Drinfeld twisting to introduce Gauss sums. (The deformation parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>v</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">v</annotation></semantics></math></span> is specialized to the inverse of the residue field cardinality.) For principal series representations of metaplectic groups, the Whittaker models are not unique. The scattering matrix for the standard intertwining operators is vector valued. For a simple reflection, it was computed by Kazhdan and Patterson, who applied it to generalized theta series. We will show that the scattering matrix on the space of Whittaker functions for a simple reflection coincides with the twisted <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix of the quantum group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><msqrt><mi>v</mi></msqrt></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_{\sqrt{v}}(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}(n))</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This is a piece of the twisted <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><msqrt><mi>v</mi></msqrt></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_{\sqrt{v}}(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}(1|n))</annotation></semantics></math></span>, mentioned above.</p>{% endraw %}
