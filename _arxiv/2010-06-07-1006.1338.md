{% raw %}<p>We consider the weakly asymmetric limit of simple exclusion process with drift to the left, starting from step Bernoulli initial data with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mo>−</mo></msub><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mo>+</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho_-&lt;\rho_+</annotation></semantics></math></span> so that macroscopically one has a rarefaction fan. We study the fluctuations of the process observed along slopes in the fan, which are given by the Hopf--Cole solution of the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang (KPZ) equation, with appropriate initial data. For slopes strictly inside the fan, the initial data is a Dirac delta function and the one point distribution functions have been computed in [Comm. Pure Appl. Math. 64 (2011) 466-537] and [Nuclear Phys. B 834 (2010) 523-542]. At the edge of the rarefaction fan, the initial data is one-sided Brownian. We obtain a new family of crossover distributions giving the exact one-point distributions of this process, which converge, as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>T</mi><mo>↗</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T\nearrow\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> to those of the Airy <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="script">A</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>→</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">B</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">M</mi></mrow></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{A}_{2\to \mathrm{BM}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> process. As an application, we prove moment and large deviation estimates for the equilibrium Hopf-Cole solution of KPZ. These bounds rely on the apparently new observation that the FKG inequality holds for the stochastic heat equation. Finally, via a Feynman-Kac path integral, the KPZ equation also governs the free energy of the continuum directed polymer, and thus our formula may also be interpreted in those terms.</p>{% endraw %}
