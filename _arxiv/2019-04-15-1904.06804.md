{% raw %}<p>Starting from an integrable rank-<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> vertex model, we construct an explicit family of partition functions indexed by compositions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>μ</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>μ</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu = (\mu_1,\dots,\mu_n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Using the Yang-Baxter algebra of the model and a certain rotation operation that acts on our partition functions, we show that they are eigenfunctions of the Cherednik-Dunkl operators <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>Y</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> for all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>≤</mo><mi>i</mi><mo>≤</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1 \leq i \leq n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and are thus equal to nonsymmetric Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>μ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_{\mu}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our partition functions have the combinatorial interpretation of ensembles of coloured lattice paths which traverse a cylinder. Applying a simple bijection to such path ensembles, we show how to recover the well-known combinatorial formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>μ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_{\mu}</annotation></semantics></math></span> due to Haglund-Haiman-Loehr.</p>{% endraw %}
