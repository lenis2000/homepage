{% raw %}<p>In 1995 Magnus posed a conjecture about the asymptotics of the recurrence coefficients of orthogonal polynomials with respect to the weights on [-1,1] of the form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>x</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>α</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>x</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>β</mi></msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><msub><mi>x</mi><mn>0</mn></msub><mo>−</mo><mi>x</mi><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>γ</mi></msup><mo>×</mo><mi>a</mi><mi>j</mi><mi>u</mi><mi>m</mi><mi>p</mi><mi>a</mi><mi>t</mi><msub><mi>x</mi><mn>0</mn></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> (1-x)^\alpha (1+x)^\beta |x_0 - x|^\gamma \times a jump at x_0, </annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>β</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>γ</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha, \beta, \gamma&gt;-1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>x</mi><mn>0</mn></msub><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x_0 \in (-1,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show rigorously that Magnus' conjecture is correct even in a more general situation, when the weight above has an extra factor, which is analytic in a neighborhood of [-1,1] and positive on the interval. The proof is based on the steepest descendent method of Deift and Zhou applied to the non-commutative Riemann-Hilbert problem characterizing the orthogonal polynomials. A feature of this situation is that the local analysis at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>x</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> has to be carried out in terms of confluent hypergeometric functions.</p>{% endraw %}
