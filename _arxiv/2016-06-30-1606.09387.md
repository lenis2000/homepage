{% raw %}<p>We consider a two dimensional random band matrix ensemble, in the limit of infinite volume and fixed but large band width <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For this model we rigorously prove smoothness of the averaged density of states. We also prove that the resulting expression coincides with Wigner's semicircle law with a precision <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>W</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>δ</mi></mrow></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W^{-2+\delta },</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta\to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W\to \infty.</annotation></semantics></math></span> The proof uses the supersymmetric approach and extends results by Disertori, Pinson and Spencer from three to two dimensions.</p>{% endraw %}
