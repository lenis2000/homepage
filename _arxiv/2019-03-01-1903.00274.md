{% raw %}<p>In this paper we consider solutions to the reflection equation related to the higher spin stochastic six vertex model. The corresponding higher spin <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix is associated with the affine quantum algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>l</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\widehat{sl(2)})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The explicit formulas for boundary <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrices for spins <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s=1/2,1</annotation></semantics></math></span> are well known. We derive difference equations for the generating function of matrix elements of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix for any spin <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> and solve them in terms of hypergeometric functions. As a result we derive the explicit formula for matrix elements of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix for arbitrary spin. In the lower- and upper- triangular cases, the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix simplifies and reduces to simple products of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Pochhammer symbols.</p>{% endraw %}
