{% raw %}<p>It is known that the bulk scaling limit of the real eigenvalues for the real Ginibre ensemble is equal in distribution to the rescaled <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit of the annihilation process <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>A</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∅</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A + A \to \emptyset</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Furthermore, deleting each particle at random in the rescaled <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit of the coalescence process <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>A</mi><mo>→</mo><mi>A</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A + A \to A</annotation></semantics></math></span>, a process equal in distribution to the annihilation process results. We use these inter-relationships to deduce from the existing literature the asymptotic small and large distance form of the gap probability for the real Ginibre ensemble. In particular, the leading form of the latter is shown to be equal to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>3</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><msqrt><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>π</mi></mrow></msqrt><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>s</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\exp(-(\zeta(3/2)/(2 \sqrt{2 \pi}))s)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> denotes the gap size and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span> denotes the Riemann zeta function. It is shown how this can be rigorously established using an asymptotic formula for matrix Fredholm operators. A determinant formula is derived for the gap probability in the finite <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> case, and this is used to illustrate the asymptotic formulas against numerical computations.</p>{% endraw %}
