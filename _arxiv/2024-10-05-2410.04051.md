{% raw %}<p>We study the process of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>K</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2K-B</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a standard one-dimensional Brownian motion and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span> is its concave majorant. In light of Pitman's <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>M</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2M-B</annotation></semantics></math></span> theorem, it was recently conjectured by Ouaki and Pitman \cite{OP} that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>K</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2K-B</annotation></semantics></math></span> has the law of the BES(5) process. The two processes share properties such as Brownian scaling, time inversion and quadratic variation, and the same one point distribution and infinitesimal generator, among many other evidences; and it remains to prove that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>K</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2K-B</annotation></semantics></math></span> is Markovian. However, we show that this conjecture is false. To better understand the similarity between these two processes, we study a degeneration of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>K</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2K-B</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show it is a mixture of BES(3), and get other properties including multiple points distribution, infinitesimal generator, and path decomposition at future infimum. We also further investigate the Markovian structure and the filtrations of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>K</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>B</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2K-B, B</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
