{% raw %}<p>We consider a two-dimensional stochastic heat equation with noise correlated at scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi><mo>≪</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho \ll 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and of strength <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>ρ</mi><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup><mi>σ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>v</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">|\log\rho|^{-1/2}\sigma(v)</annotation></semantics></math></span> depending nonlinearly on the solution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>v</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">v</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Under certain conditions, the first author and Gu have shown that the one-point statistics of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>v</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">v</annotation></semantics></math></span> converge in law as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho\to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> to the terminal value of an associated forward-backward SDE. Here, we show that the 2D stochastic heat equation is stable under renormalization with a new effective nonlinearity tied to the decoupling function of the forward-backward SDE. This allows us to extend the pointwise results to a much broader class of nonlinearities. We also show that these limiting pointwise statistics are insensitive to the fine details of the noise, and thus universal.</p>{% endraw %}
