In this paper we provide sufficient conditions for convergence of a sequence of suitably scaled discrete Gibbsian line ensembles <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>f</mi><mi>N</mi></msup><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msubsup><mi>f</mi><mn>1</mn><mi>N</mi></msubsup><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><msubsup><mi>f</mi><mi>N</mi><mi>N</mi></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f^N = (f_1^N,\dots,f_N^N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> as the number of curves <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> tends to infinity. These results apply to a broad class of discrete jump distributions, including geometric as well as any log-concave distribution whose support forms a compact integer interval.</p>{% endraw %}
