{% raw %}<p>In this note, we study the model of directed last passage percolation on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Z}^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with i.i.d. exponential weight. We consider the maximum paths from vertices <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo fence="true">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo stretchy="false">⌊</mo><msup><mi>k</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">⌋</mo><mo fence="true">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\left(0,\lfloor k^{2/3} \rfloor\right)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo stretchy="false">⌊</mo><msup><mi>k</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">⌋</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\lfloor k^{2/3} \rfloor,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span> to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(n,n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, respectively. For the coalescing point of these paths, we show that the probability for it being <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>&gt;</mo><mi>k</mi><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">&gt;kR</annotation></semantics></math></span> far away from the origin is in the order of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>R</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R^{-2/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This is motivated by a recent work of Basu, Sarkar, and Sly, where the same estimate was obtained for semi-infinite geodesics, and the optimal exponent for the finite case was left open. Our arguments also apply to other exactly solvable models of last passage percolation.</p>{% endraw %}
