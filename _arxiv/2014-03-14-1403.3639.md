{% raw %}<p>We study asymptotic behavior for determinants of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> Toeplitz matrices corresponding to symbols with two Fisher-Hartwig singularities at the distance <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>t</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2t\ge0</annotation></semantics></math></span> from each other on the unit circle. We obtain large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> asymptotics which are uniform for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>t</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0&lt;t&lt;t_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> is fixed. They describe the transition as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t\to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> between the asymptotic regimes of 2 singularities and 1 singularity. The asymptotics involve a particular solution to the Painlev\'e V equation. We obtain small and large argument expansions of this solution. As applications of our results we prove a conjecture of Dyson on the largest occupation number in the ground state of a one-dimensional Bose gas, and a conjecture of Fyodorov and Keating on the second moment of powers of the characteristic polynomials of random matrices.</p>{% endraw %}
