{% raw %}<p>We consider <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\times N</annotation></semantics></math></span> Hermitian random matrices with i.i.d. entries. The matrix is normalized so that the average spacing between consecutive eigenvalues is of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We study the connection between eigenvalue statistics on microscopic energy scales <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi><mo>≪</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta\ll1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and (de)localization properties of the eigenvectors. Under suitable assumptions on the distribution of the single matrix elements, we first give an upper bound on the density of states on short energy scales of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi><mo>∼</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>N</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta \sim\log N/N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We then prove that the density of states concentrates around the Wigner semicircle law on energy scales <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi><mo>≫</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta\gg N^{-2/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show that most eigenvectors are fully delocalized in the sense that their <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi><mi>p</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell^p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-norms are comparable with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>p</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{{1}/{p}-{1}/{2}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p\ge2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and we obtain the weaker bound <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>p</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{{2}/{3}({1}/{p}-{1}/{2})}</annotation></semantics></math></span> for all eigenvectors whose eigenvalues are separated away from the spectral edges. We also prove that, with a probability very close to one, no eigenvector can be localized. Finally, we give an optimal bound on the second moment of the Green function.</p>{% endraw %}
