{% raw %}<p>The ensemble average of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><msubsup><mo>∑</mo><mrow><mi>j</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><mi>N</mi></msubsup><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mi>i</mi><mi>k</mi><msub><mi>λ</mi><mi>j</mi></msub></mrow></msup><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">| \sum_{j=1}^N e^{i k \lambda_j} |^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> is of interest as a probe of quantum chaos, as is its connected part, the structure function. Plotting this average for model systems of chaotic spectra reveals what has been termed a dip-ramp-plateau shape. Generalising earlier work of Br\'ezin and Hikami for the Gaussian unitary ensemble, it is shown how the average in the case of the Laguerre unitary ensemble can be reduced to an expression involving the spectral density of the Jacobi unitary ensemble. This facilitates studying the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit, and so quantifying the dip-ramp-plateau effect. When the parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the Laguerre weight <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>x</mi><mi>a</mi></msup><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>x</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x^a e^{-x}</annotation></semantics></math></span> scales with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, quantitative agreement is found with the characteristic features of this effect known for the Gaussian unitary ensemble. However, for the parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a</annotation></semantics></math></span> fixed, the bulk scaled structure function is shown to have the simple functional form <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>2</mn><mi>π</mi></mfrac><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">A</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">r</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">c</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">t</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">a</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">n</mi></mrow><mtext> </mtext><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{2 \over \pi} {\rm Arctan} \, k</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and so there is no ramp-plateau transition.</p>{% endraw %}
