{% raw %}<p>We consider the fluctuations of the largest eigenvalue of sparse random matrices, the class of random matrices that includes the normalized adjacency matrices of the Erd\H{o}s-R\'enyi graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>p</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G(N, p)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show that the fluctuations of the largest eigenvalue converge to the Tracy-Widom law at a rate almost <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo>+</mo><msup><mi>p</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>4</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">O(N^{-1/3 } + p^{-2} N^{-4/3})</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the regime <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>≫</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p \gg N^{-2/3 }</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our proof builds upon the Green function comparison method initiated by Erd\H{o}s, Yau, and Yin [22]. To show a Green function comparison theorem for fine spectral scales, we implement algorithms for symbolic computations involving averaged products of Green function entries.</p>{% endraw %}
