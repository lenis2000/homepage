{% raw %}<p>Dyson's (1962) classification of matrix ensembles is reviewed from a modern perspective, and its recent extension to disordered fermion problems is motivated and described. It is explained in particular why symmetry classes are associated with large families of symmetric spaces.</p>{% endraw %}
