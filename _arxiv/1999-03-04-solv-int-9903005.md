{% raw %}<p>Consider a semi-infinite skew-symmetric moment matrix, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m_{\iy}</annotation></semantics></math></span> evolving according to the vector fields <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><msub><mi>t</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi><mi>k</mi></msup><mi>m</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>m</mi><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">⊤</mi><mi>k</mi></mrow></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pl m / \pl t_k=\Lb^k m+m \Lb^{\top k} ,</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Lb</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the shift matrix. Then the skew-Borel decomposition <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></msub><mo>:</mo><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>Q</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mi>J</mi><msup><mi>Q</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">⊤</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> m_{\iy}:= Q^{-1} J Q^{\top -1} </annotation></semantics></math></span> leads to the so-called Pfaff Lattice, which is integrable, by virtue of the AKS theorem, for a splitting involving the affine symplectic algebra. The tau-functions for the system are shown to be pfaffians and the wave vectors skew-orthogonal polynomials; we give their explicit form in terms of moments. This system plays an important role in symmetric and symplectic matrix models and in the theory of random matrices (beta=1 or 4).</p>{% endraw %}
