{% raw %}<p>In this paper we construct a new factorized representation of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix related to the affine algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_{q}(\widehat{sl_{n}})</annotation></semantics></math></span> for symmetric tensor representations with arbitrary weights. Using the 3D approach we obtain explicit formulas for the matrix elements of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix and give a simple proof that a "twisted" <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix is stochastic. We also discuss symmetries of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix, its degenerations and compare our formulas with other results available in the literature.</p>{% endraw %}
