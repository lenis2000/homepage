{% raw %}<p>We study first passage percolation (FPP) on a Gromov-hyperbolic group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> with boundary <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\partial G</annotation></semantics></math></span> equipped with the Patterson-Sullivan measure <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ν</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\nu</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We associate an i.i.d.\ collection of random passage times to each edge of a Cayley graph of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and investigate classical questions about the asymptotics of first passage time as well as the geometry of geodesics in the FPP metric. Under suitable conditions on the passage time distribution, we show that the `velocity' exists in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ν</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\nu</annotation></semantics></math></span>-almost every direction <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ξ</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi\in \partial G</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and is almost surely constant by ergodicity of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><mo>−</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G-</annotation></semantics></math></span>action on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\partial G</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For every <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ξ</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi\in \partial G</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we also show almost sure coalescence of any two geodesic rays directed towards <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ξ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Finally, we show that the variance of the first passage time grows linearly with word distance along word geodesic rays in every fixed boundary direction. This provides an affirmative answer to a conjecture of Benjamini, Tessera, and Zeitouni.</p>{% endraw %}
