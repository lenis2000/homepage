{% raw %}<p>We prove that for all values of the edge probability p(n) the largest eigenvalue of a random graph G(n,p) satisfies almost surely: \lambda_1(G)=(1+o(1))max{\sqrt{\Delta},np}, where \Delta is a maximal degree of G, and the o(1) term tends to zero as max{\sqrt{\Delta},np} tends to infinity.</p>{% endraw %}
