{% raw %}<p>We consider an interacting particle system on the one dimensional lattice <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="bold">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\bf Z</annotation></semantics></math></span> modeling combustion. The process depends on two integer parameters <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>≤</mo><mi>a</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>M</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2\le a&lt;M&lt;\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Particles move independently as continuous time simple symmetric random walks except that 1. When a particle jumps to a site which has not been previously visited by any particle, it branches into <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a</annotation></semantics></math></span> particles; 2. When a particle jumps to a site with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M</annotation></semantics></math></span> particles, it is annihilated. We start from a configuration where all sites to the left of the origin have been previously visited and study the law of large numbers and central limit theorem for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>r</mi><mi>t</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r_t</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the rightmost visited site at time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The proofs are based on the construction of a renewal structure leading to a definition of regeneration times for which good tail estimates can be performed.</p>{% endraw %}
