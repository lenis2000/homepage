{% raw %}<p>A special asymptotic solution of the Painlev\'e-2 equation with small parameter is studied. This solution has a critical point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_*</annotation></semantics></math></span> corresponding to a bifurcation phenomenon. When <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t&lt;t_*</annotation></semantics></math></span> the constructed solution varies slowly and when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><msub><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t&gt;t_*</annotation></semantics></math></span> the solution oscillates very fast. We investigate the transitional layer in detail and obtain a smooth asymptotic solution, using a sequence of scaling and matching procedures.</p>{% endraw %}
