{% raw %}<p>We consider the ensemble of Real Ginibre matrices with a positive fraction <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> of real eigenvalues. We demonstrate a large deviations principle for the joint eigenvalue density of such matrices and we introduce a two phase log-gas whose stationary distribution coincides with the spectral measure of the ensemble. Using these tools we provide an asymptotic expansion for the probability <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>p</mi><mrow><mi>α</mi><mi>n</mi></mrow><mi>n</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p^n_{\alpha n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> that an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> Ginibre matrix has <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>α</mi><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k=\alpha n</annotation></semantics></math></span> real eigenvalues and we characterize the spectral measures of these matrices.</p>{% endraw %}
