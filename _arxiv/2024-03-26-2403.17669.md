We focus on the case where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ξ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi</annotation></semantics></math></span> is given by a rescaled symmetric simple exclusion process which converges to an Ornstein--Uhlenbeck process. By scaling the Laplacian diffusively and considering the equation on a torus, we demonstrate that in dimension <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d=2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, when a suitably renormalized version of the above equation is considered, the sequence of solutions converges in law. The novel contribution in the present work is the establishment of a gradient bound on the transition probability of a fixed but arbitrary number of labelled exclusion particles.</p>{% endraw %}
