{% raw %}<p>We investigate propagation of perturbations of equilibrium states for a wide class of 1D interacting particle systems. The class of systems considered incorporates zero range, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span>-exclusion, mysanthropic, `bricklayers' models, and much more. We do not assume attractivity of the interactions. We apply Yau's relative entropy method rather than coupling arguments. The result is \emph{partial extension} of T. Sepp\"al\"ainen's recent paper. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>β</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>5</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0&lt;\beta&lt;1/5</annotation></semantics></math></span> fixed, we prove that, rescaling microscopic space and time by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, respectively <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>β</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{1+\beta}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the macroscopic evolution of perturbations of microscopic order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>β</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{-\beta}</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the equilibrium states is governed by Burgers' equation. The same statement should hold for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>β</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0&lt;\beta&lt;1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span> as in Sepp\"al\"ainen's cited paper, but our method does not seem to work for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>5</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\ge1/5</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
