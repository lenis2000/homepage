{% raw %}<p>As a simple model for single-file diffusion of hard core particles we investigate the one-dimensional symmetric exclusion process. We consider an open semi-infinite system where one end is coupled to an external reservoir of constant density <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ρ</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho^\ast</annotation></semantics></math></span> and which initially is in an non-equilibrium state with bulk density <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ρ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho_0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We calculate the exact time-dependent two-point density correlation function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>C</mi><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>l</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≡</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>n</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msub><mi>n</mi><mi>l</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>n</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>n</mi><mi>l</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&gt;</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C_{k,l}(t)\equiv &lt;n_k(t)n_l(t)&gt;- &lt;n_k(t)&gt;&lt;n_l(t)&gt;</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the mean and variance of the integrated average net flux of particles <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N(t)-N(0)</annotation></semantics></math></span> that have entered (or left) the system up to time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We find that the boundary region of the semi-infinite relaxing system is in a state similar to the bulk state of a finite stationary system driven by a boundary gradient. The symmetric exclusion model provides a rare example where such behavior can be proved rigorously on the level of equal-time two-point correlation functions. Some implications for the relaxational dynamics of entangled polymers and for single-file diffusion in colloidal systems are discussed.</p>{% endraw %}
