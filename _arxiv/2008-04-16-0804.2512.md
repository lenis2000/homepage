{% raw %}<p>We consider the sequence of the hyperspheres <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>M</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>r</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_{n,r}</annotation></semantics></math></span> i.e. the homogeneous transitive spaces - of the Cartan subgroup <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mi>D</mi><mi>i</mi><mi>a</mi><mi>g</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">SDiag(n,\Bbb R)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi><mi>L</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1...</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">SL(n,\Bbb R), n=1 ...</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and studied the normalized limit of the corresponding sequence of the invariant measures <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>m</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> on those spaces. In the case of compact groups and homogeneous spaces, as example - for classical pairs <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>S</mi><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><msup><mi>S</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1...</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(SO(n), S^{n-1}), n=1 ...</annotation></semantics></math></span> - the limit of corresponding measures is the classical infinite dimensional gaussian measure - this is well-known Maxwell-Poincare lemma. Simultaneously that Gaussian measure is a unique (up to scalar) invariant measure with respect to the action of infinite orthogonal group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">O(\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This coincidences means the asymptotic equivalence between grand and small canonical ensembles for the series of the pairs <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>S</mi><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><msup><mi>S</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(SO(n), S^{n-1})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our main result shows that situation for noncompact groups, for example for the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>S</mi><mi>D</mi><mi>i</mi><mi>a</mi><mi>g</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>M</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>r</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(SDiag(n,\Bbb R),M_{n,r})</annotation></semantics></math></span> (the definitions see below), is completely different: the limit of measures <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>m</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m_n </annotation></semantics></math></span> does not exist in literal sense, and we show that only normalized logarithmic limit of the Laplace transform of those measures does exist. In the same time there exists the measure which is invariant measure with respect to continuous analogue of Cartan subgroup of the group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><mi>L</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">GL(\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span> - this is so called infinite dimensional Lebesgue (see \cite{V}). This difference is an evidence of the non-equivalence between the grand and small canonical ensembles for the noncompact case.</p>{% endraw %}
