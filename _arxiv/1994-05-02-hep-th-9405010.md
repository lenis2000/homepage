{% raw %}<p>In the bulk scaling limit of the Gaussian Unitary Ensemble of Hermitian matrices the probability that an interval of length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> contains no eigenvalues is the Fredholm determinant of the sine kernel <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mrow><mi>sin</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>π</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sin(x-y)\over\pi(x-y)</annotation></semantics></math></span> over this interval. A formal asymptotic expansion for the determinant as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> tends to infinity was obtained by Dyson. In this paper we replace a single interval of length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>J</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">sJ</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a union of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> intervals and present a proof of the asymptotics up to second order. The logarithmic derivative with respect to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the determinant equals a constant (expressible in terms of hyperelliptic integrals) times <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span>, plus a bounded oscillatory function of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">s</annotation></semantics></math></span> (zero of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, periodic if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m=2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and in general expressible in terms of the solution of a Jacobi inversion problem), plus <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>o</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">o(1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Also determined are the asymptotics of the trace of the resolvent operator, which is the ratio in the same model of the probability that the set contains exactly one eigenvalue to the probability that it contains none. The proofs use ideas from orthogonal polynomial theory.</p>{% endraw %}
