{% raw %}<p>We consider a general class of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\times N</annotation></semantics></math></span> random matrices whose entries <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>h</mi><mrow><mi>i</mi><mi>j</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h_{ij}</annotation></semantics></math></span> are independent up to a symmetry constraint, but not necessarily identically distributed. Our main result is a local semicircle law which improves previous results [14] both in the bulk and at the edge. The error bounds are given in terms of the basic small parameter of the model, $\max_{i,j} \E \abs{h_{ij}}^2$. As a consequence, we prove the universality of the local <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-point correlation functions in the bulk spectrum for a class of matrices whose entries do not have comparable variances, including random band matrices with band width <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi><mo>≫</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>ε</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W\gg N^{1-\e_n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> with some <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ε</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\e_n&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and with a negligible mean-field component. In addition, we provide a coherent and pedagogical proof of the local semicircle law, streamlining and strengthening previous arguments from [3,4,16].</p>{% endraw %}
