{% raw %}<p>The famous \emph{circular law} asserts that if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>M</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n \times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> matrix with iid complex entries of mean zero and unit variance, then the empirical spectral distribution (ESD) of the normalized matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><msqrt><mi>n</mi></msqrt></mfrac><msub><mi>M</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} M_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges almost surely to the uniform distribution on the unit disk <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><mi>z</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">C</mi><mo>:</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>z</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{z \in \C: |z| \leq 1 \}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. After a long sequence of partial results that verified this law under additional assumptions on the distribution of the entries, the full circular law was recently established in \cite{TVcir2}. In this survey we describe some of the key ingredients used in the establishment of the circular law, in particular recent advances in understanding the Littlewood-Offord problem and its inverse.</p>{% endraw %}
