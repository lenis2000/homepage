{% raw %}<p>The one-dimensional asymmetric simple exclusion process (ASEP), where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> hard-core particles hop forward with rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and backward with rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q&lt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, is considered on a periodic lattice of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span> site. Using KPZ universality and previous results for the totally asymmetric model <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, precise conjectures are formulated for asymptotics at finite density <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>N</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho=N/L</annotation></semantics></math></span> of ASEP eigenstates close to the stationary state. The conjectures are checked with high precision using extrapolation methods on finite size Bethe ansatz numerics. For weak asymmetry <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>q</mi><mo>∼</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msqrt><mi>L</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1-q\sim1/\sqrt{L}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, double extrapolation combined with an integer relation algorithm gives an exact expression for the spectral gap up to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>10</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">10</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th order in the asymmetry.</p>{% endraw %}
