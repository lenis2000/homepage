This completes the picture of the investigation that began by Cunden, Mezzadri, O'Connell and Simm who obtained analogous results for the other three classical ensembles of random matrices, the Gaussian, the Laguerre and Jacobi. Our strategy of proof is somewhat different from the one employed previously due to the fact that the generalized Cauchy is the only classical ensemble which has a finite number of integer moments. Our arguments also apply, with straightforward modifications, to the Gaussian, Laguerre and Jacobi cases as well. We finally obtain a differential equation for the one-point density function of the eigenvalue distribution of this ensemble and establish the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> asymptotics of the moments.</p>{% endraw %}
