{% raw %}<p>An infinite log-gas formalism, due to Dyson, and independently Fogler and Shklovskii, is applied to the computation of conditioned gap probabilities at the hard and soft edges of random matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles. The conditioning is that there are <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> eigenvalues in the gap, with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≪</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>t</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n \ll |t|</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span> denoting the end point of the gap. It is found that the entropy term in the formalism must be replaced by a term involving the potential drop to obtain results consistent with known asymptotic expansions in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. With this modification made for general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the derived expansions - which are for the logarithm of the gap probabilities - are conjectured to be correct up to and including terms O<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>t</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\log|t|)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. They are shown to satisfy various consistency conditions, including an asymptotic duality formula relating <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>4</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">4/\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
