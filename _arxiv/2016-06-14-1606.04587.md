{% raw %}<p>We study the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-component priority asymmetric simple exclusion process (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ASEP) with reflecting boundaries. We obtain all invariant measures in explicit form and prove reversibility. Using the symmetry of the generator of the process under the quantum algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q[\mathfrak{gl}(n+1)]</annotation></semantics></math></span> we construct duality functions with respect to which the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ASEP is self-dual, both for the finite and the infinite integer lattice. For the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ASEP on the infinite lattice we use self-duality to derive in explicit form the time evolution of a family of measures with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span> shocks in terms of the transition probability of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span> coloured particles in a shock exclusion process with particle-dependent hopping rates and nearest-neighbour colour exchange. This process is a gas of particles that forms a bound state, corresponding to shock coalescence on macroscopic scale.</p>{% endraw %}
