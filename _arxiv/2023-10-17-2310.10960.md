{% raw %}<p>We consider the log-Gamma polymer in the half-space with bulk weights distributed as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Gamma</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\operatorname{Gamma}^{-1}(2\theta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and diagonal weights as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Gamma</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>α</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\operatorname{Gamma}^{-1}(\alpha+\theta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>θ</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\theta&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>θ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&gt;-\theta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show that in the bound phase, i.e., when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha\in (-\theta,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the endpoint of the polymer lies within an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">O(1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> stochastic window of the diagonal. This result gives the first rigorous proof of the pinned phenomena for the half-space polymers in the bound phase conjectured by Kardar(1985). We also show that the limiting quenched endpoint distribution of the polymer around the diagonal is given by a random probability mass function proportional to the exponential of a random walk with log-Gamma type increments.</p>{% endraw %}
