{% raw %}<p>We compute the number of rhombus tilings of a hexagon with sides <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>c</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>a</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>c</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a+2,b+2,c+2,a+2,b+2,c+2</annotation></semantics></math></span> with three fixed tiles touching the border. The particular case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a=b=c</annotation></semantics></math></span> solves a problem posed by Propp. Our result can also be viewed as the enumeration of plane partitions having <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a+2</annotation></semantics></math></span> rows and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>b</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b+2</annotation></semantics></math></span> columns, with largest entry <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>≤</mo><mi>c</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\le c+2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with a given number of entries <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>c</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">c+2</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the first row, a given number of entries 0 in the last column and a given bottom-left entry.</p>{% endraw %}
