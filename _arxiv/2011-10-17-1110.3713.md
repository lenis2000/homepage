{% raw %}<p>The Bernoulli sieve is the infinite "balls-in-boxes" occupancy scheme with random frequencies <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><msub><mi>W</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><msub><mi>W</mi><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>W</mi><mi>k</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P_k=W_1... W_{k-1}(1-W_k)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where $(W_k)_{k\in\mn}$ are independent copies of a random variable <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W</annotation></semantics></math></span> taking values in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(0,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Assuming that the number of balls equals <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>L</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> denote the number of empty boxes within the occupancy range. In the paper we investigate convergence in distribution of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>L</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the two cases which remained open after the previous studies. In particular, provided that $\me |\log W|=\me |\log (1-W)|=\infty$ and that the law of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W</annotation></semantics></math></span> assigns comparable masses to the neighborhoods of 0 and 1, it is shown that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>L</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> weakly converges to a geometric law. This result is derived as a corollary to a more general assertion concerning the number of zero decrements of nonincreasing Markov chains. In the case that $\me |\log W|<\infty$ and $\me |\log (1-W)|=\infty$ we derive several further possible modes of convergence in distribution of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>L</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. It turns out that the class of possible limiting laws for, properly normalized and centered, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>L</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> includes normal laws and spectrally negative stable laws with finite mean. While investigating the second problem we develop some general results concerning the weak convergence of renewal shot-noise processes. This allows us to answer a question asked in Mikosch and Resnick (2006).</p>{% endraw %}
