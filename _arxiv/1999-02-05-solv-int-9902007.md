{% raw %}<p>The asymptotic solution for the Painleve-2 equation with small parameter is considered. The solution has algebraic behavior before point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_*</annotation></semantics></math></span> and fast oscillating behavior after the point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_*</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the transition layer the behavior of the asymptotic solution is more complicated. The leading term of the asymptotics satisfies the Painleve-1 equation and some elliptic equation with constant coefficients, where the solution of the Painleve-1 equation has poles. The uniform smooth asymptotics are constructed in the interval, containing the critical point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_*</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
