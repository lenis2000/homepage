{% raw %}<p>We study the Kardar-Parisi-Zhang equation on the half-line <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>⩾</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x \geqslant 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> with Neumann type boundary condition. Stationary measures of the KPZ dynamics were characterized in recent work: they depend on two parameters, the boundary parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>u</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">u</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the dynamics, and the drift <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>v</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">-v</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the initial condition at infinity. We consider the fluctuations of the height field when the initial condition is given by one of these stationary processes. At large time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t</annotation></semantics></math></span>, it is natural to rescale parameters as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>u</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>v</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>a</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(u,v)=t^{-1/3}(a,b)</annotation></semantics></math></span> to study the critical region. In the special case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a+b=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, treated in previous works, the stationary process is simply Brownian. However, these Brownian stationary measures are particularly relevant in the bound phase (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a&lt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>) but not in the unbound phase. For instance, starting from the flat or droplet initial data, the height field near the boundary converges to the stationary process with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>b</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which is not Brownian. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>b</mi><mo>⩾</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a+b\geqslant 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we determine exactly the large time distribution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>F</mi><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>b</mi></mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">s</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">t</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">a</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">t</mi></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F_{a,b}^{\rm stat}</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the height function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h(0,t)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As an application, we obtain the exact covariance of the height field in a half-line at two times <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>≪</mo><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo>≪</mo><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1\ll t_1\ll t_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> starting from stationary initial data, as well as estimates, when starting from droplet initial data, in the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo>→</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_1/t_2\to 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
