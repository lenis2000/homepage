{% raw %}<p>Dyson's short-distance universality of the correlation functions implies the universality of P(s), the level-spacing distribution. We first briefly review how this property is understood for unitary invariant ensembles and consider next a Hamiltonian H = H_0+ V , in which H_0 is a given, non-random, N by N matrix, and V is an Hermitian random matrix with a Gaussian probability distribution. n-point correlation function may still be expressed as a determinant of an n by n matrix, whose elements are given by a kernel <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>λ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>μ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K(\lambda,\mu)</annotation></semantics></math></span> as in the H_0=0 case. From this representation we can show that Dyson's short-distance universality still holds. We then conclude that P(s) is independent of H_0.</p>{% endraw %}
