{% raw %}<p>We study a family of periodically weighted Aztec diamond dimer models near their turning points. We establish that, asymptotically, as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\rightarrow\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, their fluctuations there, scaled by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>N</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{N}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, are described by a marked GUE-corners process. This limiting point process is constructed by assigning a Bernoulli mark independently to each particle in a realization of the GUE-corners process. The Bernoulli parameters associated with the random marks reflect the periodicity of the model in the limit. To prove this result we use a double-contour integral representation of the inverse Kasteleyn matrix on a higher-genus Riemann surface, which is well-suited for asymptotic analysis.</p>{% endraw %}
