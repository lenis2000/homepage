{% raw %}<p>We consider the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-point, fixed-time large deviations of the KPZ equation with the narrow wedge initial condition. The scope consists of concave-configured, upper-tail deviations and a wide range of scaling regimes that allows time to be short, unit-order, and long. We prove the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-point large deviation principle and characterize, with proof, the corresponding spacetime limit shape. Our proof is based on the results -- from the companion paper Tsai (2023) -- on moments of the stochastic heat equation and utilizes ideas coming from a tree decomposition. Behind our proof lies the phenomenon where the major contribution of the noise concentrates around certain corridors in spacetime, and we explicitly describe the corridors.</p>{% endraw %}
