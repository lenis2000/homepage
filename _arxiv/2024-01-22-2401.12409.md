{% raw %}<p>The paper "An efficient sampling scheme for the eigenvalues of dual Wishart matrices", by I.~Santamar\'ia and V.~Elvira, [\emph{IEEE Signal Processing Letters}, vol.~28, pp.~2177--2181, 2021] \cite{SE21}, poses the question of efficient sampling from the eigenvalue probability density function of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n \times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> central complex Wishart matrices with variance matrix equal to the identity. Underlying such complex Wishart matrices is a rectangular <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R \times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>R</mi><mo>≥</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(R \ge n)</annotation></semantics></math></span> standard complex Gaussian matrix, requiring then <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>R</mi><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2Rn</annotation></semantics></math></span> real random variables for their generation. The main result of \cite{SE21} gives a formula involving just two classical distributions specifying the two eigenvalues in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The purpose of this Letter is to point out that existing results in the literature give two distinct ways to efficiently sample the eigenvalues in the general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> case. One is in terms of the eigenvalues of a tridiagonal matrix which factors as the product of a bidiagonal matrix and its transpose, with the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2n+1</annotation></semantics></math></span> nonzero entries of the latter given by (the square root of) certain chi-squared random variables. The other is as the generalised eigenvalues for a pair of bidiagonal matrices, also containing a total of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2n+1</annotation></semantics></math></span> chi-squared random variables. Moreover, these characterisation persist in the case of that the variance matrix consists of a single spike, and for the case of real Wishart matrices.</p>{% endraw %}
