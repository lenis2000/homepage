{% raw %}<p>Examples of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Jacobi ensemble specify the joint distribution of the transmission eigenvalues in scattering problems. In this context, there has been interest in the distribution of the trace, as the trace corresponds to the conductance. Earlier, in the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the trace statistic was isolated in studies of covariance matrices in multivariate statistics, where it is referred to as Pillai's <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>V</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V</annotation></semantics></math></span> statistic. In this context, Davis showed that for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> the trace statistic, and its Fourier-Laplace transform, can be characterised by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>×</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(N+1) \times (N+1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> matrix differential equations. For the Fourier-Laplace transform, this leads to a vector recurrence for the moments. However, for the distribution itself the characterisation provided was incomplete, as the connection problem of determining the linear combination of Frobenius type solutions that correspond to the statistic was not solved. We solve this connection problem for Jacobi parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>b</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b</annotation></semantics></math></span> and Dyson index <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> non-negative integers. For the other Jacobi parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a</annotation></semantics></math></span> also a non-negative integer, the power series portion of each Frobenius solution terminates to a polynomial, and the matrix differential equation gives a recurrence for their computation.</p>{% endraw %}
