We also derive the exponent corresponding to the area enclosed by a loop which is shown to be 1 for all values of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>4</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q \in (0,4)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Applying the KPZ formula we find that this value is consistent with the dimension of SLE curves and SLE duality.</p>{% endraw %}
