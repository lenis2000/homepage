{% raw %}<p>Consider a random regular graph of fixed degree <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> vertices. We study spectral properties of the adjacency matrix and of random Schr\"odinger operators on such a graph as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> tends to infinity. We prove that the integrated density of states on the graph converges to the integrated density of states on the infinite regular tree and we give uniform bounds on the rate of convergence. This allows to estimate the number of eigenvalues in intervals of size comparable to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\log_{d-1}^{-1}(n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Based on related estimates for the Green function we derive results about delocalization of eigenvectors.</p>{% endraw %}
