{% raw %}<p>In this paper we continue the study of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-operators in the six-vertex model and its higher spin generalizations. In [1] we derived a new expression for the higher spin <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix associated with the affine quantum algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>l</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\widehat{sl(2)})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Taking a special limit in this <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix we obtained new formulas for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-operators acting in the tensor product of representation spaces with arbitrary complex spin. Here we use a different strategy and construct <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-operators as integral operators with factorized kernels based on the original Baxter's method used in the solution of the eight-vertex model. We compare this approach with the method developed in [1] and find the explicit connection between two constructions. We also discuss a reduction to the case of finite-dimensional representations with (half-) integer spins.</p>{% endraw %}
