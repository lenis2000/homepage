{% raw %}<p>Internal DLA is a discrete random growth model describing growing clusters of particles. Its limiting shape and fluctuations are well understood when the underlying graph is the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional lattice or the cylinder <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo>×</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Z}_N \times \mathbb{Z}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the latter geometry, the average fluctuations of IDLA have been shown to converge to the GFF. In this note we generalise this result by showing that, for any vertex-transitive base graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> satisfying an eigenvalue convergence condition, the average fluctuations of IDLA on the cylinder <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo>×</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_N \times \mathbb{Z}</annotation></semantics></math></span> are given by a GFF. On the way, we present an improved bound on the clusters' maximal fluctuations, which is of independent interest and which implies a shape theorem for IDLA on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo>×</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_N \times \mathbb{Z}</annotation></semantics></math></span> for any vertex-transitive base graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>V</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
