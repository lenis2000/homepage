We show that when the empirical measure of the eigenvalues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> satisfies some technical conditions, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>L</mi><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L_{A_n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges towards a rotationally invariant measure on the complex plan whose support is a single ring.
