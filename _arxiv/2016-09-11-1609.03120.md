{% raw %}<p>We introduce a new family of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\times N</annotation></semantics></math></span> random real symmetric matrix ensembles, the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-checkerboard matrices, whose limiting spectral measure has two components which can be determined explicitly. All but <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> eigenvalues are in the bulk, and their behavior, appropriately normalized, converges to the semi-circle as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>; the remaining <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> are tightly constrained near <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N/k</annotation></semantics></math></span> and their distribution converges to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k \times k</annotation></semantics></math></span> hollow GOE ensemble (this is the density arising by modifying the GOE ensemble by forcing all entries on the main diagonal to be zero). Similar results hold for complex and quaternionic analogues. We isolate the two regimes by using matrix perturbation results and a nonstandard weight function for the eigenvalues, then derive their limiting distributions using a modification of the method of moments and analysis of the resulting combinatorics.</p>{% endraw %}
