{% raw %}<p>We consider finite-range asymmetric exclusion processes on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb Z</annotation></semantics></math></span> with non-zero drift. The diffusivity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is expected to be of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\mathcal O}(t^{1/3})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We prove that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≥</mo><mi>C</mi><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D(t)\ge Ct^{1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the weak (Tauberian) sense that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mo>∫</mo><mn>0</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></msubsup><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>λ</mi><mi>t</mi></mrow></msup><mi>t</mi><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>d</mi><mi>t</mi><mo>≥</mo><mi>C</mi><msup><mi>λ</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>7</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\int_0^\infty e^{-\lambda t}tD(t)dt \ge C\lambda^{-7/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda\to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The proof employs the resolvent method to make a direct comparison with the totally asymmetric simple exclusion process, for which the result is a consequence of the scaling limit for the two-point function recently obtained by Ferrari and Spohn. In the nearest neighbor case, we show further that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">tD(t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is monotone, and hence we can conclude that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≥</mo><mi>C</mi><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>t</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>7</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D(t)\ge Ct^{1/3}(\log t)^{-7/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the usual sense.</p>{% endraw %}
