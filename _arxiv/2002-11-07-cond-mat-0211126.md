{% raw %}<p>Bosons in the form of ultra cold alkali atoms can be confined to a one dimensional (1d) domain by the use of harmonic traps. This motivates the study of the ground state occupations <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>λ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> of effective single particle states <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ϕ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\phi_i</annotation></semantics></math></span>, in the theoretical 1d impenetrable Bose gas. Both the system on a circle and the harmonically trapped system are considered. The <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>λ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ϕ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\phi_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> are the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions respectively of the one body density matrix. We present a detailed numerical and analytic study of this problem. Our main results are the explicit scaled forms of the density matrices, from which it is deduced that for fixed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>i</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">i</annotation></semantics></math></span> the occupations <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>λ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda_i</annotation></semantics></math></span> are asymptotically proportional to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>N</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{N}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in both the circular and harmonically trapped cases.</p>{% endraw %}
