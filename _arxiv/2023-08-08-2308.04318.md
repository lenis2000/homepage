{% raw %}<p>In this paper we consider non-intersecting Brownian bridges, under fairly general upper and lower boundaries, and starting and ending data. Under the assumption that these boundary data induce a smooth limit shape (without empty facets), we establish two results. The first is a nearly optimal concentration bound for the Brownian bridges in this model. The second is that the bulk local statistics of these bridges along any fixed time converge to the sine process.</p>{% endraw %}
