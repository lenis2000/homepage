{% raw %}<p>The semi-infinite Atlas process is a one-dimensional system of Brownian particles, where only the leftmost particle gets a unit drift to the right. Its particle spacing process has infinitely many stationary measures, with one distinguished translation invariant reversible measure. We show that the latter is attractive for a large class of initial configurations of slowly growing (or bounded) particle densities. Key to our proof is a new estimate on the rate of convergence to equilibrium for the particle spacing in a triangular array of finite, large size systems.</p>{% endraw %}
