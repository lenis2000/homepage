{% raw %}<p>We define a new diffusive matrix model converging towards the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> -Dyson Brownian motion for all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\in [0,2]</annotation></semantics></math></span> that provides an explicit construction of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles of random matrices that is invariant under the orthogonal/unitary group. For small values of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>, our process allows one to interpolate smoothly between the Gaussian distribution and the Wigner semi-circle.
