{% raw %}<p>We study the local semicircle law for Gaussian <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles at the edge of the spectrum. We prove that at the almost optimal level of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>ϵ</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{-2/3+\epsilon}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the local semicircle law holds for all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta \geq 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> at the edge. The proof of the main theorem relies on the calculation of the moments of the tridiagonal model of Gaussian <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>-ensembles up to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-moment where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>ϵ</mi></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_n = O(n^{2/3-\epsilon})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The result is the analogous to the result of Sinai and Soshnikov for Wigner matrices, but the combinatorics involved in the calculations are different.</p>{% endraw %}
