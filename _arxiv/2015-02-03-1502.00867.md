{% raw %}<p>We study the lower tail large deviation problem for subgraph counts in a random graph. Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>H</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_H</annotation></semantics></math></span> denote the number of copies of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H</annotation></semantics></math></span> in an Erd\H{o}s-R\'enyi random graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">G</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>p</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{G}(n,p)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We are interested in estimating the lower tail probability <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">P</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>H</mi></msub><mo>≤</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>δ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">E</mi><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>H</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{P}(X_H \le (1-\delta) \mathbb{E} X_H)</annotation></semantics></math></span> for fixed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>δ</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0 &lt; \delta &lt; 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Thanks to the results of Chatterjee, Dembo, and Varadhan, this large deviation problem has been reduced to a natural variational problem over graphons, at least for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>≥</mo><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>α</mi><mi>H</mi></msub></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p \ge n^{-\alpha_H}</annotation></semantics></math></span> (and conjecturally for a larger range of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>). We study this variational problem and provide a partial characterization of the so-called "replica symmetric" phase. Informally, our main result says that for every <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>δ</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>δ</mi><mi>H</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0 &lt; \delta &lt; \delta_H</annotation></semantics></math></span> for some <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>δ</mi><mi>H</mi></msub><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta_H &gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p \to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> slowly, the main contribution to the lower tail probability comes from Erd\H{o}s-R\'enyi random graphs with a uniformly tilted edge density. On the other hand, this is false for non-bipartite <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span> close to 1.</p>{% endraw %}
