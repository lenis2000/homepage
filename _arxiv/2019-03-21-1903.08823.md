{% raw %}<p>A fundamental question in random matrix theory is to quantify the optimal rate of convergence to universal laws. We take up this problem for the Laguerre <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> ensemble, characterised by the Dyson parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and the Laguerre weight <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>x</mi><mi>a</mi></msup><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>β</mi><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x^a e^{-\beta x/2}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x &gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the hard edge limit. The latter relates to the eigenvalues in the vicinity of the origin in the scaled variable <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>↦</mo><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>4</mn><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x \mapsto x/4N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Previous work has established the corresponding functional form of various statistical quantities --- for example the distribution of the smallest eigenvalue, provided that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mrow><mo>≥</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a \in \mathbb Z_{\ge 0}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show, using the theory of multidimensional hypergeometric functions based on Jack polynomials, that with the modified hard edge scaling <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>↦</mo><mi>x</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>4</mn><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>a</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>β</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x \mapsto x/4(N+a/\beta)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the rate of convergence to the limiting distribution is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">O(1/N^2)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which is optimal. In the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a&gt; -1</annotation></semantics></math></span> the explicit functional form of the distribution of the smallest eigenvalue at this order can be computed, as it can for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a=1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta &gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. An iterative scheme is presented to numerically approximate the functional form for general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mrow><mo>≥</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a \in \mathbb Z_{\ge 2}</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
