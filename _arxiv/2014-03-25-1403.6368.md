{% raw %}<p>It was proved by Akemann, Ipsen and Kieburg that squared singular values of products of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M</annotation></semantics></math></span> complex Ginibre random matrices form a determinantal point process whose correlation kernel is expressible in terms of Meijer's <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span>-functions. Kuijlaars and Zhang recently showed that at the edge of the spectrum, this correlation kernel has a remarkable scaling limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>M</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_M(x,y)</annotation></semantics></math></span> which can be understood as a generalization of the classical Bessel kernel of Random Matrix Theory. In this paper we investigate the Fredholm determinant of the operator with the kernel <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>M</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msub><mi>χ</mi><mi>J</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_M(x,y)\chi_J(y)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a disjoint union of intervals, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi><mo>=</mo><msub><mo>∪</mo><mi>j</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>a</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>a</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J=\cup_j(a_{2j-1},a_{2j})</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>χ</mi><mi>J</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\chi_J</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the characteristic function of the set <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This Fredholm determinant is equal to the probability that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J</annotation></semantics></math></span> contains no particles of the limiting determinantal point process defined by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>M</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>y</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_M(x,y)</annotation></semantics></math></span> (the gap probability). We derive Hamiltonian differential equations associated with the corresponding Fredholm determinant, and relate them with the monodromy preserving deformation equations of the Jimbo, Miwa, Mori, Ueno and Sato theory. In the special case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>J</mi><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>s</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J=(0,s)</annotation></semantics></math></span> we give a formula for the gap probability in terms of a solution of a system of non-linear ordinary differential equations.</p>{% endraw %}
