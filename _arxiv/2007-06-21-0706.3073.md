{% raw %}<p>We introduce the tau-function of a rational d-connection and its isomonodromy transformations. We show that in a continuous limit our tau-function agrees with the Jimbo-Miwa-Ueno tau-function, compute the tau-function for the isomonodromy transformations leading to difference Painleve V and difference Painleve VI equations, and prove that the gap probability for a wide class of discrete random matrix type models can be viewed as the tau-function for an associated d-connection.</p>{% endraw %}
