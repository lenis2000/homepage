{% raw %}<p>In this paper, we study estimation of parameters in a two-parameter Potts model with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span> colors and coupling matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We characterize concrete sufficient conditions for existence of the pseudo-likelihood estimator of the Potts model, in terms of the local magnetic fields, and give sufficient conditions for the validity of the above characterization. We then provide sufficient criteria for estimation of both parameters at the optimal rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>N</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{N}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In particular, if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the scaled adjacency matrix of a graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, then we show that joint estimation is possible if either <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> has bounded degree or is irregular. In contrast, we give an example of a graph sequence <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> which is approximately regular and dense, where no consistent estimator exists. We also show that one-parameter estimation at the optimal rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>N</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{N}</annotation></semantics></math></span> holds under much milder conditions when the other parameter is known. Along the way, we develop a concentration result for mean-field Potts models using the framework of nonlinear large deviations. Compared to the Ising case, our results for the Potts case require a novel analysis across multiple colors.</p>{% endraw %}
