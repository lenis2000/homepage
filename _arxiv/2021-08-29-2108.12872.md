{% raw %}<p>In this paper we study uniformly random lozenge tilings of strip domains. Under the assumption that the limiting arctic boundary has at most one cusp, we prove a nearly optimal concentration estimate for the tiling height functions and arctic boundaries on such domains: with overwhelming probability the tiling height function is within <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mi>δ</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span> of its limit shape, and the tiling arctic boundary is within <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>δ</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{1/3+\delta}</annotation></semantics></math></span> to its limit shape, for arbitrarily small <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This concentration result will be used in [AH21] to prove that the edge statistics of simply-connected polygonal domains, subject to a technical assumption on their limit shape, converge to the Airy line ensemble.</p>{% endraw %}
