{% raw %}<p>We study the Gaussian fluctuations of a nonlinear stochastic heat equation in spatial dimension two. The equation is driven by a Gaussian multiplicative noise. The noise is white in time, smoothed in space at scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and tuned logarithmically by a factor <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><msqrt><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><msup><mi>ε</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup></mrow></msqrt></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac{1}{\sqrt{\log \varepsilon^{-1}}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in its strength. We prove that, after centering and rescaling, the solution random field converges in distribution to an Edwards-Wilkinson limit as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>↓</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon \downarrow 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The tool we used here is the Malliavin-Stein's method. We also give a functional version of this result.</p>{% endraw %}
