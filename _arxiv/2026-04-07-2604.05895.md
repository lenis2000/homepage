{% raw %}<p>This article derives full asymptotic expansions for integrals of the form \[ \int_{0}^{1}f(u)(1+q\cdot u^{n})^{w/n}du \] as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\rightarrow\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with parameters real <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>w</mi><mo mathvariant="normal">≠</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">w\neq 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q\in(-1,1]</annotation></semantics></math></span>, or positive <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>w</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">w</annotation></semantics></math></span> for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=-1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We relate the coefficients of the asymptotic expansions to Nielsen's generalized polylogarithms. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=-1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we obtain an expansion in terms of multiple zeta values, which in this setting, reduce to ordinary zeta values. A key point is that for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the integrals typically produce alternating multiple zeta values; we formulate a precise symmetry constraint on the relevant coefficient sequence under which all coefficients reduce to polynomials in ordinary zeta values. We also translate this symmetry into a statement about a binomial transform, and we verify the condition for several classical Appell-type families, like Euler, Bernoulli, Genocchi, and Hermite. Finally, we obtain precise results about the convergence of norms of random variables.</p>{% endraw %}
