{% raw %}<p>We introduce multilevel versions of Dyson Brownian motions of arbitrary parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, generalizing the interlacing reflected Brownian motions of Warren for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta=2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Such processes unify <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> corners processes and Dyson Brownian motions in a single object. Our approach is based on the approximation by certain multilevel discrete Markov chains of independent interest, which are defined by means of Jack symmetric polynomials. In particular, this approach allows to show that the levels in a multilevel Dyson Brownian motion are intertwined (at least for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\ge 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>) and to give the corresponding link explicitly.</p>{% endraw %}
