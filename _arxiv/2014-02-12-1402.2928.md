We prove that if the edges are assigned independent mean 1 exponential costs, the minimum length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> of a path from <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(0, 0, \dots, 0)</annotation></semantics></math></span> to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(1, 1, \dots, 1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges in probability to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ln</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><msqrt><mn>2</mn></msqrt><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≈</mo><mn>0.881</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ln(1+\sqrt{2}) \approx 0.881</annotation></semantics></math></span>. It has previously been shown by Fill and Pemantle (1993) that this so-called first-passage time asymptotically almost surely satisfies <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ln</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><msqrt><mn>2</mn></msqrt><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>o</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≤</mo><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>≤</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>o</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ln(1+\sqrt{2}) - o(1) \leq T_n \leq 1+o(1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and has been conjectured to converge in probability by Bollob\'as and Kohayakawa (1997). A key idea of our proof is to consider a lower bound on Richardson's model, closely related to the branching process used in the article by Fill and Pemantle to obtain the bound <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>≥</mo><mi>ln</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mrow><mo fence="true">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><msqrt><mn>2</mn></msqrt><mo fence="true">)</mo></mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>o</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_n \geq \ln\left(1+\sqrt{2}\right)-o(1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We derive an explicit lower bound on the probability that a vertex is infected at a given time. This result is formulated for a general graph and may be applicable in a more general setting.</p>{% endraw %}
