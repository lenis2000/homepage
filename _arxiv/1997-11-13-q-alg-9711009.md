{% raw %}<p>The spectral decomposition of the path space of the vertex model associated to the level <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>l</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">l</annotation></semantics></math></span> representation of the quantized affine algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>l</mi></mrow><mo>^</mo></mover><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\hat{sl}_n)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is studied. The spectrum and its degeneracy are parametrized by skew Young diagrams and what we call nonmovable tableaux on them, respectively. As a result we obtain the characters for the degeneracy of the spectrum in terms of an alternating sum of skew Schur functions. Also studied are new combinatorial descriptions (spectral decomposition) of the Kostka numbers and the Kostka--Foulkes polynomials. As an application we give a new proof of Nakayashiki--Yamada's theorem about the branching functions of the level <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>l</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">l</annotation></semantics></math></span> basic representation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>l</mi><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Λ</mi><mi>k</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">l\Lambda_k</annotation></semantics></math></span> of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi>s</mi><mi>l</mi></mrow><mo>^</mo></mover><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\hat{sl}_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a generalization of the theorem.</p>{% endraw %}
