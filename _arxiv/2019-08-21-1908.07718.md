{% raw %}<p>This paper studies the game of guessing riffle-shuffled cards with complete feedback. A deck of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> cards labelled 1 to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is riffle-shuffled once and placed on a table. A player tries to guess the cards from top and is given complete feedback after each guess. The goal is to find the guessing strategy with maximum reward (expected number of correct guesses). We give the optimal strategy for this game and prove that the maximum expected reward is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo><msqrt><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>π</mi></mrow></msqrt><mo>⋅</mo><msqrt><mi>n</mi></msqrt><mo>+</mo><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n/2+\sqrt{2/\pi}\cdot\sqrt{n}+O(1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, partially solving an open problem of Bayer and Diaconis.</p>{% endraw %}
