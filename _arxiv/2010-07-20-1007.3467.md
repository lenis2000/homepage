{% raw %}<p>Gilbert Strang posited that a permutation matrix of bandwidth <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>w</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">w</annotation></semantics></math></span> can be written as a product of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>w</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N &lt; 2w</annotation></semantics></math></span> permutation matrices of bandwidth 1. A proof employing a greedy ``parallel bubblesort'' algorithm on the rows of the permutation matrix is detailed and further points of interest are elaborated.</p>{% endraw %}
