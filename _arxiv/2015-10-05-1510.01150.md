{% raw %}<p>Continuous Time Random Walk(CTRW) is a model where particle's jumps in space are coupled with waiting times before each jump. A Continuous Time Random Walk Limit(CTRWL) is obtained by a limit procedure on a CTRW and can be used to model anomalous diffusion. The distribution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mrow><mo fence="true">(</mo><mi>d</mi><mi>x</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo fence="true">)</mo></mrow></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p\left(dx,t\right)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of a CTRWL <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>t</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_{t}</annotation></semantics></math></span> satisfies a Fractional Fokker-Planck Equation(FFPE). Since CTRWLs are usually not Markovian, their one dimensional FFPE is not enough to completely define them. In this paper we find the FFPEs of the distribution of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>t</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_{t}</annotation></semantics></math></span> at multiple times , i.e. the distribution of the random vector <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo fence="true">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>1</mn></msub></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><msub><mi>t</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></msub><mo fence="true">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\left(X_{t_{1}},...,X_{t_{n}}\right)</annotation></semantics></math></span> for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>t</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo>&lt;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>t</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t_{1}&lt;...&lt;t_{n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> for a large class of CTRWLs. This allows us to define CTRWLs by their finite dimensional FFPEs.</p>{% endraw %}
