{% raw %}<p>In 2010, Duminil-Copin and Smirnov proved a long-standing conjecture of Nienhuis, made in 1982, that the growth constant of self-avoiding walks on the hexagonal (a.k.a. honeycomb) lattice is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi><mo>=</mo><msqrt><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo><msqrt><mn>2</mn></msqrt></mrow></msqrt><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu=\sqrt{2+\sqrt{2}}.</annotation></semantics></math></span> A key identity used in that proof was later generalised by Smirnov so as to apply to a general O(n) loop model with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\in [-2,2]</annotation></semantics></math></span> (the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> corresponding to SAWs). We modify this model by restricting to a half-plane and introducing a surface fugacity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>y</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">y</annotation></semantics></math></span> associated with boundary sites (also called surface sites), and obtain a generalisation of Smirnov's identity. The critical value of the surface fugacity was conjectured by Batchelor and Yung in 1995 to be <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>y</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">c</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msqrt><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow></msqrt><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">y_{\rm c}=1+2/\sqrt{2-n}.</annotation></semantics></math></span> This value plays a crucial role in our generalized identity, just as the value of growth constant did in Smirnov's identity. For the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, corresponding to \saws\ interacting with a surface, we prove the conjectured value of the critical surface fugacity. A crucial part of the proof involves demonstrating that the generating function of self-avoiding bridges of height <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>T</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T</annotation></semantics></math></span>, taken at its critical point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span>, tends to 0 as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>T</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T</annotation></semantics></math></span> increases, as predicted from SLE theory.</p>{% endraw %}
