{% raw %}<p>The Shimura operators are a certain distinguished basis for invariant differential operators on a Hermitian symmetric space. Answering a question of Shimura, Sahi and Zhang showed that the Harish-Chandra images of these operators are specializations of certain <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi><mi>C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">BC</annotation></semantics></math></span>-symmetric interpolation polynomials that were defined by Okounkov. We consider the analogs of Shimura operators for the Hermitian symmetric superpair <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="fraktur">k</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{k})</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mo>=</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>p</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mn>2</mn><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{g}= \mathfrak{gl}(2p|2q)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">k</mi><mo>=</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>p</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>⊕</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>p</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{k}= \mathfrak{gl}(p|q)\oplus \mathfrak{gl}(p|q)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and we prove their Harish-Chandra images are specializations of certain <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi><mi>C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">BC</annotation></semantics></math></span>-supersymmetric interpolation polynomials introduced by Sergeev--Veselov.</p>{% endraw %}
