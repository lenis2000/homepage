It was conjectured by P. Winkler that for large enough <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M</annotation></semantics></math></span> the set of pairs of non-colliding trajectories <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">X</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Y</mi><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{\mathbb{X},\mathbb{Y} \} </annotation></semantics></math></span> has positive measure. In this representation Winkler's conjecture is equivalent to the existence of an infinite open cluster for large enough <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
