{% raw %}<p>We consider a three-dimensional (3D) lattice model associated with the intertwiner of the quantized coordinate ring <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>l</mi><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_q(sl_3)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and introduce a family of layer to layer transfer matrices on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m\times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> square lattice. By using the tetrahedron equation we derive their commutativity and bilinear relations mixing various boundary conditions. At <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m=n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, they lead to a new proof of the steady state probability of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-species totally asymmetric zero range process obtained recently by the authors, revealing the 3D integrability in the matrix product construction.</p>{% endraw %}
