{% raw %}<p>Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> be an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N\ts N</annotation></semantics></math></span> random symmetric matrix with independent equidistributed entries. If the law <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>P</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the entries has a finite second moment, it was shown by Wigner \cite{wigner} that the empirical distribution of the eigenvalues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, once renormalized by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>N</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{N}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, converges almost surely and in expectation to the so-called semicircular distribution as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> goes to infinity. In this paper we study the same question when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>P</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">P</annotation></semantics></math></span> is in the domain of attraction of an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>-stable law. We prove that if we renormalize the eigenvalues by a constant <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>a</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mi>α</mi></mfrac></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{\frac{1}{\alpha}}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the corresponding spectral distribution converges in expectation towards a law <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>μ</mi><mi>α</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu_\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> which only depends on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We characterize <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>μ</mi><mi>α</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu_\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> and study some of its properties; it is a heavy-tailed probability measure which is absolutely continuous with respect to Lebesgue measure except possibly on a compact set of capacity zero.</p>{% endraw %}
