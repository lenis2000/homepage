{% raw %}<p>We study the Cauchy problem for the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) hierarchy in the small dispersion limit where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\e\to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For negative analytic initial data with a single negative hump, we prove that for small times, the solution is approximated by the solution to the hyperbolic transport equation which corresponds to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\e=0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Near the time of gradient catastrophe for the transport equation, we show that the solution to the KdV hierarchy is approximated by a particular Painlev\'e transcendent. This supports Dubrovins universality conjecture concerning the critical behavior of Hamiltonian perturbations of hyperbolic equations. We use the Riemann-Hilbert approach to prove our results.</p>{% endraw %}
