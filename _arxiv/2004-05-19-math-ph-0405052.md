{% raw %}<p>We study a model of random surfaces arising in the dimer model on the honeycomb lattice. For a fixed ``wire frame'' boundary condition, as the lattice spacing <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϵ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon\to0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, Cohn, Kenyon and Propp [CKP] showed the almost sure convergence of a random surface to a non-random limit shape <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Σ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Sigma_0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In [KO], Okounkov and the author showed how to parametrize the limit shapes in terms of analytic functions, in particular constructing a natural conformal structure on them. We show here that when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Σ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Sigma_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> has no facets, for a family of boundary conditions approximating the wire frame, the large-scale surface fluctuations (height fluctuations) about <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Σ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Sigma_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> converge as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϵ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon\to0</annotation></semantics></math></span> to a Gaussian free field for the above conformal structure. We also show that the local statistics of the fluctuations near a given point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x</annotation></semantics></math></span> are, as conjectured in [CKP], given by the unique ergodic Gibbs measure (on plane configurations) whose slope is the slope of the tangent plane of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Σ</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Sigma_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
