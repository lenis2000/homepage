{% raw %}<p>Given a permutation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau</annotation></semantics></math></span> defined on a set of combinatorial objects <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S</annotation></semantics></math></span>, together with some statistic <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>f</mi><mo>:</mo><mi>S</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f:S\rightarrow \mathbb{R}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we say that the triple <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">⟨</mo><mi>S</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>τ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>f</mi><mo stretchy="false">⟩</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\langle S, \tau,f \rangle</annotation></semantics></math></span> exhibits homomesy if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>f</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f</annotation></semantics></math></span> has the same average along all orbits of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau</annotation></semantics></math></span> in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>S</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This phenomenon was noticed by Panyushev (2007) and later studied, named and extended by Propp and Roby (2013). After Propp and Roby's paper, homomesy has received a lot of attention and a number of mathematicians are intrigued by it. While seeming ubiquitous, homomesy is often surprisingly non-trivial to prove. Propp and Roby studied homomesy in the set of ideals in the product of two chains, with two well known permutations, rowmotion and promotion, the statistic being the size of the ideal. In this paper we extend their results to generalized rowmotion and promotion together with a wider class of statistics in product of two chains . Moreover, we derive some homomesy results in posets of type A and B. We believe that the framework that we set up can be used to prove similar results in wider classes of posets.</p>{% endraw %}
