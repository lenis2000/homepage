{% raw %}<p>Consider a real diagonal deterministic matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> with spectral measure converging to a compactly supported probability measure. We perturb this matrix by adding a random finite rank matrix, with delocalized eigenvectors. We show that the joint law of the extreme eigenvalues of the perturbed model satisfies a large deviation principle in the scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with a good rate function given by a variational formula. We tackle both cases when the extreme eigenvalues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> converge to the edges of the support of the limiting measure and when we allow some eigenvalues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, that we call outliers, to converge out of the bulk. We can also generalise our results to the case when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is random, with law proportional to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>n</mi><mi>T</mi><mi>r</mi><mi>a</mi><mi>c</mi><mi>e</mi><mi>V</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>X</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup><mi mathvariant="normal">d</mi><mi>X</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">e^{- n Trace V(X)}\ud X,</annotation></semantics></math></span> for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>V</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V</annotation></semantics></math></span> growing fast enough at infinity and any perturbation of finite rank.</p>{% endraw %}
