{% raw %}<p>We construct the multilevel correlation kernel for the rising GUE eigenvalue process starting from a fixed initial configuration <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>x</mi><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x^{(m)}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and show that it converges on short time scales (as quickly as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mtext>polylog</mtext><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>m</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\text{polylog}(m)</annotation></semantics></math></span>) to the extended semi-discrete sine kernel. As an application, we show fixed-energy universality of bulk local statistics of complex Hermitian Wigner matrices matching the covariance structure of GUE and with a finite <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>4</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>ε</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">4+\varepsilon</annotation></semantics></math></span> moment for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This application demonstrates that it is possible to obtain universality of bulk local statistics under near-optimal moment assumptions without using a Dyson Brownian motion relaxation step, which was a key ingredient in many results on this topic.</p>{% endraw %}
