{% raw %}<p>Random transvections generate a walk on the space of symplectic forms on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi mathvariant="bold">F</mi><mi>q</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbf{F}_q^{2n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The main result is establishing cutoff for this Markov chain. After <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n+c</annotation></semantics></math></span> steps, the walk is close to uniform while before <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n-c</annotation></semantics></math></span>, it is far from uniform. The upper bound is proved by explicitly finding and bounding the eigenvalues of the random walk. The lower bound is found by showing that the support of the walk is exponentially small if only <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n-c</annotation></semantics></math></span> steps are taken. The result can be viewed as a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-deformation of a result of Diaconis and Holmes on a random walk on matchings.</p>{% endraw %}
