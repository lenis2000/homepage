Secondly, we prove (for all complex <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>u</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">u</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>v</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">v</annotation></semantics></math></span>) a generalization of the identity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>∑</mo><mi>ν</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>c</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>b</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mi>u</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>c</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>b</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mi>v</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>dim</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>ν</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>dim</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>μ</mi><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>u</mi><mi>v</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sum\nu(c(b)+u)(c(b)+v)\dim\nu/\dim\mu=(n+1)(n+uv)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ν</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\nu</annotation></semantics></math></span> runs over immediate successors of a Young diagram <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> boxes. Here <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>c</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>b</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">c(b)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the content of a new box <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>b</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The approach is based on the interpretation of a Young diagram as a pair of interlacing sequences, so that analytic techniques may be used to solve combinatorial problems.
