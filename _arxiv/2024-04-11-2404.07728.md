{% raw %}<p>The Stochastic Burgers equation was introduced in [H. van Beijeren, R. Kutner and H. Spohn, Excess noise for driven diffusive systems, PRL, 1985] as a continuous approximation of the fluctuations of the asymmetric simple exclusion process. It is formally given by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi>t</mi></msub><mi>η</mi><mo>=</mo><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mn>2</mn></mfrac><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mi>η</mi><mo>+</mo><mi mathvariant="fraktur">w</mi><mo>⋅</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∇</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>η</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∇</mi><mo>⋅</mo><mi>ξ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\partial_t\eta =\frac{1}{2}\Delta\eta+ \mathfrak w\cdot\nabla(\eta^2) + \nabla\cdot\xi,</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ξ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi</annotation></semantics></math></span> is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional space time white noise and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">w</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak w</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a fixed non-zero vector. In the critical dimension <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d=2</annotation></semantics></math></span> at stationarity, we show that this system exhibits superdiffusve behaviour: more specifically, its bulk diffusion coefficient behaves like <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>t</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mfrac><mn>2</mn><mn>3</mn></mfrac></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\log t)^\frac23</annotation></semantics></math></span>, in a Tauberian sense, up to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\log\log\log t</annotation></semantics></math></span> corrections. This confirms a prediction made in the physics literature and complements [G. Cannizzarro, M. Gubinelli, F. Toninelli, Gaussian Fluctuations for the stochastic Burgers equation in dimension <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d\geq 2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, CMP, 2024], where the same equation was studied in the weak-coupling regime. Furthermore this model can be seen as a continuous analogue to [H.T. Yau, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>t</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mfrac><mn>2</mn><mn>3</mn></mfrac></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\log t)^\frac{2}{3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> law of the two dimensional asymmetric simple exclusion process, Annals of Mathematics, 2004].</p>{% endraw %}
