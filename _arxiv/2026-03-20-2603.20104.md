{% raw %}<p>We present computational results on principal specializations <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{S}_w(1^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of Schubert polynomials, which count reduced pipe dreams and reduced bumpless pipe dreams (RBPD). We find the first counterexample, at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>17</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=17</annotation></semantics></math></span>, to the Merzon-Smirnov conjecture (arXiv:1410.6857) that the maximum of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{S}_w(1^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span> over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is attained at a layered permutation. The simulations suggest that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mi>lim</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow></msub><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi>max</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>w</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow></msub><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>n</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lim_{n \to \infty} \log(\max_{w\in S_n}\mathfrak{S}_w(1^n))/n^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> equals the maximal layered permutations' constant from Morales-Pak-Panova (arXiv:1805.04341). We also explore the random permutation drawn from the distribution proportional to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{S}_w(1^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, revealing permuton-like asymptotics similar to those for Grothendieck polynomials by Morales-Panova-Petrov-Yeliussizov (arXiv:2407.21653). We implement and compare three recurrences for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{S}_w(1^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>: the descent formula (Macdonald), transition formula (Lascoux--Schutzenberger), and cotransition formula (Knutson). For sampling uniformly random RBPDs (whose count is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mo>∑</mo><mrow><mi>w</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow></msub><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sum_{w\in S_n} \mathfrak{S}_w(1^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>), we show that reducedness breaks the sublattice property of the ASM lattice, preventing monotone CFTP and causing false coalescence. We develop an efficient MCMC sampler with macroscopic "droop" updates for connectivity and fast mixing. Our code computes <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">S</mi><mi>w</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mn>1</mn><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{S}_w(1^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span> up to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>∼</mo><mn>20</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\sim 20</annotation></semantics></math></span> and samples random RBPDs up to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>∼</mo><mn>60</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\sim 60</annotation></semantics></math></span> on a personal computer (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>∼</mo><mn>100</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\sim 100</annotation></semantics></math></span> on a cluster).</p>{% endraw %}
