{% raw %}<p>We consider the random matrix ensemble with an external source \[ \frac{1}{Z_n} e^{-n \Tr({1/2}M^2 -AM)} dM \] defined on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\times n</annotation></semantics></math></span> Hermitian matrices, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a diagonal matrix with only two eigenvalues <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>±</mo><mi>a</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pm a</annotation></semantics></math></span> of equal multiplicity. For the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a &gt; 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we establish the universal behavior of local eigenvalue correlations in the limit <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which is known from unitarily invariant random matrix models. Thus, local eigenvalue correlations are expressed in terms of the sine kernel in the bulk and in terms of the Airy kernel at the edge of the spectrum. We use a characterization of the associated multiple Hermite polynomials by a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>3</mn><mo>×</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">3 \times 3</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrix Riemann-Hilbert problem, and the Deift/Zhou steepest descent method to analyze the Riemann-Hilbert problem in the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> limit.</p>{% endraw %}
