{% raw %}<p>In this paper we study the asymptotics (as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>) of the sequences of Laguerre polynomials with varying complex parameters <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> depending on the degree <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. More precisely, we assume that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>α</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mi>n</mi><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha_n = n A_n, </annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mi>lim</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mi>n</mi></msub><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mi>A</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \lim_n A_n=A \in \mathbb{C}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This study has been carried out previously only for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>α</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha_n\in \mathbb{R}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, but complex values of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A</annotation></semantics></math></span> introduce an asymmetry that makes the problem more difficult. The main ingredient of the asymptotic analysis is the right choice of the contour of orthogonality, which requires the analysis of the global structure of trajectories of an associated quadratic differential on the complex plane, which may have an independent interest. While the weak asymptotics is obtained by reduction to the theorem of Gonchar--Rakhmanov--Stahl, the strong asymptotic results are derived via the non-commutative steepest descent analysis based on the Riemann-Hilbert characterization of the Laguerre polynomials.</p>{% endraw %}
