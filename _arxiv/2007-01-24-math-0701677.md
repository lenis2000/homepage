{% raw %}<p>In this letter I shall review my joint results with Vadim Kuznetsov and Evgeny Sklyanin [Indag. Math. 14 (2003), 451-482, math.CA/0306242] on separation of variables for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> Jack polynomials. This approach originated from the work [RIMS Kokyuroku 919 (1995), 27-34, solv-int/9508002] where the integral representations for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> Jack polynomials was derived. Using special polynomial bases I shall obtain a more explicit expression for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> Jack polynomials in terms of generalised hypergeometric functions.</p>{% endraw %}
