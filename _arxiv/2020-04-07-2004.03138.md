{% raw %}<p>The Preisach graph is a directed graph associated with a permutation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi mathvariant="script">S</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho\in{\cal S}_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We give an explicit bijection between its vertices and increasing subsequences of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ρ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho</annotation></semantics></math></span> with the property that the length of a subsequence equals to the degree of nesting of the corresponding vertex inside a hierarchy of cycles and sub-cycles of the graph. As a consequence, the nesting degree of the Preisach graph equals the length of the longest increasing subsequence.</p>{% endraw %}
