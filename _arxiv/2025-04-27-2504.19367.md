{% raw %}<p>We study Lam's reduced random walk in a hyperbolic triangle group, which we view as a random walk in the upper half-plane. We prove that this walk converges almost surely to a point on the extended real line. We devote special attention to the reduced random walk in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>P</mi><mi>G</mi><msub><mi>L</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">PGL_2(\mathbb{Z})</annotation></semantics></math></span> (i.e., the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>3</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2,3,\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span> triangle group). In this case, we provide an explicit formula for the cumulative distribution function of the limit. This formula is written in terms of the interrobang function, a new function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">!</mo><mspace width="-0.38em"/><mo stretchy="false">?</mo><mspace></mspace><mspace width="0.1111em"/><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0.17em"></mo><mtext> ⁣</mtext><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0em">:</mo><mspace width="0.3333em"/><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">!\hspace{-3.8pt}?\colon[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}</annotation></semantics></math></span> that shares several of the remarkable analytic and arithmetic properties of Minkowski's question-mark function.</p>{% endraw %}
