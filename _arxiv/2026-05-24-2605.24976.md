The tilts <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ξ</mi><mi>j</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>θ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi_{j},\theta_{i}</annotation></semantics></math></span> enter only through an oblique projection that multiplies the trace-class kernel <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K</annotation></semantics></math></span> inside the Fredholm determinant; the BOGC operator <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>I</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>K</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A=I-K</annotation></semantics></math></span> constructed from <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>φ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varphi</annotation></semantics></math></span> is unchanged. Baik-Liao-Liu (arXiv:2603.01964) and Liu-Tripathi (arXiv:2604.24747) have recently shown that the same tilted Toeplitz minor admits a contour Fredholm-determinantal representation, in connection with the periodic Totally Asymmetric Simple Exclusion Process (TASEP). In the periodic TASEP application of Baik-Liao-Liu, the formula plays an important role in identifying the periodic KPZ fixed point with general initial data. Our formula is a companion to their Fredholm determinant and readily reduces to the original BOGC identity. The one-sided tilted Toeplitz minor (that is, when all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>θ</mi><mi>i</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\theta_i=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>) admits a bialternant form recovering Schur and Grothendieck polynomials as special cases. A Cauchy-Binet expansion realizes <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>D</mi><mi>N</mi><mrow><mi>ξ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>θ</mi></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_{N}^{\xi,\theta}</annotation></semantics></math></span> as a restricted sum over partitions of products of Jacobi-Trudi type determinants, generalizing Gessel's theorem. In the pure-shift setting this specializes to a skew Schur expansion. Finally, for finite Laurent exponential symbols, we record explicit resolvent-block flow identities and formulate the associated finite-dimensional closure problem. We also illustrate a possible asymptotic application leading to finite-rank perturbations of the Airy kernel.</p>{% endraw %}
