{% raw %}<p>We prove that the SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>κ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_\kappa</annotation></semantics></math></span> loop measure arises naturally from the conformal welding of two <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Liouville quantum gravity (LQG) disks for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>γ</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>=</mo><mi>κ</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>4</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma^2 = \kappa \in (0,4)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The proof relies on our companion work on conformal welding of LQG disks and uses as an essential tool the concept of uniform embedding of LQG surfaces. Combining our result with work of Gwynne and Miller, we get that random quadrangulations decorated by a self-avoiding polygon converge in the scaling limit to the LQG sphere decorated by the SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mrow><mn>8</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_{8/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> loop. Our result is also a key input to recent work of the first and third coauthors on the integrability of the conformal loop ensemble.</p>{% endraw %}
