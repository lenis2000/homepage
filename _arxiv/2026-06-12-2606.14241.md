{% raw %}<p>The asymmetric simple exclusion process (ASEP) is an important particle model with deep connections to orthogonal polynomials. Motivated by this connection, Corteel and Williams introduced the Koornwinder moments <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mi>λ</mi><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span> at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> t=q </annotation></semantics></math></span>, which generalize the moments of Askey--Wilson polynomials. They showed that the partition function of the two-species ASEP is equal to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mi>λ</mi><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span> for a one-row partition <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex"> \lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In this paper, we investigate a conjecture of Rains on the positivity of the minimal numerator of the Koornwinder moment <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mi>λ</mi><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We derive the first explicit formula for this moment, thereby obtaining a precise formulation of the conjecture by determining the minimal denominator of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mi>λ</mi><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We also propose a generalization of the conjecture for the more general Koornwinder moments <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>μ</mi></mrow><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_{\lambda,\mu}</annotation></semantics></math></span> indexed by two partitions at special parameter values. We prove the generalized Rains' conjecture in two special cases: <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>ξ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\xi,q)=(1,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>ξ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\xi,q)=(1,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>ξ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\xi,q)=(1,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we construct a lattice path model and obtain a combinatorial formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>μ</mi></mrow><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_{\lambda,\mu}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in terms of non-intersecting lattice paths. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>ξ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\xi,q)=(1,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we establish an explicit product formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>μ</mi></mrow><mi>Z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M^{Z}_{\lambda,\mu}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and give a combinatorial interpretation using lecture hall tableaux.</p>{% endraw %}
