{% raw %}<p>Using notation inherited from the six-vertex model, we construct diagrams that represent the action of the factorizing <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>F</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrices associated to the finite length XXZ spin-1/2 chain. We prove that these <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>F</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrices factorize the tensor <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>R</mi><mrow><mn>1...</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow><mi>σ</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R^{\sigma}_{1... n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> corresponding with elements of the permutation group. We consider in particular the diagram for the tensor <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>R</mi><mrow><mn>1...</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow><msub><mi>σ</mi><mi>c</mi></msub></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R^{\sigma_c}_{1... n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which cyclically permutes the spin chain. This leads us to a diagrammatic construction of the local spin operators <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>S</mi><mi>i</mi><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0em">±</mo></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_i^{\pm}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>S</mi><mi>i</mi><mi>z</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_i^{z}</annotation></semantics></math></span> in terms of the monodromy matrix operators.</p>{% endraw %}
