{% raw %}<p>Bergeron--Ceballos--Küstner introduced the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Fibonomial coefficients \( \qfibonom{m+n}{n}\), gave a combinatorial interpretation of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Fibonomial coefficients via a weighted path-domino tiling model, and conjectured that these polynomials are unimodal. We prove the conjecture for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\leq3</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=2</annotation></semantics></math></span> case, we give a combinatorial proof of both unimodality and symmetry by defining a nearly symmetric saturated chain decomposition on the set of tilings. For all three cases, we give an algebraic proof. Finally, for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n=3</annotation></semantics></math></span> case, we establish a more general unimodality result for certain products of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-analogs and propose several related conjectures.</p>{% endraw %}
