{% raw %}<p>Vershik and Kerov gave asymptotical bounds for the maximal and the typical dimensions of irreducible representations of symmetric groups <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. It was conjectured by G. Olshanski that the maximal and the typical dimensions of the isotypic components of tensor representations of the symmetric group admit similar asymptotical bounds. The main result of this article is the proof of this conjecture. Consider the natural representation of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi mathvariant="double-struck">C</mi><mi>N</mi></msup><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mo>⊗</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\mathbb{C}^N)^{\otimes n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Its isotypic components are parametrized by Young diagrams with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> cells and at most <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> rows. P. Biane found the limit shape of Young diagrams when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mtext> </mtext><msqrt><mi>n</mi></msqrt><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\rightarrow\infty,\ \sqrt{n}/N\rightarrow c</annotation></semantics></math></span>. By showing that this limit shape is the unique solution to a variational problem, it is proven here, that after scaling, the maximal and the typical dimensions of isotypic components lie between positive constants. A new proof of Biane's limit-shape theorem is obtained.</p>{% endraw %}
