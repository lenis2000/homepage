{% raw %}<p>In a recent letter, new representations were proposed for the pair of sequences (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma,\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span>), as defined formally by Bailey in his famous lemma. Here we extend and prove this result, providing pairs (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma,\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span>) labelled by the Lie algebra A<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_{N-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, two non-negative integers <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a partition <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>, whose parts do not exceed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N-1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our results give rise to what we call a ``higher-level'' Bailey lemma. As an application it is shown how this lemma can be applied to yield general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-series identities, which generalize some well-known results of Andrews and Bressoud.</p>{% endraw %}
