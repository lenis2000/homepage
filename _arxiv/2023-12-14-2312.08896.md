{% raw %}<p>The moments of the real eigenvalues of real Ginibre matrices are investigated from the viewpoint of explicit formulas, differential and difference equations, and large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> expansions. These topics are inter-related. For example, a third order differential equation can be derived for the density of the real eigenvalues, and this can be used to deduce a second order difference equation for the general complex moments <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>p</mi></mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">r</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_{2p}^{\rm r}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The latter are expressed in terms of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>3</mn></msub><msub><mi>F</mi><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_3 F_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> hypergeometric functions, with a simplification to the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>2</mn></msub><msub><mi>F</mi><mn>1</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{}_2 F_1</annotation></semantics></math></span> hypergeometric function possible for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, allowing for the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> expansion of these moments to be obtained. The large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> expansion involves both integer and half integer powers of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The three term recurrence then provides the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> expansion of the full sequence <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><msubsup><mi>M</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>p</mi></mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">r</mi></msubsup><msubsup><mo stretchy="false">}</mo><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{ M_{2p}^{\rm r} \}_{p=0}^\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Fourth and third order linear differential equations are obtained for the moment generating function and for the Stieltjes transform of the real density, respectively, and properties of the large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> expansion of these quantities are determined.</p>{% endraw %}
