{% raw %}<p>We introduce a general framework allowing to apply the theory of regularity structures to discretisations of stochastic PDEs. The approach pursued in this article is that we do not focus on any one specific discretisation procedure. Instead, we assume that we are given a scale <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon &gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a "black box" describing the behaviour of our discretised objects at scales below <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon </annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
