{% raw %}<p>A systematic study of the properties of particle and charge correlation functions in the two-dimensional Coulomb gas confined to a one-dimensional domain is undertaken. Two versions of this system are considered: one in which the positive and negative charges are constrained to alternate in sign along the line, and the other where there is no charge ordering constraint. Both systems undergo a zero-density Kosterlitz-Thouless type transition as the dimensionless coupling <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Γ</mi><mo>:</mo><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>q</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>k</mi><mi>T</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Gamma := q^2 / kT</annotation></semantics></math></span> is varied through <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Γ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Gamma = 2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the charge ordered system we use a perturbation technique to establish an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>r</mi><mn>4</mn></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">O(1/r^4)</annotation></semantics></math></span> decay of the two-body correlations in the high temperature limit. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Γ</mi><mo>→</mo><msup><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Gamma \rightarrow 2^+</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the low-fugacity expansion of the asymptotic charge-charge correlation can be resummed to all orders in the fugacity. The resummation leads to the Kosterlitz renormalization equations.</p>{% endraw %}
