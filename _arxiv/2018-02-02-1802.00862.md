{% raw %}<p>Consider the Aldous Markov chain on the space of rooted binary trees with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> labeled leaves in which at each transition a uniform random leaf is deleted and reattached to a uniform random edge. Now, fix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>≤</mo><mi>k</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1\le k &lt; n</annotation></semantics></math></span> and project the leaf mass onto the subtree spanned by the first <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span> leaves. This yields a binary tree with edge weights that we call a "decorated <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-tree with total mass <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>." We introduce label swapping dynamics for the Aldous chain so that, when it runs in stationarity, the decorated <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-trees evolve as Markov chains themselves, and are projectively consistent over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>≤</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k\le n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The construction of projectively consistent chains is a crucial step in the construction of the Aldous diffusion on continuum trees by the present authors, which is the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\rightarrow \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> continuum analogue of the Aldous chain and will be taken up elsewhere. Some of our results have been generalized to Ford's alpha model trees.</p>{% endraw %}
