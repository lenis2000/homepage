{% raw %}<p>We consider an occupancy scheme in which "balls" are identified with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> points sampled from the standard exponential distribution, while the role of "boxes" is played by the spacings induced by an independent random walk with positive and nonlattice steps. We discuss the asymptotic behavior of five quantities: the index <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>K</mi><mi>n</mi><mo>∗</mo></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_n^*</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the last occupied box, the number <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> of occupied boxes, the number <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_{n,0}</annotation></semantics></math></span> of empty boxes whose index is at most <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>K</mi><mi>n</mi><mo>∗</mo></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_n^*</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the index <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>W</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the first empty box and the number of balls <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>Z</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Z_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the last occupied box. It is shown that the limiting distribution of properly scaled and centered <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>K</mi><mi>n</mi><mo>∗</mo></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_n^*</annotation></semantics></math></span> coincides with that of the number of renewals not exceeding <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\log n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. A similar result is shown for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>W</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> under a side condition that prevents occurrence of very small boxes. The condition also ensures that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_{n,0}</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges in distribution. Limiting results for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>Z</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Z_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> are established under an assumption of regular variation.</p>{% endraw %}
