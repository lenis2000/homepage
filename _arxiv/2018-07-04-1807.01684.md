{% raw %}<p>We set the foundation for a series of works aimed at proving strong relations between uniform random planar maps and Liouville quantum gravity (LQG). Our method relies on a bijective encoding of site-percolated planar triangulations by certain 2D lattice paths. Our bijection parallels in the discrete setting the \emph{mating-of-trees} framework of LQG and Schramm-Loewner evolutions (SLE) introduced by Duplantier, Miller, and Sheffield. Combining these two correspondences allows us to relate uniform site-percolated triangulations to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mrow><mn>8</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{8/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-LQG and SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>6</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_6</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In particular, we establish the convergence of several functionals of the percolation model to continuous random objects defined in terms of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mrow><mn>8</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{8/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-LQG and SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>6</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_6</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For instance, we show that the exploration tree of the percolation converges to a branching SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>6</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_6</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and that the collection of percolation cycles converges to the conformal loop ensemble CLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>6</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_6</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We also prove convergence of counting measure on the pivotal points of the percolation. Our results play an essential role in several other works, including a program for showing convergence of the conformal structure of uniform triangulations and works which study the behavior of random walk on the uniform infinite planar triangulation.</p>{% endraw %}
