We show that the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>th centralizer algebra can be naturally projected onto the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(n-1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>th one, so that one can form the projective limit of the centralizer algebras as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> fixed. The main result of the paper is a precise description of this limit (or stable) centralizer algebra, denoted by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>m</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_m</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We explicitly construct an algebra isomorphism <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>m</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><mi>Z</mi><mo>⊗</mo><msub><mi>Y</mi><mi>m</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_m=Z\otimes Y_m</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Z</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a commutative algebra and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>Y</mi><mi>m</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y_m</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the so-called twisted Yangian associated to the rank <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> classical Lie algebra of type B, C, or D. The algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Z</annotation></semantics></math></span> may be viewed as the algebra of virtual Laplace operators; it is isomorphic to the algebra of polynomials with countably many indeterminates. The twisted Yangian <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>Y</mi><mi>m</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y_m</annotation></semantics></math></span> (and hence the algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>m</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_m</annotation></semantics></math></span>) can be described in terms of a system of generators with quadratic and linear defining relations which are conveniently presented in R-matrix form involving the so-called reflection equation. This extends the earlier work on the type A case by the second author.</p>{% endraw %}
