We attempt to generalize the renormalization procedure which gives the Hopf-Cole solution for the single layer equation and our <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>h</mi><mn>1</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">h_1</annotation></semantics></math></span> (solution to the first layer) coincides with this solution. However, we observe that cancellation of logarithmic divergences that occurs at the first layer does not hold at higher layers and develop explicit combinatorial formulae for them.</p>{% endraw %}
