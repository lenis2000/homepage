{% raw %}<p>Guibert and Linusson introduced the family of doubly alternating Baxter permutations, i.e. Baxter permutations <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma \in S_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, such that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>σ</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma^{-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span> are alternating. They proved that the number of such permutations in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>S</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_{2n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>S</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">S_{2n+1}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the Catalan number <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>C</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In this paper we explore the expected limit shape of such permutations, following the approach by Miner and Pak.</p>{% endraw %}
