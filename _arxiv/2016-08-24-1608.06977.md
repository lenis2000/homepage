{% raw %}<p>In this paper we study the joint distributional convergence of the largest eigenvalues of the sample covariance matrix of a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional time series with iid entries when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges to infinity together with the sample size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We consider only heavy-tailed time series in the sense that the entries satisfy some regular variation condition which ensures that their fourth moment is infinite. In this case, Soshnikov [31, 32] and Auffinger et al. [2] proved the weak convergence of the point processes of the normalized eigenvalues of the sample covariance matrix towards an inhomogeneous Poisson process which implies in turn that the largest eigenvalue converges in distribution to a Fr\'echet distributed random variable. They proved these results under the assumption that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> are proportional to each other. In this paper we show that the aforementioned results remain valid if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span> grows at any polynomial rate. The proofs are different from those in [2, 31, 32]; we employ large deviation techniques to achieve them. The proofs reveal that only the diagonal of the sample covariance matrix is relevant for the asymptotic behavior of the largest eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenvectors which are close to the canonical basis vectors. We also discuss extensions of the results to sample autocovariance matrices.</p>{% endraw %}
