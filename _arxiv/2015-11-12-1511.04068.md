{% raw %}<p>We give bijections between bipolar-oriented (acyclic with unique source and sink) planar maps and certain random walks, which show that the uniformly random bipolar-oriented planar map, decorated by the "peano curve" surrounding the tree of left-most paths to the sink, converges in law with respect to the peanosphere topology to a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mrow><mn>4</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{4/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Liouville quantum gravity surface decorated by an independent Schramm-Loewner evolution with parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>12</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa=12</annotation></semantics></math></span> (i.e., SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>12</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_{12}</annotation></semantics></math></span>). This result is universal in the sense that it holds for bipolar-oriented triangulations, quadrangulations, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>-angulations, and maps in which face sizes are mixed.</p>{% endraw %}
