We prove that the local eigenvalue statistics coincide with the GOE/GUE in the fixed energy sense after time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>≳</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t \gtrsim 1/N</annotation></semantics></math></span> if the density of states of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>V</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V</annotation></semantics></math></span> is bounded above and below down to scales <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>η</mi><mo>≪</mo><mi>t</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\eta \ll t</annotation></semantics></math></span> in a window of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi><mo>≫</mo><msqrt><mi>t</mi></msqrt><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L \gg \sqrt{t}.</annotation></semantics></math></span> Our results imply that fixed energy universality holds for essentially any random matrix ensemble for which averaged energy universality was previously known.
