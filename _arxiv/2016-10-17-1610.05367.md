Asymptotic events studied include a central limit theorem and large deviation result for the number of points in a growing interval <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>λ</mi><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">[0,\lambda]</annotation></semantics></math></span> as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We study these results for the square root of the hard edge process. In this setting many of these behaviors mimic those of the Sine<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>β</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> process.</p>{% endraw %}
