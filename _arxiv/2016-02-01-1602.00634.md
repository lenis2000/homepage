{% raw %}<p>Consider the product <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">GX</annotation></semantics></math></span> of two rectangular complex random matrices coupled by a constant matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Ω</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Omega</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> can be thought to be a Gaussian matrix and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a bi-invariant polynomial ensemble. We prove that the squared singular values form a biorthogonal ensemble in Borodin's sense, and further that for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span> being Gaussian the correlation kernel can be expressed as a double contour integral. When all but finitely many eigenvalues of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">Ω</mi><mrow></mrow></msup><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">Ω</mi><mo lspace="0em" rspace="0em">∗</mo></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Omega^{} \Omega^{*}</annotation></semantics></math></span> are equal, the corresponding correlation kernel is shown to admit a phase transition phenomenon at the hard edge in four different regimes as the coupling matrix changes. Specifically, the four limiting kernels in turn are the Meijer G-kernel for products of two independent Gaussian matrices, a new critical and interpolating kernel, the perturbed Bessel kernel and the finite coupled product kernel associated with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">GX</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the special case that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>X</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X</annotation></semantics></math></span> is also a Gaussian matrix and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Ω</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Omega</annotation></semantics></math></span> is scalar, such a product has been recently investigated by Akemann and Strahov. We also propose a Jacobi-type product and prove the same transition.</p>{% endraw %}
