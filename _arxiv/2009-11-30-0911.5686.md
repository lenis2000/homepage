{% raw %}<p>We study the small dispersion limit for the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>u</mi><mi>t</mi></msub><mo>+</mo><mn>6</mn><mi>u</mi><msub><mi>u</mi><mi>x</mi></msub><mo>+</mo><msup><mi>ϵ</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><msub><mi>u</mi><mrow><mi>x</mi><mi>x</mi><mi>x</mi></mrow></msub><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">u_t+6uu_x+\epsilon^{2}u_{xxx}=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> in a critical scaling regime where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x</annotation></semantics></math></span> approaches the trailing edge of the region where the KdV solution shows oscillatory behavior. Using the Riemann-Hilbert approach, we obtain an asymptotic expansion for the KdV solution in a double scaling limit, which shows that the oscillations degenerate to sharp pulses near the trailing edge. Locally those pulses resemble soliton solutions of the KdV equation.</p>{% endraw %}
