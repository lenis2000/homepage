For every Iwahori Whittaker function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϕ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\phi</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and for every <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>g</mi><mo>∈</mo><mover accent="true"><mi>G</mi><mo stretchy="true">~</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">g\in\widetilde{G}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we evaluate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϕ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>g</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\phi(g)</annotation></semantics></math></span> by recurrence relations over the Weyl group using novel "vector Demazure-Whittaker operators." The general formula and strategy of proof are inspired by ideas appearing in the theory of integrable systems. Specializing to the case of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="bold">G</mi><mo>=</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="bold">G</mi><mi mathvariant="bold">L</mi></mrow><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbf{G} = \mathbf{GL}_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we construct a solvable lattice model of a new type associated with the quantum affine super group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>r</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>n</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}(r|n))</annotation></semantics></math></span> and prove that its partition function equals <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ϕ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>g</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\phi(g)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Remarkably, there is a bijection between the boundary data specifying the partition function and the data determining all values of the Whittaker functions.</p>{% endraw %}
