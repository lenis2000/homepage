{% raw %}<p>Consider an infinite system \[\partial_tu_t(x)=(\mathscr{L}u_t)(x)+ \sigma\bigl(u_t(x)\bigr)\partial_tB_t(x)\] of interacting It\^{o} diffusions, started at a nonnegative deterministic bounded initial profile. We study local and global features of the solution under standard regularity assumptions on the nonlinearity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We will show that, locally in time, the solution behaves as a collection of independent diffusions. We prove also that the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>th moment Lyapunov exponent is frequently of sharp order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>k</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, in contrast to the continuous-space stochastic heat equation whose <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k</annotation></semantics></math></span>th moment Lyapunov exponent can be of sharp order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>k</mi><mn>3</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k^3</annotation></semantics></math></span>. When the underlying walk is transient and the noise level is sufficiently low, we prove also that the solution is a.s. uniformly dissipative provided that the initial profile is in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi><mn>1</mn></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi mathvariant="bold">Z</mi><mi>d</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\ell^1(\mathbf {Z}^d)</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
