{% raw %}<p>We obtain scaling and local limit results for large random Young tableaux of fixed shape <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>λ</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda^0</annotation></semantics></math></span> via the asymptotic analysis of a determinantal point process due to Gorin and Rahman (2019). More precisely, we prove: (1) an explicit description of the limiting surface of a uniform random Young tableau of shape <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>λ</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda^0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, based on solving a complex-valued polynomial equation; (2) a simple criteria to determine if the limiting surface is continuous in the whole domain; (3) and a local limit result in the bulk of a random Poissonized Young tableau of shape <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>λ</mi><mn>0</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda^0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our results have several consequences, for instance: they lead to explicit formulas for the limiting surface of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span>-shaped tableaux, generalizing the results of Pittel and Romik (2007) for rectangular shapes; they imply that the limiting surface for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span>-shaped tableaux is discontinuous for almost-every <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span>-shape; and they give a new one-parameter family of infinite random Young tableaux, constructed from the so-called random infinite bead process.</p>{% endraw %}
