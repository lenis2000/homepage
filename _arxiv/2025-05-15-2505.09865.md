The explicit functional forms of the correlation kernels then imply that the general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-point correlation functions exhibit an asymptotic expansion in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which moreover can be lifted to an asymptotic in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the spacing distributions and their generating function. We use <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Painlev\'e characterisations to show that the functional form of the first correction is related to the leading term via a second derivative. In the case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta = 2</annotation></semantics></math></span> this finding has immediate consequence in interpreting the empirical Riemann zeros spacing distribution at large height, and that of their thinning. Explicit functional forms are used to show that the spectral form factors for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta =1,2</annotation></semantics></math></span> and 4 also admit an asymptotic expansion in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>N</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/N^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Differential relations are identified expressing the first and second correction in terms of the limiting functional form, and evidence is presented that they hold for general <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
