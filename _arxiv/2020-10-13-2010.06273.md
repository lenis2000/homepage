{% raw %}<p>We study the feasible region for consecutive patterns of pattern-avoiding permutations. More precisely, given a family <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal C</annotation></semantics></math></span> of permutations avoiding a fixed set of patterns, we consider the limit of proportions of consecutive patterns on large permutations of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal C</annotation></semantics></math></span>. These limits form a region, which we call the consecutive patterns feasible region for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal C</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We determine the dimension of the consecutive patterns feasible region for all families <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal C</annotation></semantics></math></span> closed either for the direct sum or the skew sum. These families include for instance the ones avoiding a single pattern and all substitution-closed classes. We further show that these regions are always convex and we conjecture that they are always polytopes. We prove this conjecture when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="script">C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal C</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the family of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau</annotation></semantics></math></span>-avoiding permutations, with either <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau</annotation></semantics></math></span> of size three or <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>τ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tau</annotation></semantics></math></span> a monotone pattern. Furthermore, in these cases we give a full description of the vertices of these polytopes via cycle polytopes. Along the way, we discuss connections of this work with the problem of packing patterns in pattern-avoiding permutations and to the study of local limits for pattern-avoiding permutations.</p>{% endraw %}
