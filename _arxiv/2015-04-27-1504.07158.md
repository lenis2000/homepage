{% raw %}<p>We present a model of anomalous diffusion consisting of an ensemble of particles undergoing homogeneous Brownian motion except for confinement by randomly placed reflecting boundaries. For power-law distributed compartment sizes, we calculate exact and asymptotic values of the ensemble averaged mean squared displacement and find that it increases subdiffusively, as either a power or the logarithm of time. Numerical simulations show that the probability density function of the displacement is non-Gaussian. We discuss the relevance of the model for the analysis of single-particle tracking experiments and its relation to other sources of subdiffusion. In particular we discuss an intimate connection with diffusion on percolation processes.</p>{% endraw %}
