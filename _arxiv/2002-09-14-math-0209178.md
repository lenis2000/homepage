{% raw %}<p>Let G be a random subgraph of the n-cube where each edge appears randomly and independently with probability p. We prove that the largest eigenvalue of the adjacency matrix of G is almost surely \lambda_1(G)= (1+o(1)) max(\Delta^{1/2}(G),np), where \Delta(G) is the maximum degree of G and o(1) term tends to zero as max (\Delta^{1/2}(G), np) tends to infinity.</p>{% endraw %}
