{% raw %}<p>We obtain orthogonal polynomial self-duality functions for multi-species version of the symmetric exclusion process (SEP<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2j)</annotation></semantics></math></span>) and the independent random walker process (IRW) on a finite undirected graph. In each process, we have <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n&gt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span> species of particles. In addition, we allow up to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2j</annotation></semantics></math></span> particles to occupy each site in the multi-species SEP<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2j)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The duality functions for the multi-species SEP<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2j)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the multi-species IRW come from unitary intertwiners between different <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>∗</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">*</annotation></semantics></math></span>-representations of the special linear Lie algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">s</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{sl}_{n+1}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the Heisenberg Lie algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="fraktur">h</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{h}_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, respectively. The analysis leads to multivariate Krawtchouk polynomials as orthogonal duality functions for the multi-species SEP<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2j)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and homogeneous products of Charlier polynomials as orthogonal duality functions for the multi-species IRW.</p>{% endraw %}
