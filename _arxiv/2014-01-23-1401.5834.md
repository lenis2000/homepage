This is an analog of a continuous-time non-commutative random walk on the group von Neumann algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>v</mi><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">vN(U(N))</annotation></semantics></math></span> constructed in [15], and is a variant of discrete-time non-commutative random walks on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U(\mathfrak{gl}_N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> [2,9]. It is also shown that when restricting to the Gelfand-Tsetlin subalgebra of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>U</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U(\mathfrak{gl}_N),</annotation></semantics></math></span> the non-commutative random walk matches a (2+1)-dimensional random surface model introduced in [7]. As an application, it is then proved that the moments converge to an explicit Gaussian field along time-like paths. Combining with [7] which showed convergence to the Gaussian free field along space-like paths, this computes the entire three-dimensional Gaussian field. In particular, it matches a Gaussian field from eigenvalues of random matrices [5].</p>{% endraw %}
