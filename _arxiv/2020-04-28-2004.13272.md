{% raw %}<p>In this paper we consider the existence and uniqueness of pure states with some fixed slope <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><msup><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(s, t) \in [0, 1]^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> for a general ferroelectric six-vertex model. First, we show there is an open subset <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">H</mi><mo>⊂</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><msup><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{H} \subset [0, 1]^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which is parameterized by the region between two explicit hyperbolas, such that there is no pure state for the ferroelectric six-vertex model of any slope <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="fraktur">H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(s, t) \in \mathfrak{H}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Second, we show that there is a unique pure state for this model of any slope <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>s</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>t</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(s, t)</annotation></semantics></math></span> on the boundary <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∂</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\partial \mathfrak{H}</annotation></semantics></math></span> of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathfrak{H}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. These results confirm predictions of Bukman-Shore from 1995.</p>{% endraw %}
