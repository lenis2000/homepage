{% raw %}<p>A growing random graph is constructed by successively sampling without replacement an element from the pool of virtual vertices and edges. At start of the process the pool contains <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> virtual vertices and no edges. Each time a vertex is sampled and occupied, the edges linking the vertex to previously occupied vertices are added to the pool of virtual elements. We focus on the edge-counting at times when the graph has <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≤</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\leq N</annotation></semantics></math></span> occupied vertices. Two different Poisson limits are identified for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≍</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\asymp N^{1/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>−</mo><mi>n</mi><mo>≍</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N-n\asymp 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. For the bulk of the process, when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>≍</mo><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\asymp N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the scaled number of edges is shown to fluctuate about a deterministic curve, with fluctuations being of the order of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mn>3</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N^{3/2}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and approximable by a Gaussian bridge.</p>{% endraw %}
