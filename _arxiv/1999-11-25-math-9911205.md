{% raw %}<p>We consider a one-dimensional totally asymmetric nearest-neighbor zero-range process with site-dependent jump-rates - an environment. For each environment p we prove that the set of all invariant measures is the convex hull of a set of product measures with geometric marginals. As a consequence we show that for environments p satisfying certain asymptotic property, there are no invariant measures concentrating on configurations with critical density bigger than <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ρ</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>p</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho^*(p)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, a critical value. If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ρ</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>p</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho^*(p)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is finite we say that there is phase-transition on the density. In this case we prove that if the initial configuration has asymptotic density strictly above <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>ρ</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>p</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\rho^*(p)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, then the process converges to the maximal invariant measure.</p>{% endraw %}
