{% raw %}<p>We consider disordered pinning models, when the return time distribution of the underlying renewal process has a polynomial tail with exponent <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha \in (1/2,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This corresponds to a regime where disorder is known to be relevant, i.e. to change the critical exponent of the localization transition and to induce a non-trivial shift of the critical point. We show that the free energy and critical curve have an explicit universal asymptotic behavior in the weak coupling regime, depending only on the tail of the return time distribution and not on finer details of the models. This is obtained comparing the partition functions with corresponding continuum quantities, through coarse-graining techniques.</p>{% endraw %}
