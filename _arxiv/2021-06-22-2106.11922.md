{% raw %}<p>Iterating the skew RSK correspondence discovered by Sagan and Stanley in the late '80s, we define a deterministic dynamics on the space of pairs of skew Young tableaux <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>P</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(P,Q)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We find that this skew RSK dynamics displays conservation laws which, in the picture of Viennot's shadow line construction, identify generalizations of Greene invariants. The introduction of a novel realization of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th Kashiwara operators reveals that the skew RSK dynamics possesses symmetries induced by an affine bicrystal structure, which, combined with connectedness properties of Demazure crystals, leads to its linearization. Studying asymptotic evolution of the dynamics started from a pair of skew tableaux <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>P</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(P,Q)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we discover a new bijection <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Υ</mi><mo>:</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>P</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>Q</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>↦</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>V</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>W</mi><mo separator="true">;</mo><mi>κ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ν</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Upsilon : (P,Q) \mapsto (V,W; \kappa, \nu)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Here <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>V</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>W</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(V,W)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a pair of vertically strict tableaux, i.e., column strict filling of Young diagrams with no condition on rows, with shape prescribed by the Greene invariant, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa</annotation></semantics></math></span> is an array of non-negative weights and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ν</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\nu</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a partition. An application of this construction is the first bijective proof of Cauchy and Littlewood identities involving <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials. New identities relating sums of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker and Schur polynomials are also presented.</p>{% endraw %}
