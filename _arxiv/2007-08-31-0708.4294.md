{% raw %}<p>This paper explores large sample properties of the two-parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\alpha,\theta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> Poisson--Dirichlet Process in two contexts. In a Bayesian context of estimating an unknown probability measure, viewing this process as a natural extension of the Dirichlet process, we explore the consistency and weak convergence of the the two-parameter Poisson--Dirichlet posterior process. We also establish the weak convergence of properly centered two-parameter Poisson--Dirichlet processes for large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>θ</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>n</mi><mi>α</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\theta+n\alpha.</annotation></semantics></math></span> This latter result complements large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>θ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\theta</annotation></semantics></math></span> results for the Dirichlet process and Poisson--Dirichlet sequences, and complements a recent result on large deviation principles for the two-parameter Poisson--Dirichlet process. A crucial component of our results is the use of distributional identities that may be useful in other contexts.</p>{% endraw %}
