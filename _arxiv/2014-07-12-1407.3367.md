{% raw %}<p>We study a new process, which we call ASEP<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(q,j)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where particles move asymmetrically on a one-dimensional integer lattice with a bias determined by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q\in (0,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and where at most <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo>∈</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2j\in\mathbb{N}</annotation></semantics></math></span> particles per site are allowed. The process is constructed from a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mi>j</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(2j+1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional representation of a quantum Hamiltonian with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">s</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)</annotation></semantics></math></span> invariance by applying a suitable ground-state transformation. After showing basic properties of the process ASEP<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>j</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(q,j)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we prove self-duality with several self-duality functions constructed from the symmetries of the quantum Hamiltonian. By making use of the self-duality property we compute the first <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-exponential moment of the current for step initial conditions (both a shock or a rarefaction fan) as well as when the process is started from an homogeneous product measure.</p>{% endraw %}
