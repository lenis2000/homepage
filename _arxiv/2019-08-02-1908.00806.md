{% raw %}<p>We propose solutions of the quantum Q-systems of types <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>B</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B_N,C_N,D_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> in terms of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-difference operators, generalizing our previous construction for the Q-system of type <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>A</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The difference operators are interpreted as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker limits of discrete time evolutions of Macdonald-van Diejen type operators. We conjecture that these new operators act as raising and lowering operators for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker functions, which are special cases of graded characters of fusion products of KR-modules.</p>{% endraw %}
