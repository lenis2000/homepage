{% raw %}<p>Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W</annotation></semantics></math></span> be a finite Weyl group and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mi>W</mi><mo>^</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\hat{W}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> be the corresponding affine Weyl group. We show that a large element in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mi>W</mi><mo>^</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\hat{W}}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, randomly generated by (reduced) multiplication by simple generators, almost surely has one of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>W</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">|W|</annotation></semantics></math></span>-specific shapes. Equivalently, a reduced random walk in the regions of the affine Coxeter arrangement asymptotically approaches one of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>W</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">|W|</annotation></semantics></math></span>-many directions. The coordinates of this direction, together with the probabilities of each direction can be calculated via a Markov chain on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>W</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">W</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our results, applied to type <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mover accent="true"><mi>A</mi><mo>~</mo></mover><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\tilde{A}_{n-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, show that a large random <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>-core obtained from the natural growth process has a limiting shape which is a piecewise-linear graph. In this case, our random process is a periodic analogue of TASEP, and our limiting shapes can be compared with Rost's theorem on the limiting shape of TASEP.</p>{% endraw %}
