{% raw %}<p>In a recent paper, Griffin, Ono and Warnaar present a framework for Rogers-Ramanujan type identities using Hall-Littlewood polynomials to arrive at expressions of the form \[\sum_{\lambda : \lambda_1 \leq m} q^{a|\lambda|}P_{2\lambda}(1,q,q^2,\ldots ; q^{n}) = \text{"Infinite product modular function"}\] for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a = 1,2</annotation></semantics></math></span> and any positive integers <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. A recent paper of Rains and Warnaar presents further Rogers-Ramanujan type identities involving sums of terms <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>q</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>λ</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup><msub><mi>P</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>q</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><msup><mi>q</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo><mo separator="true">;</mo><msup><mi>q</mi><mi>n</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q^{|\lambda|/2}P_{\lambda}(1,q,q^2,\ldots;q^n)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. It is natural to attempt to reformulate these various identities to match the well-known Andrews-Gordon identities they generalize. Here, we find combinatorial formulas to replace the Hall-Littlewood polynomials and arrive at such expressions.</p>{% endraw %}
