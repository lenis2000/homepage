{% raw %}<p>The free-fermion point refers to a <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">GL</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>2</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>×</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">GL</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\operatorname{GL}(2)\times\operatorname{GL}(1)</annotation></semantics></math></span> parametrized Yang-Baxter equation within the six-vertex model. It has been known for a long time that this is connected with the quantum group <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\mathfrak{gl}(1|1))</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We demonstrate that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>-matrices from the finite quantum superalgebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\mathfrak{gl}(1|1))</annotation></semantics></math></span> produce a dense subset of the free-fermionic Yang-Baxter equations of the six-vertex model, matching those of the prime, simple modules in the affine quantum superalgebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mover accent="true"><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="true">^</mo></mover><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{gl}}(1|1))</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Either of these quantum groups can be used to generate the full free-fermion point, and we discuss them both. Our discussion includes 6 families of six-vertex models used by Brubaker, Bump, and Friedberg in connection with Tokuyama's theorem, a deformation of the Weyl character formula. Thus our work gives quantum group interpretations for those models, known informally as Tokuyama ice.</p>{% endraw %}
