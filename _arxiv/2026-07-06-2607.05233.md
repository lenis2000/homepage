{% raw %}<p>We prove that the number of lozenge tilings of a certain triangular region <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="script">T</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{T}_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is given by the formula \[T_n=\prod_{\substack{1\leq a<b\leq 3n+2\\(a,b)\not=(n+1,2n+2)}}\left|1+ζ^a+ζ^b\right|^{1/3},\] where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ζ</mi><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>e</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>π</mi><mi>i</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>3</mn><mi>n</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>3</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\zeta=e^{2\pi i/(3n+3)}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This answers a question of Ciucu and Krattenthaler, both by finding the exact formula and by explaining why <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> has many prime factors. The proof reduces the lozenge tiling enumeration problem to evaluating the determinant of the bipartite adjacency matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>M</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> of the dual graph of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="script">T</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{T}_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and then evaluates this determinant by diagonalising <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>M</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
