{% raw %}<p>We show that in the continuum limit watersheds dividing drainage basins are Schramm-Loewner Evolution (SLE) curves, being described by one single parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Several numerical evaluations are applied to ascertain this. All calculations are consistent with SLE<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>κ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_\kappa</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1.734</mn><mo>±</mo><mn>0.005</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa=1.734\pm0.005</annotation></semantics></math></span>, being the only known physical example of an SLE with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>κ</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\kappa&lt;2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This lies outside the well-known duality conjecture, bringing up new questions regarding the existence and reversibility of dual models. Furthermore it constitutes a strong indication for conformal invariance in random landscapes and suggests that watersheds likely correspond to a logarithmic Conformal Field Theory (CFT) with central charge <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>c</mi><mo>≈</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>7</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">c\approx-7/2</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
