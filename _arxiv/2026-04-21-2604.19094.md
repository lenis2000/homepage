{% raw %}<p>Linek's 1989 problem asks whether the numbers of independent sets of trees avoid infinitely many positive integers. We show that the set of natural numbers realized as the number of independent sets of a tree has a lower growth exponent of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0.1966</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0.1966</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We further prove that the set of positive integers representable by connected planar graphs has asymptotic density one. Lastly, we establish a phase transition: the number of independent sets of graphs with fewer than <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>V</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d|V|</annotation></semantics></math></span> edges for any <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d&lt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span> is contained in a set of density zero, whereas, following Shkredov's recent breakthrough on Zaremba's conjecture in continued fraction theory, there exists a constant <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D</annotation></semantics></math></span> such that the number of independent sets of graphs with at most <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>D</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>V</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D|V|</annotation></semantics></math></span> edges covers all positive integers.</p>{% endraw %}
