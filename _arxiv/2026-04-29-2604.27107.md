For given <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>u</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>v</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>w</mi><mo>∈</mo><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">u,v,w\in S_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> and integer <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the stretched Schubert coefficients are defined as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>f</mi><mrow><mi>u</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>v</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>w</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>:</mo><mo>=</mo><msubsup><mi>c</mi><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>∗</mo><mi>u</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>N</mi><mo>∗</mo><mi>v</mi></mrow><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>∗</mo><mi>w</mi></mrow></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f_{u,v,w}(N):=c_{N*u,N*v}^{N*w}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our main result is that the function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>f</mi><mrow><mi>u</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>v</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>w</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f_{u,v,w}(N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is eventually quasi-polynomial. This proves Kirillov's conjecture (2004), that the generating function for the sequence <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><msub><mi>f</mi><mrow><mi>u</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>v</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>w</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{f_{u,v,w}(N)\}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is rational. For the proof, we use combinatorics of pipe dreams to show that Schubert coefficients are given as an alternating sum of the numbers of integer points in certain polytopes. These polytopes behave nicely under stretching, and we use Ehrhart theory to obtain the result. As a consequence of the proof, we also present new counterexamples to the saturation conjecture for Schubert coefficients, and give computational applications.</p>{% endraw %}
