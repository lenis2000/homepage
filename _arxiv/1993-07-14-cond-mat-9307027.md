{% raw %}<p>We study analytically a model where particles with a hard-core repulsion diffuse on a finite one-dimensional lattice with space-dependent, asymmetric hopping rates. The system dynamics are given by the \mbox{U<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mi>q</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_{q}</annotation></semantics></math></span>[SU(2)]}-symmetric Hamiltonian of a generalized anisotropic Heisenberg antiferromagnet. Exploiting this symmetry we derive exact expressions for various correlation functions. We discuss the density profile and the two-point function and compute the correlation length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ξ</mi><mi>s</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi_s</annotation></semantics></math></span> as well as the correlation time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ξ</mi><mi>t</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi_t</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The dynamics of the density and the correlations are shown to be governed by the energy gaps of a one-particle system. For large systems <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ξ</mi><mi>s</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi_s</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ξ</mi><mi>t</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi_t</annotation></semantics></math></span> depend only on the asymmetry. For small asymmetry one finds <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>ξ</mi><mi>t</mi></msub><mo>∼</mo><msubsup><mi>ξ</mi><mi>s</mi><mn>2</mn></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\xi_t \sim \xi_s^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> indicating a dynamical exponent <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>z</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">z=2</annotation></semantics></math></span> as for symmetric diffusion.</p>{% endraw %}
