They are indexed by a pair of positive real numbers <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>p</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>r</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(p,r)</annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p&gt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>r</mi><mo>≤</mo><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0 &lt; r \le p</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and form the moments of a probability density function.
