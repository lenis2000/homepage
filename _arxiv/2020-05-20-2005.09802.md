{% raw %}<p>This paper studies the asymptotic distribution of descents <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">des</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>w</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\des(w)</annotation></semantics></math></span> in a permutation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>w</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">w</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and its inverse, distributed according to the Mallows measure. The Mallows measure is a non-uniform probability measure on permutations introduced to study ranked data. Under this measure, permutations are weighted according to the number of inversions they contain, with the weighting controlled by a parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The main results are a Berry-Esseen theorem for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">des</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>w</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">des</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>w</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\des(w)+\des(w^{-1})</annotation></semantics></math></span> as well as a joint central limit theorem for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">des</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>w</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">des</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>w</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\des(w),\des(w^{-1}))</annotation></semantics></math></span> to a bivariate normal with a non-trivial correlation depending on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The proof uses Stein's method with size-bias coupling along with a regenerative process associated to the Mallows measure.</p>{% endraw %}
