{% raw %}<p>We study a planar Coulomb gas confined to a sufficiently smooth Jordan arc <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span> in the complex plane, at inverse temperature <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta &gt; 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Let \[\bar{Z}_{n}^\beta(\gamma) = Z_{n}^\beta(\gamma)/\left(2 \textrm{cap}(\gamma)\right)^{\beta n^2/2+(1-\beta/2)n}\] be the normalized partition function. We compute the free energy as the number of particles tends to infinity, including the constant term: \[ \lim_{n\to \infty}\log \frac{\bar{Z}_{n}^\beta(\gamma)}{\bar{Z}_{n}^\beta([-1,1])} = \frac{1}{24}J^A(\gamma) + \frac{1}{8} \left( \sqrt{ \frac{\beta}{2} } - \sqrt{ \frac{2}{\beta}} \right)^2 J^{F}(\gamma). \] Here <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mover accent="true"><mi>Z</mi><mo>ˉ</mo></mover><mi>n</mi><mi>β</mi></msubsup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\bar{Z}_{n}^\beta([-1,1])</annotation></semantics></math></span> is an explicit Selberg integral, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>J</mi><mi>A</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>γ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J^A(\gamma)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is half the Loewner energy of a certain Jordan curve associated to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span> plus a covariance term, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>J</mi><mi>F</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>γ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J^F(\gamma)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the Fekete energy, related to the zero temperature limit of the model. We also prove an asymptotic formula for the Laplace transform of linear statistics for sufficiently regular test functions. As a consequence, the centered empirical measure converges to a Gaussian field with explicit asymptotic mean, and asymptotic variance given by the Dirichlet energy of the bounded harmonic extension of the test function outside of the arc. A key tool in our analysis is the arc-Grunsky operator <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B</annotation></semantics></math></span> associated to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span>, reminiscent to but different from the classical Grunsky operator. We derive several basic properties the arc-Grunsky operator, including an estimate analogous to the strengthened Grunsky inequality and the relation to the Dirichlet integral. In our proofs <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>J</mi><mi>A</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>γ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">J^A(\gamma)</annotation></semantics></math></span> arises from the Fredholm determinant <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>det</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>I</mi><mo>+</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\det(I+B)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a substantial part of the paper is devoted to relating this to the Loewner energy.</p>{% endraw %}
