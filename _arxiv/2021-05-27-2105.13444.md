{% raw %}<p>Here we consider the image of the principal minor map of symmetric matrices over an arbitrary unique factorization domain <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>. By exploiting a connection with symmetric determinantal representations, we characterize the image of the principal minor map through the condition that certain polynomials coming from so-called Rayleigh differences are squares in the polynomial ring over <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In almost all cases, one can characterize the image of the principal minor map using the orbit of Cayley's hyperdeterminant under the action of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>S</mi><msub><mi>L</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>R</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mi>n</mi></msup><mo>⋊</mo><msub><mi>S</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(SL_2(R))^{n} \rtimes S_{n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Over the complex numbers, this recovers a characterization of Oeding from 2011, and over the reals, the orbit of a single additional quadratic inequality suffices to cut out the image. Applications to other symmetric determinantal representations are also discussed.</p>{% endraw %}
