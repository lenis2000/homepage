{% raw %}<p>We study joint estimation of the inverse temperature and magnetization parameters <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>β</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(\beta,B)</annotation></semantics></math></span> of an Ising model with a non-negative coupling matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\times n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, given one sample from the Ising model. We give a general bound on the rate of consistency of the bi-variate pseudolikelihood estimator. Using this, we show that estimation at rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{-1/2}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is always possible if <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the adjacency matrix of a bounded degree graph. If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_n</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the scaled adjacency matrix of a graph whose average degree goes to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>+</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">+\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the situation is a bit more delicate. In this case estimation at rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{-1/2}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is still possible if the graph is not regular (in an asymptotic sense). Finally, we show that consistent estimation of both parameters is impossible if the graph is Erd\"os-Renyi with parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> free of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span>, thus confirming that estimation is harder on approximately regular graphs with large degree.</p>{% endraw %}
