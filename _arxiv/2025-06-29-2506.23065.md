For fixed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we determine the decay rate of the spatial covariance function, showing that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">C</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">v</mi></mrow><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mi>h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>h</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">]</mo><mo>∼</mo><mfrac><mi>t</mi><mi>x</mi></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\rm Cov}[h(t,x),h(t,0)]\sim \frac{t}{x}</annotation></semantics></math></span> as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
