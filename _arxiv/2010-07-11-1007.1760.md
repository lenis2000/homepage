{% raw %}<p>We consider the factorization of permutations into bandwidth 1 permutations, which are products of mutually nonadjacent simple transpositions. We exhibit an upper bound on the minimal number of such factors and thus prove a conjecture of Gilbert Strang: a banded permutation of bandwidth <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>w</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">w</annotation></semantics></math></span> can be represented as the product of at most <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>w</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2w-1</annotation></semantics></math></span> permutations of bandwidth 1. An analogous result holds also for infinite and cyclically banded permutations.</p>{% endraw %}
