{% raw %}<p>Our work deals with symmetric rational functions and probabilistic models based on the fully inhomogeneous six vertex (ice type) model satisfying the free fermion condition. Two families of symmetric rational functions <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>F</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F_\lambda,G_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span> are defined as certain partition functions of the six vertex model, with variables corresponding to row rapidities, and the labeling signatures <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo>=</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>λ</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo>≥</mo><mo>…</mo><mo>≥</mo><msub><mi>λ</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>∈</mo><msup><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mi>N</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda=(\lambda_1\ge \ldots\ge \lambda_N)\in \mathbb{Z}^N</annotation></semantics></math></span> encoding boundary conditions. These symmetric functions generalize Schur symmetric polynomials, as well as some of their variations, such as factorial and supersymmetric Schur polynomials. Cauchy type summation identities for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>F</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F_\lambda,G_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span> and their skew counterparts follow from the Yang-Baxter equation. Using algebraic Bethe Ansatz, we obtain a double alternant type formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>F</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span> and a Sergeev-Pragacz type formula for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>G</mi><mi>λ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G_\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In the spirit of the theory of Schur processes, we define probability measures on sequences of signatures with probability weights proportional to products of our symmetric functions. We show that these measures can be viewed as determinantal point processes, and we express their correlation kernels in a double contour integral form. We present two proofs: The first is a direct computation of Eynard-Mehta type, and the second uses non-standard, inhomogeneous versions of fermionic operators in a Fock space coming from the algebraic Bethe Ansatz for the six vertex model. We also interpret our determinantal processes as random domino tilings of a half-strip with inhomogeneous domino weights. In the bulk, we show that the lattice asymptotic behavior of such domino tilings is described by a new determinantal point process on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{Z}^{2}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which can be viewed as an doubly-inhomogeneous generalization of the extended discrete sine process.</p>{% endraw %}
