{% raw %}<p>We consider the adjacency matrices of sparse random graphs from the Chung-Lu model, where edges are added independently between the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> vertices with varying probabilities <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>p</mi><mrow><mi>i</mi><mi>j</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p_{ij}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The rank of the matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>p</mi><mrow><mi>i</mi><mi>j</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(p_{ij})</annotation></semantics></math></span> is some fixed positive integer. We prove that the distribution of eigenvalues is given by the solution of a functional self-consistent equation. We prove a local law down to the optimal scale and prove bulk universality. The results are parallel to \cite{Erdos2013b} and \cite{Landon2015}.</p>{% endraw %}
