{% raw %}<p>We consider a stochastic heat conduction model for solids composed by N interacting atoms. The system is in contact with two heat baths at different temperature <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The bulk dynamics conserve two quantities: the energy and the deformation between atoms. If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></msub><mo mathvariant="normal">≠</mo><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_\ell \neq T_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>, a heat flux takes place in the system. For large <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the system adopts a linear temperature profile between <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">ℓ</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_\ell</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>T</mi><mi>r</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T_r</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We establish the hydrodynamic limit for the two conserved quantities. We introduce the fluctuations field of the energy and of the deformation in the non-equilibrium steady state. As <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> goes to infinity, we show that this field converges to a Gaussian field and we compute the limiting covariance matrix. The main contribution of the paper is the study of large deviations for the temperature profile in the non-equilibrium stationary state. A variational formula for the rate function is derived following the recent macroscopic fluctuation theory of Bertini et al.</p>{% endraw %}
