{% raw %}<p>We study a two-component asymmetric simple exclusion process (ASEP) that is equivalent to the ASEP with second-class particles. We prove self-duality with respect to a family of duality functions which are shown to arise from the reversible measures of the process and the symmetry of the generator under the quantum algebra <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>U</mi><mi>q</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><msub><mrow><mi mathvariant="fraktur">g</mi><mi mathvariant="fraktur">l</mi></mrow><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">]</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">U_q[\mathfrak{gl}_3]</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We construct all invariant measures in explicit form and discuss some of their properties. We also prove a sum rule for the duality functions.</p>{% endraw %}
