{% raw %}<p>L\'evy matrices are symmetric random matrices whose entry distributions lie in the domain of attraction of an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>-stable law. For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha &lt; 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, predictions from the physics literature suggest that high-dimensional L\'{e}vy matrices should display the following phase transition at a point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_{\mathrm{mob}}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Eigenvectors corresponding to eigenvalues in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(-E_{\mathrm{mob}},E_{\mathrm{mob}})</annotation></semantics></math></span> should be delocalized, while eigenvectors corresponding to eigenvalues outside of this interval should be localized. Further, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_{\mathrm{mob}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is given by the (presumably unique) positive solution to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>E</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>α</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda(E,\alpha) =1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda</annotation></semantics></math></span> is an explicit function of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>E</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We prove the following results about high-dimensional L\'{e}vy matrices. (1) If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>E</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>α</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda(E,\alpha) &gt; 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> then eigenvectors with eigenvalues near <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>E</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E</annotation></semantics></math></span> are delocalized. (2) If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>E</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E</annotation></semantics></math></span> is in the connected components of the set <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo fence="false" stretchy="true" minsize="1.2em" maxsize="1.2em">{</mo><mi>x</mi><mo>:</mo><mi>λ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>α</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn><mo fence="false" stretchy="true" minsize="1.2em" maxsize="1.2em">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\big\{ x : \lambda(x,\alpha) &lt; 1 \big\}</annotation></semantics></math></span> containing <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>±</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\pm \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>, then eigenvectors with eigenvalues near <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>E</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E</annotation></semantics></math></span> are localized. (3) For <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> sufficiently near <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0</annotation></semantics></math></span> or <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, there is a unique positive solution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>E</mi><mo>=</mo><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E = E_{\mathrm{mob}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>λ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>E</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>α</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda(E,\alpha) = 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, demonstrating the existence of a (unique) phase transition. (a) If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> is close to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, then <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_{\mathrm{mob}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> scales approximately as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>α</mi><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>α</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">|\log \alpha|^{-2/\alpha}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. (b) If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> is close to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, then <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">m</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">o</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">b</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_{\mathrm{mob}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> scales as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><mi>α</mi><msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(1-\alpha)^{-1}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our proofs proceed through an analysis of the local weak limit of a L\'{e}vy matrix, given by a certain infinite-dimensional, heavy-tailed operator on the Poisson weighted infinite tree.</p>{% endraw %}
