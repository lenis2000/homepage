{% raw %}<p>In this paper we introduce a procedure that, given a solution to the Yang-Baxter equation as input, produces a stochastic (or Markovian) solution to (a possibly dynamical version of) the Yang-Baxter equation. We then apply this "stochasticization procedure" to obtain three new, stochastic solutions to several different forms of the Yang-Baxter equation. The first is a stochastic, elliptic solution to the dynamical Yang-Baxter equation; the second is a stochastic, higher rank solution to the dynamical Yang-Baxter equation; and the third is a stochastic solution to a dynamical variant of the tetrahedron equation.</p>{% endraw %}
