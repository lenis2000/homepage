{% raw %}<p>For biased random walk on the infinite cluster in supercritical i.i.d.\ percolation on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">Z</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Z^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where the bias of the walk is quantified by a parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&gt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, it has been conjectured (and partly proved) that there exists a critical value <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>c</mi></msub><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta_c&gt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span> such that the walk has positive speed when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>c</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&lt;\beta_c</annotation></semantics></math></span> and speed zero when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>c</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&gt;\beta_c</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In this paper, biased random walk on the infinite cluster of a certain translation invariant percolation process on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">Z</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Z^2</annotation></semantics></math></span> is considered. The example is shown to exhibit the opposite behavior to what is expected for i.i.d.\ percolation, in the sense that it has a critical value <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>c</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta_c</annotation></semantics></math></span> such that, for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>c</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&lt;\beta_c</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the random walk has speed zero, while, for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><msub><mi>β</mi><mi>c</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&gt;\beta_c</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the speed is positive. Hence the monotonicity in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> that is part of the conjecture for i.i.d.\ percolation cannot be extended to general translation invariant percolation processes.</p>{% endraw %}
