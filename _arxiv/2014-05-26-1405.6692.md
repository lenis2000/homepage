{% raw %}<p>In this paper we show the strong existence and the pathwise uniqueness of an infinite-dimensional Stochastic Differential Equation (SDE) corresponding to the bulk limit of Dyson's Brownian Motion (DBM), for all <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\geq 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our construction applies to an explicit and general class of initial conditions, including the lattice configuration <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><msub><mi>x</mi><mi>i</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">}</mo><mo>=</mo><mi mathvariant="double-struck">Z</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{x_i\}=\mathbb{Z}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the sine process. We further show the convergence of the finite to infinite-dimensional SDE. This convergence concludes the determinantal formula of Katori and Tanemura (2010) for the solution of this SDE at <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta=2</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
