{% raw %}<p>We investigate the high-temperature behavior of the directed polymer model in dimension <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1+2</annotation></semantics></math></span>. More precisely we study the difference <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Δ</mi><mi mathvariant="monospace">F</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>β</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Delta \mathtt{F}(\beta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> between the quenched and annealed free energies for small values of the inverse temperature <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This quantity is associated to localization properties of the polymer trajectories, and is related to the overlap fraction of two replicas. Adapting recent techniques developed by the authors in the context of the disordered pinning model (Berger and Lacoin, arXiv:1503.07315 [math-ph]), we identify the sharp asymptotic high temperature behavior \[\lim_{\beta\to 0} \, \beta^2 \log \Delta \mathtt{F}(\beta) = -\pi \, .\]</p>{% endraw %}
