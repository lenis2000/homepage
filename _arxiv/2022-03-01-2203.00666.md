{% raw %}<p>We consider the Cole-Hopf solution of the (1+1)-dimensional KPZ equation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi mathvariant="script">H</mi><mi>f</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{H}^f(t,x)</annotation></semantics></math></span> started with initial data <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>f</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In this article, we study the sample path properties of the KPZ temporal process <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi mathvariant="script">H</mi><mi>t</mi><mi>f</mi></msubsup><mo>:</mo><mo>=</mo><msup><mi mathvariant="script">H</mi><mi>f</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>t</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{H}_t^f := \mathcal{H}^f(t,0)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We show that for a very large class of initial data which includes any deterministic continuous initial data that grows slower than parabola (<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>f</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>≪</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><msup><mi>x</mi><mn>2</mn></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">f(x) \ll 1 + x^2</annotation></semantics></math></span>) as well as narrow wedge and stationary initial data, temporal increments of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi mathvariant="script">H</mi><mi>t</mi><mi>f</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathcal{H}_t^f</annotation></semantics></math></span> are well approximated by increments of a fractional Brownian motion of Hurst parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mn>4</mn></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac14</annotation></semantics></math></span>. As a consequence, we obtain several sample path properties of KPZ temporal properties including variation of the temporal process, law of iterated logarithms, modulus of continuity and Hausdorff dimensions of the set of points with exceptionally large increments.</p>{% endraw %}
