{% raw %}<p>We study inhomogeneous <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials which extend both <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker and stable Grothendieck polynomials. We prove that inhomogeneous <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials (in countably many variables) form a basis of certain commutative ring extending the ring of symmetric functions to a subring of its completion. We then describe positive specializations of that ring and relate them with a subset of Macdonald-positive specializations of the ring of symmetric functions. We also show some related probability distributions obtained from positive specializations of inhomogeneous <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q</annotation></semantics></math></span>-Whittaker polynomials.</p>{% endraw %}
