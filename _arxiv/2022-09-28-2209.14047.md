{% raw %}<p>The Ferrari-Spohn diffusion process arises as limit process for the 2D Ising model as well as random walks with area penalty. Motivated by the 3D Ising model, we consider <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M</annotation></semantics></math></span> such diffusions conditioned not to intersect. We show that the top process converges to the Airy<span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mrow></mrow><mn>2</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">_2</annotation></semantics></math></span> process as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We then explain the relation with the 3D Ising model and present some conjectures about it.</p>{% endraw %}
