{% raw %}<p>We consider a self-similar fragmentation process in which the generic particle of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x</annotation></semantics></math></span> is replaced at probability rate <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>x</mi><mi>α</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x^\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>, by its offspring made of smaller particles, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span> is some positive parameter. The total of offspring sizes may be both larger or smaller than <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>x</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">x</annotation></semantics></math></span> with positive probability. We show that under certain conditions the typical size in the ensemble is of the order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>α</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^{-1/\alpha}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and that the empirical distribution of sizes converges to a random limit which we characterise in terms of the reproduction law.</p>{% endraw %}
