{% raw %}<p>In this article we consider products of real random matrices with fixed size. Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>A</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>A</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mo>…</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">A_1,A_2, \dots </annotation></semantics></math></span> be i.i.d <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>k</mi><mo>×</mo><mi>k</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">k \times k</annotation></semantics></math></span> real matrices, whose entries are independent and identically distributed from probability measure <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Let <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><msub><mi>A</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><msub><mi>A</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo>…</mo><msub><mi>A</mi><mi>n</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">X_n = A_1A_2\dots A_n</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Then it is conjectured that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="double-struck">P</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mtext> has all real eigenvalues</mtext><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>→</mo><mn>1</mn><mtext> as </mtext><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">.</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mathbb{P}(X_n \text{ has all real eigenvalues}) \rightarrow 1 \text{ as } n \rightarrow \infty.</annotation></semantics></math></span> We show that the conjecture is true when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> has an atom.</p>{% endraw %}
