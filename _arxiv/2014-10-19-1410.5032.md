{% raw %}<p>Answering a question by Angel, Holroyd, Martin, Wilson and Winkler, we show that the maximal number of non-colliding coupled simple random walks on the complete graph <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which take turns, moving one at a time, is monotone in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We use this fact to couple <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">⌈</mo><mfrac><mi>N</mi><mn>4</mn></mfrac><mo stretchy="false">⌉</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lceil \frac N4 \rceil</annotation></semantics></math></span> such walks on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>K</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">K_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, improving the previous <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi mathvariant="normal">Ω</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>N</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Omega(N/\log N)</annotation></semantics></math></span> lower bound of Angel et al. We also introduce a new generalization of simple avoidance coupling which we call partially ordered simple avoidance coupling and provide a monotonicity result for this extension as well.</p>{% endraw %}
