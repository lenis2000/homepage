It is also known that these axioms are satisfied for the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mrow><mn>8</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{8/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-LQG metric constructed by Miller and Sheffield (2013-2016). We also show that any such metric is locally bi-H\"older continuous with respect to the Euclidean metric and compute the optimal H\"older exponents in both directions. Finally, we show that LQG geodesics cannot spend a long time near a straight line or the boundary of a metric ball.
