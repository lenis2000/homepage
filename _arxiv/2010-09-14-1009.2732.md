{% raw %}<p>Consider a system of particles evolving as independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) random walks. Initial fluctuations in the particle density get translated over time with velocity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mi>v</mi><mo>⃗</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\vec{v}</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the common mean velocity of the random walks. Consider a box centered around an observer who starts at the origin and moves with constant velocity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mover accent="true"><mi>v</mi><mo>⃗</mo></mover></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\vec{v}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. To observe interesting fluctuations beyond the translation of initial density fluctuations, we measure the net flux of particles over time into this moving box. We call this the ``box-current" process. We generalize this current process to a distribution valued process. Scaling time by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> and space by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>n</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{n}</annotation></semantics></math></span> gives current fluctuations of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mi>d</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>4</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n^{d/4}</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the space dimension. The scaling limit of the normalized current process is a distribution valued Gaussian process with given covariance. The limiting current process is equal in distribution to the solution of a given stochastic partial differential equation which is related to the generalized Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process.</p>{% endraw %}
