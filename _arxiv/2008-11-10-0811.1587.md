{% raw %}<p>We study the asymptotic behavior of the appropriately scaled and possibly perturbed spectral measure <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> of large random real symmetric matrices with heavy tailed entries. Specifically, consider the N by N symmetric matrix <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>Y</mi><mi>N</mi><mi>σ</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y_N^\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span> whose (i,j) entry is <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>i</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>j</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mrow><mi>i</mi><mi>j</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma(i/N,j/N)X_{ij}</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mrow><mi>i</mi><mi>j</mi></mrow></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>i</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>j</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">(X_{ij}, 0&lt;i&lt;j+1&lt;\infty)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is an infinite array of i.i.d real variables with common distribution in the domain of attraction of an <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha</annotation></semantics></math></span>-stable law, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>α</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0&lt;\alpha&lt;2</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>σ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a deterministic function. For a random diagonal <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">D_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> independent of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>Y</mi><mi>N</mi><mi>σ</mi></msubsup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">Y_N^\sigma</annotation></semantics></math></span> and with appropriate rescaling <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>a</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a_N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we prove that the distribution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>μ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\mu</annotation></semantics></math></span> of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msubsup><mi>a</mi><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msubsup><msubsup><mi>Y</mi><mi>N</mi><mi>σ</mi></msubsup><mo>+</mo><msub><mi>D</mi><mi>N</mi></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a_N^{-1}Y_N^\sigma + D_N</annotation></semantics></math></span> converges in mean towards a limiting probability measure which we characterize. As a special case, we derive and analyze the almost sure limiting spectral density for empirical covariance matrices with heavy tailed entries.</p>{% endraw %}
