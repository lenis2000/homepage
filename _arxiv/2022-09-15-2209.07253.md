{% raw %}<p>The Freud ensemble of random matrices is the unitary invariant ensemble corresponding to the weight <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>n</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>x</mi><msup><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mi>β</mi></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\exp(-n |x|^{\beta})</annotation></semantics></math></span>, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, on the real line. We consider the local behaviour of eigenvalues near zero, which exhibits a transition in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>. If <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\ge 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, it is described by the standard sine process. Below the critical value <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>, it is described by a process depending on the value of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and we determine the first two terms of the large gap probability in it. This so called weak confinement range <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>0</mn><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>β</mi><mo>&lt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">0&lt;\beta&lt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span> corresponds to the Freud weight with the indeterminate moment problem. We also find the multiplicative constant in the asymptotic expansion of the Freud multiple integral for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta\ge 1</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
