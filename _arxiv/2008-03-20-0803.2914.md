{% raw %}<p>Let $\sum_{\beta\in\nats^d} F_\beta x^\beta$ be a multivariate power series. For example <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo>∑</mo><msub><mi>F</mi><mi>β</mi></msub><msup><mi>x</mi><mi>β</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sum F_\beta x^\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span> could be a generating function for a combinatorial class. Assume that in a neighbourhood of the origin this series represents a nonentire function <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>F</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>G</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><msup><mi>H</mi><mi>p</mi></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F=G/H^p</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>G</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">G</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H</annotation></semantics></math></span> are holomorphic and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>p</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">p</annotation></semantics></math></span> is a positive integer. Given a direction $\alpha\in\pnats^d$ for which the asymptotics are controlled by a smooth point of the singular variety <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H = 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we compute the asymptotics of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>F</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mi>α</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">F_{n \alpha}</annotation></semantics></math></span> as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We do this via multivariate singularity analysis and give an explicit formula for the full asymptotic expansion. This improves on earlier work of R. Pemantle and the second author and allows for more accurate numerical approximation, as demonstrated by our examples.</p>{% endraw %}
