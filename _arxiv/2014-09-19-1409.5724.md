{% raw %}<p>We introduce the dynamical sine-Gordon equation in two space dimensions with parameter <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>β</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta</annotation></semantics></math></span>, which is the natural dynamic associated to the usual quantum sine-Gordon model. It is shown that when <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>β</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mfrac><mrow><mn>16</mn><mi>π</mi></mrow><mn>3</mn></mfrac><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta^2 \in (0,\frac{16\pi}{3})</annotation></semantics></math></span> the Wick renormalised equation is well-posed. In the regime <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>β</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>4</mn><mi>π</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta^2 \in (0,4\pi)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the Da Prato-Debussche method applies, while for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>β</mi><mn>2</mn></msup><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">[</mo><mn>4</mn><mi>π</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mfrac><mrow><mn>16</mn><mi>π</mi></mrow><mn>3</mn></mfrac><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\beta^2 \in [4\pi,\frac{16\pi}{3})</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the solution theory is provided via the theory of regularity structures (Hairer 2013). We also show that this model arises naturally from a class of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">2+1</annotation></semantics></math></span>-dimensional equilibrium interface fluctuation models with periodic nonlinearities. The main mathematical difficulty arises in the construction of the model for the associated regularity structure where the role of the noise is played by a non-Gaussian random distribution similar to the complex multiplicative Gaussian chaos recently analysed by Lacoin, Rhodes and Vargas (2013).</p>{% endraw %}
