{% raw %}<p>The Tracy-Widom distribution that has been much studied in recent years can be thought of as an extreme value distribution. We discuss interpolation between the classical extreme value distribution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\exp(-\exp(-x))</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the Gumbel distribution and the Tracy-Widom distribution. There is a family of determinantal processes whose edge behaviour interpolates between a Poisson process with density <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>exp</mi><mo>⁡</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\exp(-x)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and the Airy kernel point process. This process can be obtained as a scaling limit of a grand canonical version of a random matrix model introduced by Moshe, Neuberger and Shapiro. We also consider the deformed GUE ensemble, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>M</mi><mo>=</mo><msub><mi>M</mi><mn>0</mn></msub><mo>+</mo><msqrt><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>S</mi></mrow></msqrt><mi>V</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M=M_0+\sqrt{2S} V</annotation></semantics></math></span>, with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>M</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">M_0</annotation></semantics></math></span> diagobal with independent elements and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>V</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">V</annotation></semantics></math></span> from GUE. Here we do not see a transition from Tracy-Widom to Gumbel, but rather a transition from Tracy-Widom to Gaussian.</p>{% endraw %}
