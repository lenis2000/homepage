{% raw %}<p>The two parameter Poisson-Dirichlet distribution <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>P</mi><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">PD(\alpha,\theta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the distribution of an infinite dimensional random discrete probability. It is a generalization of Kingman's Poisson-Dirichlet distribution. The two parameter Dirichlet process <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Π</mi><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>ν</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Pi_{\alpha,\theta,\nu_0}</annotation></semantics></math></span> is the law of a pure atomic random measure with masses following the two parameter Poisson-Dirichlet distribution. In this article we focus on the construction and the properties of the infinite dimensional symmetric diffusion processes with respective symmetric measures <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>P</mi><mi>D</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">PD(\alpha,\theta)</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi mathvariant="normal">Π</mi><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>θ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><msub><mi>ν</mi><mn>0</mn></msub></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\Pi_{\alpha,\theta,\nu_0}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The methods used come from the theory of Dirichlet forms.</p>{% endraw %}
