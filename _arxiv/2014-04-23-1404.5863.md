{% raw %}<p>We study the stochastic Allen-Cahn equation driven by a noise term with intensity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msqrt><mi>ε</mi></msqrt></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\sqrt{\varepsilon}</annotation></semantics></math></span> and correlation length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span> in two and three spatial dimensions. We study diagonal limits <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ε</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta, \varepsilon \to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and describe fully the large deviation behaviour depending on the relationship between <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The recently developed theory of regularity structures allows to fully analyse the behaviour of solutions for vanishing correlation length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta</annotation></semantics></math></span> and fixed noise intensity <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon</annotation></semantics></math></span>. One key fact is that in order to get non-trivial limits as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta \to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, it is necessary to introduce diverging counterterms. The theory of regularity structures allows to rigorously analyse this renormalisation procedure for a number of interesting equations. Our main result is a large deviation principle for these renormalised solutions. One interesting feature of this result is that the diverging renormalisation constants disappear at the level of the large deviations rate function. We apply this result to derive a sharp condition on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>δ</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>ε</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\delta, \varepsilon</annotation></semantics></math></span> that guarantees a large deviation principle for diagonal schemes <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi>δ</mi><mo>→</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\varepsilon, \delta \to 0</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the equation without renormalisation.</p>{% endraw %}
