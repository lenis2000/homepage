The particles exhibit a drift, but there is no preference given between first-class and second-class particles. The quantum group symmetry leads to reversible measures and a self-duality for the particle system. Additionally, a new method is developed to construct a symmetric interacting particle system from the Casimir element of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>s</mi><msub><mi>o</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi>n</mi></mrow></msub></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">so_{2n}</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
