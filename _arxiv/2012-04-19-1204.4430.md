{% raw %}<p>The squared Bessel process is a 1-dimensional diffusion process related to the squared norm of a higher dimensional Brownian motion. We study a model of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> non-intersecting squared Bessel paths, with all paths starting at the same point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">a&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> at time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=0</annotation></semantics></math></span> and ending at the same point <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>b</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">b&gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span> at time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. Our interest lies in the critical regime <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>a</mi><mi>b</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">ab=1/4</annotation></semantics></math></span>, for which the paths are tangent to the hard edge at the origin at a critical time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t^*\in (0,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The critical behavior of the paths for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n\to\infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> is studied in a scaling limit with time <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>t</mi><mo>=</mo><msup><mi>t</mi><mo>∗</mo></msup><mo>+</mo><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">t=t^*+O(n^{-1/3})</annotation></semantics></math></span> and temperature <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>T</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>O</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msup><mi>n</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">T=1+O(n^{-2/3})</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This leads to a critical correlation kernel that is defined via a new Riemann-Hilbert problem of size <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>4</mn><mo>×</mo><mn>4</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">4\times 4</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The Riemann-Hilbert problem gives rise to a new Lax pair representation for the Hastings-McLeod solution to the inhomogeneous Painlev\'e II equation <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">&quot;</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mi>x</mi><mi>q</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mn>2</mn><msup><mi>q</mi><mn>3</mn></msup><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>x</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>−</mo><mi>ν</mi><mo separator="true">,</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q&quot;(x) = xq(x)+2q^3(x)-\nu,</annotation></semantics></math></span> where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ν</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>α</mi><mo>+</mo><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\nu=\alpha+1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span> with <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha&gt;-1</annotation></semantics></math></span> the parameter of the squared Bessel process. These results extend our recent work with Kuijlaars and Zhang \cite{DKZ} for the homogeneous case <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ν</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\nu = 0</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
