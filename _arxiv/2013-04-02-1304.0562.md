{% raw %}<p>We consider branching Brownian motion on the real line with the following selection mechanism: Every time the number of particles exceeds a (large) given number <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, only the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> right-most particles are kept and the others killed. After rescaling time by <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mrow><mi>log</mi><mo>⁡</mo></mrow><mn>3</mn></msup><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\log^3N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, we show that the properly recentred position of the <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">⌈</mo><mi>α</mi><mi>N</mi><mo stretchy="false">⌉</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lceil \alpha N\rceil</annotation></semantics></math></span>-th particle from the right, <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>α</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mn>0</mn><mo separator="true">,</mo><mn>1</mn><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\alpha\in(0,1)</annotation></semantics></math></span>, converges in law to an explicitly given spectrally positive L\'evy process. This behaviour has been predicted to hold for a large class of models falling into the universality class of the FKPP equation with weak multiplicative noise [Brunet et al., Phys. Rev. E \textbf{73}(5), 056126 (2006)] and is proven here for the first time for such a model.</p>{% endraw %}
