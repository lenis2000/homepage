{% raw %}<p>We study a process of generating random positive integer weight sequences <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><msub><mi>W</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{ W_n \}</annotation></semantics></math></span> where the gaps between the weights <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mo stretchy="false">{</mo><msub><mi>X</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>=</mo><msub><mi>W</mi><mi>n</mi></msub><mo>−</mo><msub><mi>W</mi><mrow><mi>n</mi><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow></msub><mo stretchy="false">}</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\{ X_n = W_n - W_{n-1} \}</annotation></semantics></math></span> are i.i.d. positive integer-valued random variables. We show that as long as the gap distribution has finite <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mn>2</mn></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\frac{1}{2}</annotation></semantics></math></span>-moment, almost surely, the resulting weight sequence is asymptotically complete, i.e., all large enough multiples of the gcd of the possible gap values can be written as a sum of distinct weights. We then show a much stronger result that if the gap distribution has a moment generating function with large enough radius of convergence, then every large enough multiple of the gcd of gap values can be written as a sum of <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m</annotation></semantics></math></span> distinct weights for any fixed <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>m</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">m \geq 2</annotation></semantics></math></span>.</p>{% endraw %}
