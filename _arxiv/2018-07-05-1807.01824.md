{% raw %}<p>We study an oriented first passage percolation model for the evolution of a river delta. This model is exactly solvable and occurs as the low temperature limit of the beta random walk in random environment. We analyze the asymptotics of an exact formula from [4] to show that, at any fixed positive time, the width of a river delta of length <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>L</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L</annotation></semantics></math></span> approaches a constant times <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>L</mi><mrow><mn>2</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>3</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L^{2/3}</annotation></semantics></math></span> with Tracy-Widom GUE fluctuations of order <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msup><mi>L</mi><mrow><mn>4</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>9</mn></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">L^{4/9}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. This result can be rephrased in terms of particle systems. We introduce an exactly solvable particle system on the integer half line and show that after running the system for only finite time the particle positions have Tracy-Widom fluctuations.</p>{% endraw %}
