These formulas generalise the known <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>r</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">r=1</annotation></semantics></math></span> case of the Pieri-type formulas for the nonsymmetric Macdonald polynomials <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>E</mi><mi>η</mi></msub><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>z</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">E_\eta(z)</annotation></semantics></math></span>.
