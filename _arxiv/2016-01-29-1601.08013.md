{% raw %}<p>We consider the stochastic wave and heat equations with affine multiplicative Gaussian noise which is white in time and behaves in space like the fractional Brownian motion with index <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi><mo>∈</mo><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mn>4</mn></mfrac><mo separator="true">,</mo><mfrac><mn>1</mn><mn>2</mn></mfrac><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H \in (\frac14,\frac12)</annotation></semantics></math></span>. The existence and uniqueness of the solution to these equations has been proved recently by the authors. In the present note we show that these solutions have modifications which are H\"older continuous in space of order smaller than <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H</annotation></semantics></math></span>, and H\"older continuous in time of order smaller than <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma</annotation></semantics></math></span>, where <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>H</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma=H</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the wave equation and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>γ</mi><mo>=</mo><mi>H</mi><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\gamma=H/2</annotation></semantics></math></span> for the heat equation.</p>{% endraw %}
