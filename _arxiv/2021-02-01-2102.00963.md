{% raw %}<p>Consider the normalized adjacency matrices of random <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-regular graphs on <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span> vertices with fixed degree <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d\geq3</annotation></semantics></math></span>. We prove that, with probability <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mo>−</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mn>1</mn><mo>+</mo><mi>ε</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1-N^{-1+{\varepsilon}}</annotation></semantics></math></span> for any <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>ε</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>0</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\varepsilon} &gt;0</annotation></semantics></math></span>, the following two properties hold as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi><mo>→</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∞</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N \to \infty</annotation></semantics></math></span> provided that <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi><mo>≥</mo><mn>3</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d\geq3</annotation></semantics></math></span>: (i) The eigenvalues are close to the classical eigenvalue locations given by the Kesten-McKay distribution. In particular, the extremal eigenvalues are concentrated with polynomial error bound in <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>N</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">N</annotation></semantics></math></span>, i.e. <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><msub><mi>λ</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo separator="true">,</mo><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><msub><mi>λ</mi><mi>N</mi></msub><mi mathvariant="normal">∣</mi><mo>≤</mo><mn>2</mn><mo>+</mo><msup><mi>N</mi><mrow><mo>−</mo><mi>c</mi></mrow></msup></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\lambda_2, |\lambda_N|\leq 2+N^{-c}</annotation></semantics></math></span>. (ii) All eigenvectors of random <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>d</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">d</annotation></semantics></math></span>-regular graphs are completely delocalized.</p>{% endraw %}
