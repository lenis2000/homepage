We describe these limiting distributions in terms of Gnedin and Olshanski's bi-infinite extension of the Mallows model. We also investigate these limiting distributions, and study the behaviour of the constants involved in the Gaussian limit laws. We for example show that as <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>↓</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q\downarrow 1</annotation></semantics></math></span> the expected number of 1-cycles tends to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mn>1</mn><mi mathvariant="normal">/</mi><mn>2</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">1/2</annotation></semantics></math></span> -- which, curiously, differs from the value corresponding to <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>=</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q=1</annotation></semantics></math></span>. In addition we exhibit an interesting "oscillating" behaviour in the limiting probability measures for <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>q</mi><mo>&gt;</mo><mn>1</mn></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">q&gt;1</annotation></semantics></math></span> and <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> odd versus <span class="katex"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"><semantics><mrow><mi>n</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">n</annotation></semantics></math></span> even.</p>{% endraw %}
